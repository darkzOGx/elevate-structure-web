## What Foundation Problems Require Engineering?

**Direct Answer:** Foundation problems requiring engineering include significant settlement, major crack patterns, slab heave, foundation movement affecting the structure, and any repairs requiring building permits. Minor cosmetic cracks may not require engineering, but structural concerns should always be professionally evaluated.

### Settlement Issues

Settlement problems include:

- Differential settlement causing wall cracks
- Perimeter settlement from poor drainage
- Interior settlement from soil consolidation
- Corner settlement from localized conditions

### Foundation Cracks

Crack patterns indicating structural concerns:

- Stair-step cracks in masonry
- Diagonal cracks radiating from corners
- Horizontal cracks from lateral pressure
- Cracks wider than 1/4 inch

### Slab Problems

Slab issues requiring engineering:

- Heaving from expansive soils
- Significant slab cracking
- Slab separation from stem walls
- Water intrusion through slabs

### Structural Movement

Building symptoms of foundation problems:

- Doors and windows not operating
- Visible floor slopes
- Wall separations from ceilings
- Exterior brick or stucco cracking

## What Causes Foundation Problems in Rancho San Diego?

**Direct Answer:** Common causes include expansive clay soils that swell and shrink with moisture changes, poor drainage directing water toward foundations, inadequate original construction, plumbing leaks saturating soils, and tree roots removing moisture or invading foundations. Understanding the cause is essential for designing effective repairs.

### Expansive Soils

Clay soil behavior:

- Swelling when wet
- Shrinking when dry
- Seasonal movement cycles
- Concentrated at perimeters

### Drainage Problems

Water-related damage:

- Poor site grading
- Clogged or missing gutters
- Inadequate downspout discharge
- Hardscape directing water to foundation

### Construction Deficiencies

Original construction issues:

- Inadequate footing size
- Insufficient reinforcement
- Poor concrete quality
- Improper curing

### Site Conditions

Environmental factors:

- Tree root desiccation
- Slope creep
- Irrigation system effects
- Neighboring construction impact

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Do all foundation cracks need repair?

Not all cracks are structural concerns. Hairline cracks from shrinkage are common and typically not problematic. Larger or growing cracks warrant evaluation.

### Can foundation problems be permanently fixed?

Most foundation problems can be effectively repaired if the underlying cause is addressed. Without addressing causes like drainage issues, problems may recur.

### Do I need a permit for foundation repair?

Structural foundation repairs typically require building permits and PE-stamped plans.

### How long do foundation repairs last?

Properly designed and constructed foundation repairs should last the life of the structure when underlying causes are addressed.
