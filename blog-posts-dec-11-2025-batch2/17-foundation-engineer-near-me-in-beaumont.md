## What Does a Foundation Engineer Do in Beaumont?

A foundation engineer designs and evaluates foundation systems that safely transfer building loads to the ground. In Beaumont, licensed Professional Engineers design foundations for new construction, evaluate existing foundations with problems, and prepare repair designs—all based on geotechnical recommendations and California Building Code requirements.

Foundation engineering requires understanding both structural engineering principles and soil mechanics, working closely with geotechnical engineers when soil investigations are needed.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How long does a foundation assessment take?

Site inspection is typically 1-2 hours. Report delivery is usually within 1-2 weeks.

### Do I need a geotechnical report?

New construction typically requires geotechnical reports. Assessments and repairs may or may not, depending on circumstances.

### Can you fix my foundation?

We design repairs—contractors perform the work. We work with experienced foundation repair contractors who execute our designs.

### What's the difference between structural and geotechnical engineers?

Structural engineers design the foundation structure. Geotechnical engineers evaluate soil conditions. Both are often needed for foundation projects.
