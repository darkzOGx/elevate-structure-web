## What Foundation Problems Are Common in Torrance?

**Direct Answer:**
Torrance foundations commonly experience settlement causing uneven floors and cracked finishes, shrinkage cracking from original construction or aging concrete, soil movement damage from expansive clay or poor drainage, and deterioration of older concrete or reinforcement. Each problem type requires different repair approaches, making accurate diagnosis essential before repair.

**Settlement**
Foundations settle when underlying soils compress, shift, or wash away. Settlement creates sloping floors, cracked walls, and sticking doors.

**Concrete Cracking**
Cracks develop from shrinkage, settlement, soil pressure, or seismic movement. Some cracks are cosmetic; others indicate structural concerns.

**Soil Movement**
Expansive clay soils swell when wet and shrink when dry, creating cyclical stress on foundations. Poor drainage concentrates water, accelerating damage.

**Deterioration**
Older concrete deteriorates over time, particularly if original mix was poor or reinforcement is corroding. Surface spalling and structural weakening result.
