## Structural Engineering Design

### What Structural Engineers Design

Structural engineering focuses on the load-bearing elements that keep buildings safe and stable. In Montebello, structural engineers design:

**Foundation Systems**
- Shallow foundations (spread footings, continuous footings)
- Deep foundations (piles, caissons, drilled piers)
- Mat foundations for heavy loads
- Post-tensioned slabs
- Retaining walls and shoring systems

**Building Frames**
- Wood frame construction (most Montebello residential)
- Steel frame structures (commercial, industrial)
- Concrete frame buildings (multi-story, parking structures)
- Masonry structures (reinforced CMU walls)
- Hybrid systems combining multiple materials

**Seismic Force-Resisting Systems**
Critical for Montebello's Seismic Zone 4 location:
- Shear walls (wood or concrete)
- Moment-resisting frames
- Braced frames
- Dual systems
- Base isolation (specialized applications)

**Specialty Structures**
- Canopies and awnings
- Mezzanines and platforms
- Equipment supports
- Stairs and railings
- Balconies and decks

### When You Need Structural Engineering

**Residential Projects:**
- New home construction
- Room additions and expansions
- Second-story additions
- Load-bearing wall removal
- Foundation repairs
- Seismic retrofitting
- ADUs (Accessory Dwelling Units)

**Commercial Projects:**
- New commercial buildings
- Tenant improvements affecting structure
- Roof equipment installations
- Building expansions
- Structural modifications

## Civil Engineering Design

### What Civil Engineers Design

Civil engineering focuses on site development and infrastructure. In Montebello, civil engineers design:

**Grading & Drainage**
- Site grading plans
- Surface drainage systems
- Subsurface drainage
- Erosion control measures
- Slope stabilization

**Stormwater Management**
Critical for Montebello's LA County location:
- Stormwater detention basins
- Bioswales and rain gardens
- Permeable paving systems
- Water quality treatment measures
- WQMP (Water Quality Management Plan) compliance

**Utility Design**
- Water service and distribution
- Sewer laterals and systems
- Storm drain connections
- Dry utilities coordination (gas, electric, telecom)
- Fire water systems

**Site Improvements**
- Parking lot design and striping
- Driveway and access design
- Sidewalks and pedestrian paths
- ADA accessibility compliance
- Landscape coordination

**Roadway Design**
- Private street design
- Intersection improvements
- Traffic circulation
- Signage and striping

### When You Need Civil Engineering

**Residential Projects:**
- New home construction on raw land
- Significant grading or drainage work
- Driveway design
- Retaining walls over 4 feet
- Hillside development

**Commercial Projects:**
- New commercial development
- Site redevelopment
- Parking lot construction
- Stormwater compliance (projects over 1 acre)
- Utility extensions

## Geotechnical Engineering

### What Geotechnical Engineers Do

Geotechnical engineers analyze soil conditions and provide foundation recommendations:

**Soil Investigation**
- Borings and test pits
- Laboratory testing
- Soil classification
- Bearing capacity determination
- Groundwater evaluation

**Foundation Recommendations**
- Foundation type selection
- Bearing pressure limits
- Settlement predictions
- Seismic considerations
- Special foundation requirements

**Slope Stability**
- Hillside development analysis
- Landslide potential assessment
- Retaining wall design parameters
- Erosion control recommendations

### When You Need Geotechnical Engineering

**Required for:**
- New construction (most jurisdictions)
- Hillside development
- Large additions
- Poor soil conditions
- Foundation problems

**Montebello Soil Conditions:**
- Generally good bearing capacity in most areas
- Some areas with expansive clays
- Variable groundwater levels
- Seismic considerations (liquefaction potential in some areas)

## Fire Protection Engineering

### What Fire Protection Engineers Design

Fire protection engineers design systems to detect, suppress, and prevent fires:

**Fire Sprinkler Systems**
- Automatic sprinkler design
- Hydraulic calculations
- Water supply analysis
- Sprinkler head selection and placement
- Special hazard protection

**Fire Alarm Systems**
- Smoke and heat detection
- Manual pull stations
- Notification devices
- Emergency communication
- System monitoring

**Fire Suppression Systems**
- Kitchen hood suppression
- Clean agent systems
- Foam suppression
- CO2 systems
- Water mist systems

### When You Need Fire Protection Engineering

**Required for:**
- Commercial buildings (most types)
- Multi-family residential (3+ units)
- Industrial facilities
- Restaurants with commercial kitchens
- High-hazard occupancies

**Montebello Requirements:**
- Follow California Fire Code
- LA County Fire Department jurisdiction (some areas)
- Montebello Fire Department requirements
- Water flow testing required

## Cost Implications by Engineering Type

### Typical Cost Ranges (Montebello Projects)

**Residential:**
- Structural only: $2,500-$15,000
- Structural + Civil: $3,500-$20,000
- Full MEP coordination: +$3,000-$10,000
- Geotechnical investigation: $2,000-$5,000

**Commercial:**
- Structural only: $10,000-$100,000+
- Civil site design: $8,000-$75,000+
- MEP design: $15,000-$150,000+
- Fire protection: $5,000-$50,000+

### Factors Affecting Multi-Discipline Costs

**Project Complexity**
- Simple projects: Baseline cost
- Moderate complexity: +25-40%
- High complexity: +50-100%+

**Coordination Requirements**
- Single discipline: Baseline
- Two disciplines: +30-50%
- Three+ disciplines: +60-100%+

**Regulatory Compliance**
- Standard requirements: Baseline
- Enhanced compliance: +15-30%
- Specialized permits: +30-60%+
