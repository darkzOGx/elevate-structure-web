## Fee Structures Explained

### Fixed Fee

**How It Works:**
Engineer provides a lump sum price for defined scope of work.

**Advantages:**
- Budget certainty
- No surprises
- Clear deliverables

**Best For:**
- Well-defined projects
- Standard residential work
- Straightforward commercial projects

**Typical for Baldwin Park:**
- Most residential additions
- Standard commercial tenant improvements
- ADUs and garage conversions

### Hourly Rate

**How It Works:**
Engineer bills for actual time spent at agreed hourly rate.

**Typical Rates in Baldwin Park:**
- Junior engineer: $120-$160/hour
- Senior engineer: $160-$220/hour
- Principal engineer: $220-$300/hour

**Advantages:**
- Flexibility for scope changes
- Pay only for work performed
- Good for phased projects

**Best For:**
- Undefined scope
- Consulting and feasibility studies
- Ongoing construction support

### Percentage of Construction Cost

**How It Works:**
Engineering fee calculated as percentage of total construction cost.

**Typical Percentages:**
- Simple residential: 1-2%
- Complex residential: 2-4%
- Commercial: 3-6%
- Industrial: 4-8%

**Example:**
$500,000 construction cost × 3% = $15,000 engineering fee

**Advantages:**
- Scales with project size
- Industry-standard approach
- Aligns engineer and owner interests

**Best For:**
- Large commercial projects
- Design-build projects
- Projects with uncertain scope
