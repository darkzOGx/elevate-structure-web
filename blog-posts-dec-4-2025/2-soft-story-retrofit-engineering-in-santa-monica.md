## What Is Soft-Story Retrofit Engineering in Santa Monica?

**Direct Answer:** Soft-story retrofit engineering is the structural design process of strengthening buildings with weak ground floors‚Äîtypically apartment buildings with tuck-under parking or large commercial openings. In Santa Monica, this involves installing steel moment frames, plywood shear walls, or other lateral force-resisting systems to prevent collapse during earthquakes. All designs require PE-stamped plans and city permit approval.

### Why Soft-Story Buildings Are Dangerous

Soft-story buildings have ground floors that are significantly weaker than upper floors due to:

- **Open Parking Areas**: Tuck-under parking with minimal walls
- **Large Storefronts**: Commercial spaces with extensive glazing
- **Missing Shear Walls**: Lack of lateral bracing at ground level

During earthquakes, the weak story collapses while upper floors remain relatively intact‚Äîa phenomenon that killed 16 people in the 1994 Northridge earthquake at the Northridge Meadows apartment complex.

### Santa Monica's Seismic Risk

Santa Monica sits near several active faults:

- **Santa Monica Fault**: Runs directly through the city
- **Hollywood Fault**: 8 miles east
- **Newport-Inglewood Fault**: 10 miles south

The USGS estimates a 60% probability of a magnitude 6.7+ earthquake in Southern California within the next 30 years.

## What Are the Santa Monica Soft-Story Retrofit Requirements?

**Direct Answer:** Santa Monica's mandatory retrofit ordinance applies to wood-frame buildings with 3+ stories OR 2 stories with 5+ units that have soft, weak, or open-front ground floors. Building owners must complete engineering evaluation, submit retrofit plans, obtain permits, and finish construction by city-mandated deadlines. Non-compliance results in escalating fines and potential building condemnation.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What happens if I miss the Santa Monica soft-story retrofit deadline?

The city issues daily fines starting at $250-$1,000 per day, escalating over time. Continued non-compliance can result in building condemnation, prohibition on collecting rent, and personal liability for owners in any earthquake. Properties become difficult to sell or refinance without compliance.

### Can I pass retrofit costs to my Santa Monica tenants?

Santa Monica's rent control ordinance allows landlords to pass through certain capital improvement costs to tenants over time, subject to limitations. Consult with a local attorney familiar with Santa Monica's Rent Control Board rules for specific guidance.

### How long does soft-story construction take?

Typical construction takes 8-16 weeks depending on building size and retrofit complexity. Most work occurs during business hours at ground level. Tenants can usually remain in their units, though parking access may be temporarily limited.

### Do I need to relocate tenants during retrofit construction?

Generally no. Most soft-story retrofits affect only the ground floor/parking level. Upper-floor tenants may experience noise and some inconvenience but rarely need to relocate. Ground-floor commercial tenants may need temporary modifications.

### What's the difference between steel moment frames and shear walls?

Steel moment frames are rigid frames that resist lateral forces through beam-column connections, leaving openings clear. Plywood shear walls are solid panels that resist forces but block openings. Frames cost more but preserve parking; walls cost less but reduce open space.

### Is my building definitely subject to the ordinance?

If you received a screening letter from Santa Monica, your building is on the mandatory compliance list. If you haven't received notice but own a wood-frame building with 3+ stories or 2 stories with 5+ units that has open ground-floor parking, contact the Building & Safety Division to verify status.
