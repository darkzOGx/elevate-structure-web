### Do Del Mar oceanfront properties require Coastal Commission approval?

Most Del Mar oceanfront properties fall within Coastal Commission jurisdiction. New construction, significant remodels, and foundation work typically require a Coastal Development Permit (CDP). The City of Del Mar processes some permits locally; others go to the Coastal Commission directly.

### How close to the bluff edge can I build in Del Mar?

Setback requirements depend on bluff height, erosion rate, and foundation type. The Coastal Commission typically requires setbacks based on 50-75 year erosion projections with safety factors. Deep caisson foundations extending below erosion zones may allow closer placement than shallow foundations.

### How do I protect my Del Mar home from bluff erosion?

Options include: foundation underpinning or replacement with deep caissons, bluff stabilization (soldier piles, drainage improvements), and in some cases managed retreat. The Coastal Commission generally prefers alternatives to hard armoring. Engineering evaluation determines the best approach for your property.

### What's the timeline for oceanfront foundation work in Del Mar?
