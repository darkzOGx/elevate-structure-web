## What Is Parking Structure Engineering?

**Direct Answer:** Parking structure engineering involves the specialized design of buildings or structures dedicated to vehicle parking, requiring expertise in concrete construction, post-tensioning systems, durability design, and seismic force-resisting systems. This engineering discipline addresses unique challenges including heavy concentrated wheel loads, corrosive environments from vehicle fluids and de-icing salts, ramping systems, and the need for economical, open floor plans with minimal columns to maximize parking efficiency.

Parking structures represent a distinct building type with requirements that differ significantly from typical commercial buildings. The combination of exposed conditions, vehicle impacts, moving loads, and long spans creates demanding design constraints that require specialized expertise.

**Key Parking Structure Types:**

**Above-Grade Parking Garages**
Free-standing structures with multiple levels, typically cast-in-place or precast concrete. These provide the highest parking density for surface area but require significant structural investment.

**Podium Parking Structures**
Parking levels beneath residential or commercial development, integrated into mixed-use projects. Common in urban infill development throughout Burbank's downtown.

**Subterranean Parking**
Below-grade parking beneath buildings, requiring specialized waterproofing and drainage. Increasingly common for high-value commercial and residential projects.

**Surface Parking Structures**
Single-level covered parking with roof systems, providing weather protection without multi-level construction costs.

## What Are Seismic Requirements for Parking Structures in Los Angeles County?

**Direct Answer:** Burbank and Los Angeles County parking structures are classified in Seismic Design Category D, requiring special seismic force-resisting systems such as special moment frames or special shear walls. The open floor plans and ramping systems create unique seismic challenges including diaphragm discontinuities, irregular mass distribution, and potential for torsional response. Parking structures must maintain serviceability after moderate earthquakes and life safety in major events.

**Seismic Design Category D Requirements**

Burbank's seismic parameters reflect:
- Proximity to Verdugo Fault
- Sierra Madre Fault zone influence
- Site Class C/D typical for valley sites
- SDC D classification for most structures

**Permitted Seismic Systems:**

| System | R Factor | Height Limit | Common Use |
|--------|----------|--------------|------------|
| Special RC Shear Walls | 5-6 | 160' | Most common |
| Special RC Moment Frames | 8 | Unlimited | Open perimeter |
| Special Steel Moment Frames | 8 | Unlimited | Hybrid structures |
| Ordinary RC Shear Walls | 4-5 | 35' | Low-rise only |

**Parking Structure-Specific Seismic Issues:**

**Ramp Discontinuities**
Ramping systems create diaphragm discontinuities that affect load path:
- Collector reinforcement at ramp edges
- Strut-and-tie modeling for force transfer
- Connection design for out-of-plane forces
- Deformation compatibility analysis

**Short Column Effects**
Partial-height walls at ramps create short, stiff columns:
- Potential for brittle failure modes
- Intentional flexural weakening
- Seismic isolation in some cases
- Careful force distribution

**Large Plan Irregularity**
Long, narrow parking structures may have:
- Torsional irregularity
- Reentrant corner effects
- Non-parallel system irregularity

**Movement Joints**
Large structures may require seismic separation joints:
- Joint location coordination
- Waterproofing at joints
- Fire rating maintenance
- Architectural treatment

## What Durability and Waterproofing Requirements Apply?

**Direct Answer:** Parking structures face severe durability challenges from vehicle fluids, carbonation, chloride exposure (coastal areas), and freeze-thaw cycles (mountainous regions). California building codes and ACI 362.1R requirements mandate specific concrete cover, material specifications, drainage design, and protective systems to achieve the expected 50+ year service life. Proper durability design reduces life-cycle costs by minimizing repairs and extending structure serviceability.

**Concrete Durability Requirements**

**Concrete Materials**
- Minimum f'c = 4,000 psi (5,000 psi preferred)
- Low permeability requirements (per ACI 362.1R)
- Maximum water-cement ratio limits
- Air entrainment for freeze-thaw resistance
- Corrosion-inhibiting admixtures recommended

**Reinforcement Protection**
- Minimum concrete cover: 1-1/2" (clear) to 2" (top of slabs)
- Epoxy-coated reinforcement for exposed surfaces
- Stainless steel at critical locations
- Proper bar support and consolidation

**Drainage Systems**
Effective drainage prevents ponding and accelerates drying:
- Floor slopes (minimum 1.5%, 2% preferred)
- Floor drains at low points
- Trench drains at ramp bases
- Downspout/piping systems
- Oil/water separators

**Waterproofing Systems**

For podium and subterranean structures:

**Traffic-Bearing Membranes**
- Elastomeric traffic coatings (top deck)
- Membrane systems for below-grade
- Joint sealants at movement joints
- Protection board over below-grade membrane

**Subterranean Waterproofing**
- Positive-side or negative-side systems
- Drainage board and filter fabric
- Underslab drainage systems
- Hydrostatic pressure relief

**Expansion Joints**

Critical for controlling cracking:
- Location at ramp transitions
- Maximum spacing per structure type
- Waterproof joint systems
- Traffic-rated joint covers

## What Are Common Parking Structure Engineering Challenges?

**Direct Answer:** Common parking structure engineering challenges in Burbank include designing efficient layouts within irregular site constraints, coordinating complex post-tensioning sequences, addressing seismic force paths through ramping systems, and meeting aggressive construction schedules. Professional Engineers address these through iterative layout optimization, detailed force flow analysis, and construction-focused design approaches.

**Challenge 1: Irregular Site Geometry**

**Problem:** Urban infill sites often have irregular boundaries that don't match ideal parking geometry.

**Burbank Example:** Downtown mixed-use projects must fit parking within existing street grids and property lines that create acute angles and odd dimensions.

**Solution:**
- Optimized column grid for irregular geometry
- Efficient space layout algorithms
- Combination of standard and compact spaces
- Ramp placement to minimize loss

**Challenge 2: Post-Tensioning Coordination**

**Problem:** Post-tensioned parking structures require careful coordination of tendon placement, stressing sequence, and restraint effects.

**Solution:**
- Early PT specialist involvement
- Restraint analysis for shear wall locations
- Clear construction sequence requirements
- Pour strip locations at columns

**Challenge 3: Ramp Structural Complexity**

**Problem:** Ramping systems create discontinuities in the floor diaphragm and complex force paths.

**Solution:**
- Explicit modeling of ramp forces
- Collector elements at ramp-to-floor connections
- Short column avoidance strategies
- Deformation compatibility checks

**Challenge 4: Vehicle Impact Considerations**

**Problem:** Vehicles can strike structural elements, requiring impact-resistant design.

**Solution:**
- Vehicle barriers per IBC requirements
- Column protection at vulnerable locations
- Barrier anchorage design
- Crash-tested systems where appropriate

## Frequently Asked Questions

**How many parking spaces per 1,000 SF does Burbank require?**

Parking requirements vary by use:
- Office: 3-4 spaces per 1,000 SF
- Retail: 4-5 spaces per 1,000 SF
- Residential: 1-2 spaces per unit (varies by unit size)
- Restaurant: 10 spaces per 1,000 SF
Requirements may be reduced for transit-adjacent development or through parking studies.

**What is the typical cost to construct a parking structure in California?**

Construction costs (not engineering) typically range:
- Cast-in-place PT: $25,000-$40,000 per space
- Precast concrete: $22,000-$35,000 per space
- Steel frame: $28,000-$45,000 per space
- Subterranean: $45,000-$80,000 per space
Costs vary significantly based on site conditions, finishes, and local market.

**Should parking structures be post-tensioned or conventionally reinforced?**

Post-tensioned (PT) construction offers advantages for most parking:
- Longer spans (60'+ typical)
- Thinner slabs (reduced floor-to-floor height)
- Better crack control
- Improved durability
Conventional reinforcement may be economical for small structures with short spans.

**How long does a concrete parking structure last?**

Well-designed and maintained parking structures should achieve:
- Design life: 50+ years
- Major repairs: 20-30 year intervals
- Routine maintenance: Annual
Actual life depends heavily on maintenance quality and exposure conditions.

**What fire protection is required for parking structures?**

Open parking structures (with required openings) are generally exempt from sprinkler requirements. Enclosed or mechanically ventilated garages may require:
- Sprinkler systems
- Smoke detection
- Exhaust systems
- Fire department connections

**Can existing parking structures be expanded?**

Existing structures can often be expanded through:
- Vertical additions (if foundation adequate)
- Horizontal extensions
- Ramp reconfigurations
Structural evaluation is required to confirm existing capacity and connection requirements.
