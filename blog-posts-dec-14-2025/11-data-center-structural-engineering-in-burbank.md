## What Data Center Projects Require Structural Engineering in Burbank?

**Direct Answer:** Data center projects requiring structural engineering include new data center construction, server room build-outs, colocation cage installations, floor capacity reinforcement, equipment anchorage, raised floor systems, generator installations, and cooling system support structures.

**Floor Capacity Engineering**
Data center equipment creates concentrated floor loads far exceeding typical office building design. Structural analysis determines if existing floors can support proposed equipment or if reinforcement is needed.

**Raised Floor Systems**
Raised access floors require structural support design, seismic bracing, and coordination with underfloor cooling and cable management systems.

**Equipment Anchorage**
Server cabinets, UPS systems, generators, and cooling equipment require seismic anchorage per California Building Code. Mission-critical facilities often exceed minimum code requirements.

**Generator & Mechanical Support**
Backup generators, cooling towers, and HVAC equipment require structural support design including roof capacity verification and vibration isolation.

## What Are Critical Structural Considerations for Data Centers?

**Direct Answer:** Critical considerations include floor load capacity for concentrated equipment loads, seismic anchorage for mission-critical systems, vibration isolation for sensitive equipment, structural support for cooling systems, and redundant structural systems matching facility reliability requirements.

**Concentrated Floor Loads**
Server racks create point loads of 2,500-5,000+ pounds, far exceeding typical 100 PSF office loads. Floor capacity analysis and potential reinforcement is essential.

**Seismic Reliability**
Mission-critical data centers require enhanced seismic design to maintain operations during and after earthquakes. Equipment anchorage often exceeds code minimums.

**Vibration Control**
Generators, cooling equipment, and even server fans create vibration requiring isolation from sensitive equipment. Structural design addresses vibration paths and isolation systems.
