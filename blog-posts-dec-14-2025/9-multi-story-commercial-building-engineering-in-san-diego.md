## What Are Key Structural Systems for San Diego Multi-Story Buildings?

**Direct Answer:** Key structural systems include special moment frames, buckling-restrained braced frames, special shear walls, and core wall systems. Selection depends on building height, configuration, site constraints, and cost-performance optimization within San Diego's Seismic Design Category D requirements.

**Special Moment Frames**
Steel or concrete moment frames provide lateral resistance while allowing architectural flexibility. Common for mid-rise buildings with open floor plates.

**Braced Frame Systems**
Concentrically or eccentrically braced frames provide efficient lateral resistance. Buckling-restrained braced frames (BRBF) offer enhanced performance for taller buildings.

**Shear Wall Systems**
Concrete or reinforced masonry shear walls provide high stiffness and strength. Often used in residential mixed-use buildings with repetitive floor plans.

**Core Wall Systems**
Central core walls in high-rise buildings house elevators and stairs while providing lateral resistance. Outrigger systems may enhance performance in tall buildings.
