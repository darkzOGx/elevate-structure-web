## What Are Key Design Considerations for Chino Steel Buildings?

**Direct Answer:** Key considerations include seismic force-resisting system selection (moment frames vs. braced frames), connection design for ductility requirements, foundation design for lateral loads, and coordination with PEMB manufacturers when applicable.

**Seismic System Selection**
California's seismic requirements dictate steel building lateral systems. Options include special moment frames, special concentrically braced frames, buckling-restrained braced frames, and eccentrically braced frames.

**Connection Detailing**
Steel connections must meet ductility requirements for seismic design categories. Pre-qualified connections and demand-critical welds require detailed specification.

**Foundation Design**
Steel buildings require foundation systems resisting both gravity and lateral loads. Column bases, anchor bolts, and foundation ties need careful design.

**PEMB Coordination**
When using pre-engineered systems, California-specific engineering addresses seismic adaptations, local load requirements, and foundation design often not included in manufacturer packages.
