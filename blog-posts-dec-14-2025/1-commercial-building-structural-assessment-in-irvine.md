## What Is a Commercial Building Structural Assessment?

A commercial building structural assessment is a comprehensive evaluation performed by a licensed Professional Engineer to determine the structural integrity, safety, and code compliance of a commercial property. In California, these assessments analyze foundations, framing systems, connections, load paths, and overall building performance against current California Building Code (CBC) requirements and seismic design standards.

Commercial assessments in Irvine are particularly critical due to the city's concentration of Class A office buildings, technology campuses, and retail developments. The assessment identifies existing deficiencies, evaluates structural capacity for proposed modifications, and provides PE-stamped documentation required for transactions, permits, and financing.

## What Are Common Structural Issues Found in Irvine Commercial Buildings?

**Direct Answer:** Common issues include inadequate seismic bracing in pre-1994 buildings, foundation settlement from expansive soils, deteriorated tilt-up panel connections, roof diaphragm deficiencies, and tenant improvement modifications that compromised original structural design.

**Seismic Deficiencies (Pre-1994 Construction)**
Buildings constructed before the 1994 Northridge earthquake often lack adequate lateral bracing, moment frame connections, and foundation anchorage required by current codes. Assessment identifies retrofit needs and compliance paths.

**Foundation Settlement & Soil Issues**
Irvine's varied soil conditions, including expansive clays in some areas, can cause differential settlement affecting floor flatness, door operation, and structural connections.

**Tilt-Up Panel Connection Deterioration**
Older tilt-up warehouses and industrial buildings may have corroded or inadequate panel-to-roof connections requiring evaluation and potential strengthening.

**Unauthorized Tenant Modifications**
Previous tenants may have made structural modifications without permits, including mezzanine installations, wall removals, or equipment anchorage that affects building safety.
