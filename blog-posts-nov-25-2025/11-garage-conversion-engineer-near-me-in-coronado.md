**Direct Answer:**

## Why Do Coronado Garage Conversions Need Engineering?

**Direct Answer:**
Coronado garage conversions need engineering because garages weren't designed as habitable spaceâ€”they have lower ceiling heights, sloped floors, inadequate insulation, and different structural requirements than living areas. California Building Code requires habitable spaces to meet specific standards for floor loads (40 psf minimum), ceiling height (7' minimum), egress windows, and lateral resistance. A licensed engineer ensures conversions meet these requirements safely.

**Code Differences Between Garages and Living Space:**

**Floor Requirements**
- Garage: 4" slab, often sloped, designed for vehicle loads (point loads)
- Living: Level floor, 40 psf uniform live load, insulated

**Ceiling Height**
- Garage: No minimum (often 8-9')
- Living: 7' minimum habitable rooms
- Need to verify adequate height after raised floor

**Wall Construction**
- Garage: May have fire-rated assembly to house
- Living: Requires insulation, electrical, proper framing

**Egress Requirements**
- Garage: No egress windows required
- Living: Egress windows required in bedrooms

**Seismic/Lateral Systems**
- Garage: Relies on garage door opening (weak point)
- Living: Must provide adequate bracing

## What Are Common Garage Conversion Challenges in Coronado?

**Direct Answer:**
Common garage conversion challenges in Coronado include low ceiling heights requiring careful floor system design, sloped garage floors needing level floor solutions, inadequate foundations requiring reinforcement, parking replacement requirements, and historic district considerations. Solutions include engineered sleeper floor systems, foundation thickening, tandem parking configurations, and designs respecting neighborhood character.

**Challenge 1: Ceiling Height**

**Problem:** After adding raised floor, ceiling may be below 7' minimum.

**Solution:**
- Low-profile floor systems
- Recessing into existing slab
- Exposed ceiling (removing drywall)
- May eliminate some garages as candidates

**Challenge 2: Sloped Floors**

**Problem:** Garage floors slope 1/8" to 1/4" per foot for drainage.

**Solution:**
- Tapered sleepers
- Self-leveling systems
- Raised floor framing
- Engineering for drainage underneath

**Challenge 3: Foundation Adequacy**

**Problem:** Garage foundations may not meet living space requirements.

**Coronado Example:** A 1940s garage had 8" stem walls inadequate for new exterior door opening.

**Solution:**
- Foundation assessment
- Thickening if needed
- New footings for modifications
- Connection to existing structure
