## What Is Foundation Waterproofing Engineering in National City?

**Direct Answer:**
Foundation waterproofing engineering involves designing systems that prevent water infiltration through foundation walls, slabs, and joints. In National City, waterproofing designs typically combine membrane barriers, drainage boards, interior/exterior drainage systems, and sump management to address both groundwater pressure and surface water infiltration.

Waterproofing protects against liquid water penetration while dampproofing addresses only vapor transmission. Below-grade spaces and high water table areas require true waterproofing systems designed for hydrostatic pressure.

## What Waterproofing Systems Are Used in National City?

**Direct Answer:**
Waterproofing system types include sheet membrane systems for positive-side protection, liquid-applied membranes for complex geometries, drainage board systems for water management, and interior drainage systems for existing construction. Selection depends on construction type, water conditions, and access.

**Exterior Membrane Systems**
Applied to outside of foundation walls before backfilling. Provides positive-side waterproofing that keeps water away from concrete entirely.

**Interior Drainage Systems**
Interior French drains and sump systems manage water that reaches foundation interior. Common for retrofit applications where exterior access is limited.

**Drainage Board Systems**
Dimpled plastic sheets direct water to drains while protecting membrane from backfill damage. Essential component of comprehensive systems.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**What's the difference between waterproofing and dampproofing?**
Dampproofing resists moisture vapor but not liquid water under pressure. Waterproofing creates a complete barrier against water penetration including under hydrostatic pressure.

**Can existing foundations be waterproofed?**
Yes, though exterior waterproofing requires excavation. Interior drainage systems offer effective moisture management without excavation.
