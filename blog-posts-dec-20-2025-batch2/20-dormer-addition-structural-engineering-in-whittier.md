## What Is Dormer Addition Structural Engineering?

**Direct Answer:**
Dormer addition structural engineering involves designing roof projections that add headroom and windows to attic spaces, including dormer framing, roof structural modifications, floor reinforcement, and integration with existing structure. In Whittier, dormer designs address ceiling joist capacity, roof load redistribution, and seismic requirements.

Dormers increase usable floor area in attics by raising portions of the roof. This roof modification requires careful engineering to maintain structural integrity while adding windows and livable space.

## What Types of Dormers Work for Whittier Homes?

**Direct Answer:**
Common dormer types include gable dormers with peaked roofs matching home style, shed dormers providing maximum headroom with single-slope roofs, hip dormers for complex roof forms, and eyebrow dormers for architectural character.

**Gable Dormers**
Traditional peaked-roof dormers complement Whittier's older home styles. Multiple small dormers can be more attractive than single large dormers.

**Shed Dormers**
Full-width or partial shed dormers maximize usable space. Popular for practical attic conversions.

**Floor Reinforcement**
Existing ceiling joists typically need reinforcement or replacement to serve as floor joists for living space.
