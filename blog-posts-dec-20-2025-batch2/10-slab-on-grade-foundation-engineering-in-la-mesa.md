## What Is Slab-on-Grade Foundation Engineering in La Mesa?

**Direct Answer:**
Slab-on-grade foundation engineering involves designing concrete slabs poured directly on prepared soil to serve as both foundation and floor system. In La Mesa, slab designs typically include integral footings at perimeter and bearing walls, reinforcement for soil conditions, moisture barriers, and insulation for energy compliance. PE-stamped plans ensure adequate strength for building loads and resistance to soil movement.

The slab-on-grade system combines foundation and floor into one element, reducing construction steps and costs compared to separate crawl space or basement foundations. Proper engineering accounts for soil bearing capacity, expansion potential, and load distribution.

## What Types of Slab Foundations Are Used in La Mesa?

**Direct Answer:**
Slab foundation types include conventional reinforced slabs with rebar mesh, post-tensioned slabs with tensioned cables, monolithic pour slabs with integral footings, and thickened-edge slabs with increased perimeter depth. Selection depends on soil conditions, loads, and cost considerations.

**Conventional Reinforced Slabs**
Standard rebar reinforcement in grid pattern. Cost-effective for good soil conditions with minimal expansion potential.

**Post-Tensioned Slabs**
Steel cables tensioned after concrete cures provide active resistance to cracking. Preferred for expansive soils common in La Mesa.

**Monolithic Slabs**
Perimeter footings and slab poured together in single operation. Efficient for standard residential construction.
