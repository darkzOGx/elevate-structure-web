## What Are Common Raised Foundation Problems in Santee?

**Direct Answer:**
Common problems include deteriorated wood piers requiring replacement, sagging beams from rot or inadequate sizing, failed concrete block piers, moisture-damaged mudsills, and settlement causing uneven floors. Professional Engineers assess conditions and design appropriate repairs.

**Deteriorated Wood Piers**
Original wood posts decay over decades, especially in moist crawl spaces. Replacement with properly-sized posts on concrete pads restores support.

**Sagging Floor Beams**
Undersized or damaged beams cause bouncy or sloping floors. Sistering, replacement, or additional support restores proper load capacity.

**Moisture and Pest Damage**
Termites and moisture cause widespread wood deterioration. Comprehensive repairs address damage while improving crawl space conditions.
