## What Are the Structural Requirements for Irvine Home Additions?

**Direct Answer:**
Irvine home additions must comply with California Building Code seismic requirements, Title 24 energy standards, and local amendments. Key structural requirements include proper foundation design for Zone 4 seismic conditions, adequate lateral bracing, and verified load paths from roof to foundation through both new and existing construction.

**Seismic Design Requirements**

Irvine's Seismic Design Category D classification requires:
- Continuous load paths from roof to foundation
- Adequate shearwall lengths and distributions
- Proper hold-down anchorage at shearwall ends
- Foundation anchorage meeting current code

Older Irvine homes built to earlier codes may require upgrades where new construction connects.

**Foundation Requirements**

Typical Irvine soil conditions allow conventional foundation systems:
- Standard spread footings: 12-18" wide minimum
- Continuous footings under braced walls
- Slab-on-grade with appropriate reinforcement
- Connection to existing foundation per engineering details

**Framing Requirements**

Modern addition framing must meet:
- Minimum lumber grades (typically #2 or better)
- Engineered header sizes over openings
- Proper blocking and strapping
- Fire-blocking between floors
- Draft-stopping as required
