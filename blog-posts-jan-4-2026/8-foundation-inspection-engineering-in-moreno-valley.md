## What Are Common Foundation Issues in Moreno Valley?

**Quick Answer:** Moreno Valley foundations commonly exhibit expansive soil-related cracking, settlement from poor soil conditions, differential movement across building footprints, and age-related deterioration. Professional inspection distinguishes between significant structural issues and cosmetic conditions not requiring immediate action.

**Issue 1: Expansive Soil Movement**

**Symptoms:** Diagonal cracks at corners, doors/windows sticking, floor slope variations.

**Moreno Valley Context:** Many Moreno Valley areas have expansive soils causing seasonal movement. Inspection determines whether movement is within acceptable limits or indicates foundation inadequacy.

**Evaluation Approach:** We assess crack patterns, floor elevations, and movement indicators to determine severity and recommend appropriate response.

**Issue 2: Settlement**

**Symptoms:** Cracking concentrated in one area, noticeable floor slope toward one direction, separation at floor/wall junctions.

**Evaluation Approach:** Floor elevation surveys identify settlement patterns. We analyze causes and design underpinning or other repairs when warranted.

**Issue 3: Concrete Deterioration**

**Symptoms:** Spalling, exposed reinforcement, surface degradation.

**Evaluation Approach:** We assess deterioration extent and structural impact, recommending repair or monitoring based on severity.
