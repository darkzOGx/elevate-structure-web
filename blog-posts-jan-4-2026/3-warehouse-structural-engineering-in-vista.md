### Do I need a structural engineer for a warehouse in Vista?
Yes, California requires licensed Professional Engineers to design commercial and industrial building structures. The City of Vista Building Department requires PE-stamped structural plans for all warehouse construction and significant modifications.

### How long does warehouse engineering take in Vista?
New warehouse engineering typically takes 10-16 weeks from project start through permit-ready documents. Simple shell buildings may complete faster, while complex automated facilities may require additional time.

### What structural systems are common for Vista warehouses?
Steel braced frames and tilt-up concrete are most common for Vista warehouses. Pre-engineered metal buildings serve smaller facilities. System selection depends on span requirements, budget, and construction timeline.

### How much does warehouse engineering cost in Vista?
Warehouse structural engineering typically ranges from $15,000 for small buildings to $55,000+ for large distribution centers. Engineering fees generally run 0.5-1.5% of construction cost depending on complexity.
