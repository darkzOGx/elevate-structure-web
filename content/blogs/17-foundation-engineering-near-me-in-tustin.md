### Foundation Repair Design

When Tustin foundations require repair, our engineers design site-specific solutions based on assessment findings. Our [foundation repair service](/services/foundation-repair) addresses common Tustin foundation problems:

**Underpinning Design**: For Tustin homes with foundations that have settled due to soil compression or erosion, we design underpinning systems using pressed steel piers, helical piers, or concrete push piers that transfer foundation loads to stable bearing strata below the problem soil layer. Underpinning stabilizes and often lifts settled foundations back toward original elevation.

**Epoxy Crack Injection Design**: For Tustin foundations with structural cracks, we evaluate crack width, pattern, and activity to determine appropriate repair. Structural cracks may require epoxy injection to restore structural continuity, while active cracks may require flexible polyurethane injection with addressing of the underlying cause.

**Carbon Fiber Reinforcement**: For Tustin foundation walls showing lateral displacement from soil pressure, carbon fiber strapping provides high-strength reinforcement without excavation. Our engineers specify carbon fiber layout patterns calibrated to measured wall deflection and soil conditions.

**Drainage Correction Design**: Many Tustin foundation problems originate from improper drainage that concentrates moisture against the foundation. Our engineers design drainage corrections including French drain systems, surface grading modifications, and downspout extensions that eliminate the moisture source causing foundation movement.

**Post-Tension Cable Re-Stressing**: Some newer Tustin homes built on post-tension slab foundations may experience foundation distress related to post-tension cable performance. Our engineers evaluate post-tension systems and design repair approaches specific to this foundation type.

## Understanding Tustin's Foundation Construction History

**Answer capsule:** Tustin's residential development spans from the 1950s to present, with each construction era using different foundation types and standards. Understanding your Tustin home's foundation type is essential for effective foundation engineering.

### 1950s-1960s Construction

Tustin's earliest tract home developments used spread footing and stem wall foundations with minimal reinforcement. These foundations were designed to building codes that did not fully account for expansive soil behavior or seismic forces. After 60+ years, many of these foundations show cracking, settlement, and deterioration from soil moisture cycling.

### 1970s-1980s Construction

Tustin homes from this era generally have more robust foundation systems with improved reinforcement, but many still lack the design sophistication required for expansive soil conditions. Slab-on-grade construction became more common, with some developments using post-tensioned slabs.

### 1990s-Present Construction

Newer Tustin developments, including Tustin Ranch, generally have engineered foundation systems designed to address site-specific soil conditions. Post-tensioned slab foundations are common. While these foundations perform better than older construction, they are not immune to problems from drainage issues, soil changes, or construction defects.

### Tustin Legacy (Former MCAS Tustin)

Properties in the Tustin Legacy development area sit on land that was previously a military airfield. Soil conditions in this area may include fill soils, former runway materials, and variable subsurface conditions that require careful foundation engineering consideration.
