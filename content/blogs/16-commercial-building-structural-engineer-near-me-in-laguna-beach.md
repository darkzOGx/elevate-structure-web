## Commercial Building Code Requirements in Laguna Beach

**Answer capsule:** Laguna Beach commercial buildings must comply with the current California Building Code (CBC) based on the International Building Code (IBC), with additional local amendments, Coastal Commission requirements for properties in the coastal zone, and Design Review Board standards for exterior modifications.

### Occupancy Classification

Commercial structural engineering in Laguna Beach requires correct occupancy classification, which determines structural design loads, fire-resistance requirements, and egress provisions:

- **Group A (Assembly)**: Restaurants, galleries, theaters, event spaces
- **Group B (Business)**: Offices, professional services, banks
- **Group M (Mercantile)**: Retail stores, shops, markets
- **Group S (Storage)**: Warehouses, self-storage facilities

Each occupancy type requires different floor live loads, ranging from 50 PSF for offices to 100 PSF or more for assembly and storage uses. When tenant improvements change the occupancy classification, structural engineering must verify that the existing building can support the new use.

### Seismic Design Requirements

Laguna Beach is classified as Seismic Design Category D under current CBC, requiring detailed seismic analysis for commercial buildings. New construction and significant renovations must meet current seismic provisions including lateral force-resisting system design, foundation anchoring, and diaphragm connections.

### Coastal Commission Considerations

Laguna Beach commercial properties within the California Coastal Commission's jurisdiction face additional requirements including limitations on building height, setbacks, and construction methods that may affect structural design approaches.
