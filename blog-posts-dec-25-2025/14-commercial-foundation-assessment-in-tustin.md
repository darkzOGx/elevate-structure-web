## What Is a Commercial Foundation Assessment?

**Direct Answer:** A commercial foundation assessment is a comprehensive evaluation by a licensed Professional Engineer examining foundation condition, structural performance, and any deficiencies in commercial buildings. Commercial assessments differ from residential inspections in scope and complexity. Commercial buildings have larger foundations, higher loads, and often more complex structural systems requiring specialized engineering expertise.

## What Does a Commercial Foundation Assessment Include?

**Direct Answer:** A commercial foundation assessment in Tustin includes visual inspection of all accessible foundation elements, measurement of cracks or movement, review of available construction documents, analysis of any distress patterns, floor level survey if needed, and a comprehensive written report with PE certification.

**Assessment Components**

| Component | What's Examined |
|-----------|----------------|
| Exterior inspection | Foundation walls, grade beams, slab edges |
| Interior inspection | Accessible foundation areas, basement/crawl space |
| Floor survey | Level measurements across building |
| Crack mapping | Location, width, and pattern of cracks |
| Movement analysis | Evidence of settlement, heave, or lateral movement |
| Document review | Original plans, previous reports, permits |

**Report Contents**

- Executive summary of findings
- Detailed description of conditions
- Photographs documenting key observations
- Analysis of any deficiencies
- Recommendations for repairs if needed
- Opinion of structural adequacy
- PE stamp and certification

## What Foundation Issues Are Common in Tustin Commercial Buildings?

**Direct Answer:** Common foundation issues in Tustin commercial buildings include settlement from soil consolidation, slab cracking from loads or soil movement, expansion joint failures, joint deterioration, water intrusion, and reinforcement corrosion. The specific issues depend on building age, type, and site conditions.

**Settlement Issues**

Commercial buildings on fill or in areas with variable soils may experience differential settlement that cracks slabs and causes structural distress.

**Slab-on-Grade Problems**

- Control joint curling and spalling
- Random cracking from shrinkage or loading
- Surface deterioration from wear or chemical exposure
- Heaving from expansive soils

**Structural Foundation Issues**

- Cracking in grade beams or footings
- Tilt-up panel-to-foundation connection distress
- Column pedestal deterioration
- Reinforcement corrosion exposure
