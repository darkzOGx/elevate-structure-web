## What Are Helical Pier Foundation Systems?

**Direct Answer:** Helical piers are manufactured steel foundation elements with spiral-shaped bearing plates that are mechanically advanced into the ground to reach load-bearing soil or bedrock. They transfer structural loads through the helical plates to competent soil strata below problem soils, providing stable foundation support without extensive excavation.

Helical piers work particularly well in Hermosa Beach's sandy coastal soils because they can be installed through weak surface materials to reach denser layers below. The installation is low-vibration and minimizes disturbance to existing structures and neighboring properties in Hermosa Beach's closely-spaced residential areas.

For coastal communities like Hermosa Beach, Manhattan Beach, and Redondo Beach, helical piers offer advantages over traditional deep foundations including faster installation, immediate loading capability, and minimal site disturbance. ## How Do Helical Piers Work in Hermosa Beach?

**Direct Answer:** Helical piers are installed by rotating them into the ground using hydraulic equipment until the helical plates engage competent bearing soil or bedrock. The installation torque is monitored to verify adequate capacity, and structural brackets connect the piers to the existing or new foundation, transferring building loads to stable soil strata.

**Installation Process**

1. **Site Evaluation**: Our engineers assess soil conditions and determine required pier depth and capacity
2. **Pier Selection**: We specify appropriate shaft diameter and helix configuration for Hermosa Beach soils
3. **Mechanical Installation**: Piers are rotated into ground using specialized hydraulic equipment
4. **Torque Verification**: Installation torque confirms adequate bearing capacity reached
5. **Bracket Connection**: Structural brackets attach piers to foundation system
6. **Load Transfer**: Building weight transfers through piers to stable soil below

**Why Helical Piers Work in Coastal Soils**

Hermosa Beach's sandy, marine-influenced soils often have weak upper layers that can't adequately support structures. Helical piers bypass these problem soils entirely, anchoring into denser materials typically found 15-30 feet below grade in the South Bay.
