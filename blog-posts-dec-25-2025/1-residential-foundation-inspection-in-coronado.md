## What Are Common Foundation Problems in Coronado?

**Direct Answer:** Common foundation issues in Coronado include salt-related concrete deterioration, moisture intrusion from high water tables, settlement in sandy soils, drainage problems, and seismic-related cracking. The island's unique coastal environment creates conditions that accelerate these problems compared to inland locations.

**Salt Damage and Concrete Deterioration**

Coronado's salt air environment causes chloride intrusion in concrete, leading to reinforcement corrosion and spalling. We frequently identify this issue in properties near the beach and bay, particularly in homes built before modern protective coatings were standard.

**High Water Table Issues**

The island's low elevation and proximity to water means many Coronado properties experience hydrostatic pressure on foundations during wet seasons. This can cause cracking, water intrusion, and structural movement.

**Sandy Soil Settlement**

Sandy soils common throughout Coronado can settle unevenly over time, particularly after periods of heavy rain or irrigation changes. This differential settlement causes foundation cracking and structural distress.

**Drainage Deficiencies**

Older Coronado homes often have inadequate drainage systems that allow water to pool near foundations. Combined with the high water table, this creates conditions favorable for foundation problems.

## When Should You Get a Foundation Inspection in Coronado?

**Direct Answer:** You should schedule a foundation inspection in Coronado when purchasing a home, if you notice warning signs like cracks or sticking doors, after seismic events, every 5-7 years for preventive maintenance, or before major renovations. Coastal properties may benefit from more frequent inspections due to environmental factors.

**Warning Signs Requiring Immediate Inspection**

- Cracks in walls, floors, or ceilings (especially diagonal cracks)
- Doors or windows that stick or won't close properly
- Gaps between walls and floors or ceilings
- Sloping or uneven floors
- Cracks in the foundation visible from outside
- Water intrusion in basement or crawl space
- Musty odors indicating moisture problems

**Pre-Purchase Inspections**

Before buying a home in Coronado, a foundation inspection by a licensed engineer is essential. The high property values ($2.1M+ median) make it critical to understand any foundation issues before closing.
