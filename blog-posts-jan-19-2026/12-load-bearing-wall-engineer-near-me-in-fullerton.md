## How Do You Know If a Wall Is Load Bearing?

**Direct Answer:**
Load bearing walls typically run perpendicular to floor and ceiling joists, support structure above (another wall, floor, or roof), and transfer loads to the foundation. However, determining load-bearing status requires professional analysis of your specific home's framing system. Visual inspection alone cannot definitively identify all load bearing walls.

**Indicators of Load Bearing Walls:**

**Common Signs:**
- Wall runs perpendicular to joists above
- Wall sits directly over foundation or beam
- Wall supports visible structure above
- Wall is located in center of house
- Multiple walls align vertically

**Indicators of Non-Bearing Walls:**
- Wall runs parallel to joists above
- Wall doesn't align with structure below
- Wall was added during renovation
- Wall is thin (interior partition)

**Why Professional Analysis Is Essential:**

**Complex Situations:**
- Some parallel walls DO carry loads
- Point loads can bear on any wall
- Modified homes have altered load paths
- Partial walls may carry full loads

**Engineering Analysis Includes:**
- Review of home's overall structure
- Identification of load paths
- Calculation of actual loads
- Foundation support verification
