## What Does a Foundation Inspection Include in Fountain Valley?

**Direct Answer:**
A foundation inspection in Fountain Valley includes visual examination of foundation concrete for cracks and deterioration, elevation measurements to detect settlement, assessment of drainage conditions, evaluation of foundation connections to the structure above, and documentation with photographs and written report. ### Visual Examination

**Exterior Foundation**
Engineers examine accessible foundation exterior for cracks, spalling, discoloration, or other distress. Perimeter inspection reveals condition at grade line and above.

## What Are Common Foundation Issues in Fountain Valley?

**Direct Answer:**
Common foundation issues in Fountain Valley include settlement cracks from soil compression, moisture-related damage from poor drainage, concrete deterioration from age or environmental exposure, inadequate foundation bolting in older homes, and slab cracks from shrinkage or movement. The area's soil conditions and construction practices from different eras create various potential issues.

### Settlement and Movement

**Soil Compression**
As soils under foundations compress over time, settlement occurs. Uniform settlement causes few problems, but differential settlementâ€”where parts settle more than othersâ€”creates damage.

**Expansive Soils**
Some Fountain Valley soils expand and contract with moisture changes. This cyclical movement can damage foundations over time, creating cracks and displacement.

**Poor Compaction**
Foundations built on inadequately compacted fill may settle as soils consolidate. This is more common in areas developed on former agricultural land.

### Moisture-Related Problems

**Drainage Issues**
Poor drainage allows water to pool near foundations, saturating soils and potentially causing movement. Standing water also contributes to concrete deterioration.

**Plumbing Leaks**
Slab leaks or underground pipe failures introduce water under foundations. Persistent moisture causes soil changes and potential foundation problems.

**High Water Table**
In some Fountain Valley areas, high groundwater levels affect foundation performance and durability.

### Concrete Deterioration

**Age-Related Decay**
Concrete doesn't last forever. Older foundations may show deterioration including surface erosion, spalling, and material breakdown.

**Chemical Attack**
Soil chemistry can attack concrete. Sulfate-rich soils, common in some Orange County areas, gradually deteriorate concrete.

**Reinforcement Corrosion**
When moisture reaches steel reinforcement, corrosion causes expansion that cracks surrounding concrete.

### Structural Deficiencies

**Inadequate Bolting**
Many homes built before modern codes lack foundation bolts. This creates seismic vulnerability where homes can slide off foundations during earthquakes.

**Undersized Foundation**
Older foundations may be smaller than current codes require. While grandfathered, they may be inadequate for planned modifications.

### Slab Issues

**Shrinkage Cracks**
Concrete shrinks as it cures, creating common shrinkage cracks. These are usually cosmetic but should be evaluated.

**Slab Heave**
When soils under slabs expand, slabs can heave upward. This is particularly problematic with expansive soils.
