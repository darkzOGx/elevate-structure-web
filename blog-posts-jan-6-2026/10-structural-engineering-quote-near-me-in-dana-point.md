Larger projects require more engineering analysis, more detailed plans, and more coordination. Most engineers quote based partly on project square footage.

Long spans, cantilevers, unusual geometries, and non-standard materials require more engineering effort. A simple rectangular addition costs less to engineer than a complex curved design.

**Number of Stories**
Multi-story structures require analysis of load paths through multiple levels and may require more sophisticated structural systems.

### Site Conditions

**Hillside Location**
Dana Point's hillside neighborhoods present engineering challenges including slope stability, retaining walls, and foundation systems for sloped terrain. Hillside projects typically cost 20-40% more than flat-lot projects.

**Soil Conditions**
Dana Point soil conditions vary from sandy beach areas to clay hillsides. Poor soil conditions may require more extensive foundation engineering or geotechnical coordination.
