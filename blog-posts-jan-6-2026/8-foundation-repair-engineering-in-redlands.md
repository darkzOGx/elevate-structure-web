## What Types of Foundation Repair Services Are Available?

**Direct Answer:**
Foundation repair services include foundation evaluation and assessment, underpinning design, crack repair engineering, drainage improvement design, seismic retrofit, and post-repair verification. Redlands projects commonly address settlement from expansive soils and older foundation systems needing strengthening.

**Foundation Evaluation and Assessment**
Effective repair begins with understanding what's wrong and why. We conduct comprehensive foundation evaluations including visual inspection, elevation surveys, crack mapping, and condition assessment. Our evaluations identify the type and extent of distress and inform repair recommendations.

**Underpinning Design**
Underpinning extends foundation support to greater depth or transfers loads to more stable materials. We design underpinning systems including push piers, helical piers, and concrete underpinning appropriate for site conditions and structural requirements.

**Crack Repair Engineering**
Foundation cracks require evaluation to determine significance and appropriate repair. We assess crack patterns, movement indicators, and structural implications, then design repair approaches ranging from epoxy injection to structural reinforcement.

**Drainage Improvement Design**
Water is frequently the root cause of foundation problems. We design drainage improvements including surface grading modifications, French drains, and waterproofing systems that address moisture-related foundation distress.

**Seismic Retrofit**
Many older Redlands homes have foundations that don't meet current seismic standards. We design seismic retrofits including foundation bolting, cripple wall bracing, and foundation strengthening that improve earthquake resistance.

**Post-Repair Monitoring**
Following repairs, monitoring ensures problems are resolved. We provide monitoring recommendations and can conduct follow-up evaluations confirming repair effectiveness.

### Expansive Soil Settlement

**Soil Movement Effects**
Redlands' expansive clay soils shrink and swell with moisture changes, creating foundation movements. Seasonal cycles and irrigation variations cause repeated movements that can progressively damage foundations.

**Settlement Patterns**

### Foundation Cracking

**Crack Types**
Foundation cracks range from minor shrinkage cracks to significant structural fractures. Crack patterns provide clues about causesâ€”horizontal cracks may indicate lateral earth pressure, while diagonal cracks often indicate differential settlement.

### Drainage Problems

**Water Effects**
Poor drainage concentrates water near foundations, saturating soils and causing expansion. Adequate drainage is essential for long-term foundation performance in Redlands' climate.

### Seismic Vulnerability

**Older Foundations**
Many Redlands homes predate modern seismic codes and lack connections between foundation and wood framing. These homes are vulnerable to sliding off foundations during earthquakes.
