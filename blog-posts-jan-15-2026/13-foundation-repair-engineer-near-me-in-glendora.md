## What Foundation Problems Require Engineering?

Foundation problems in Glendora requiring engineering include significant cracking, settlement, heaving from expansive soils, water damage, and seismic damage. Engineering costs range from $2,000-$5,000 for assessment and repair design to $8,000-$15,000 for complex repairs with underpinning. **Foundation Settlement**
Settlement occurs when soil beneath foundations compresses or moves, causing sections to drop. Engineers evaluate settlement patterns, determine causes, and design repair methods such as underpinning or helical piers to stabilize and potentially lift settled foundations.

**Foundation Cracking**
While minor cracks may be cosmetic, significant cracking indicates structural movement requiring evaluation. Engineers determine whether cracks indicate ongoing problems, design repairs addressing underlying causes, and specify crack repair methods.

**Expansive Soil Damage**
Glendora-area expansive clay soils swell when wet and shrink when dry, creating foundation movement and damage. Engineers design solutions addressing soil behavior, drainage improvement, and foundation stabilization.

**Water and Drainage Damage**
Poor drainage undermines foundations and causes settlement. Engineers evaluate drainage conditions, design improvements, and specify foundation repairs addressing water-related damage.

### How do I know if my foundation needs repair?

Signs indicating potential foundation problems include visible cracks in foundation or walls, uneven or sloping floors, doors and windows that stick, gaps between walls and floors, and visible foundation movement. A professional engineer can evaluate whether symptoms indicate problems requiring repair.

### What causes foundation problems in Glendora?

Common Glendora foundation problem causes include expansive soils that swell and shrink, poor drainage undermining foundations, original construction deficiencies, seismic movement, and tree root damage. Engineers investigate to determine specific causes affecting individual properties.

### What is foundation underpinning?

Foundation underpinning involves extending foundation depth to reach stable soil, typically using pushed or helical piers installed beneath existing foundations. Underpinning stabilizes settled foundations and can sometimes lift them back toward original position. Engineers design underpinning systems appropriate for specific conditions.
