## Frequently Asked Questions

**How do I know if I need a structural engineer for my commercial project?**

You likely need a structural engineer if your project involves load-bearing elements, new openings in walls or floors, additions, heavy equipment installation, or seismic improvements. Torrance Building Division requires structural engineering for most commercial construction permits.

**How long does commercial structural engineering take?**

Timeline depends on project scope. Simple tenant improvements may take 2-4 weeks for engineering; complex projects may take 6-12 weeks. Plan check adds additional time, typically 4-8 weeks for commercial projects in Torrance.

**What permits are required for commercial construction in Torrance?**

Commercial construction requires building permits from Torrance Building Division. Depending on scope, you may also need mechanical, electrical, and plumbing permits. Some projects require planning approval or other agency permits.

**Can you help with emergency commercial structural issues?**

Yes. We provide emergency response for structural concerns in commercial buildings. Contact us immediately if you observe structural damage, collapse risk, or other urgent conditions requiring professional evaluation.

**Do you work with contractors and architects?**

**What information do I need to start a commercial structural engineering project?**

Helpful information includes building address, project description, available existing drawings, architectural plans if available, and project timeline. We can advise on additional requirements during initial consultation.

**Do you provide structural observation during construction?**
