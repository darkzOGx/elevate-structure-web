## What Does an ADU Structural Engineer Do in Placentia?

An ADU structural engineer near you in Placentia performs the engineering analysis and produces the structural construction documents required to build a code-compliant accessory dwelling unit. This work is technical, precise, and essential to both permit approval and construction safety.

### Structural Analysis and Design

Your ADU structural engineer analyzes gravity loads (dead loads, live loads, roof loads), lateral loads (wind and seismic forces), and soil conditions to determine the appropriate structural system for your Placentia ADU. This analysis produces specifications for:

- **Foundation design**: Concrete footings, grade beams, and slab-on-grade specifications sized for Placentia's soil conditions
- **Wall framing**: Stud sizes, spacing, sheathing requirements, and hold-down specifications for lateral force resistance
- **Roof and floor framing**: Beam sizes, joist specifications, span tables, and connection details
- **Seismic bracing**: Shear wall locations, anchor bolt patterns, and Simpson Strong-Tie connector schedules specific to Placentia's Seismic Design Category
- **Connection details**: Every structural connection from roof to foundation detailed for the building department

### Garage Conversion Engineering

Placentia's residential neighborhoods contain thousands of attached and detached garages that homeowners want to convert into ADUs. Garage conversion structural engineering is distinct from new construction because the engineer must evaluate the existing structure, determine what modifications are needed, and design reinforcements. Common engineering tasks for Placentia garage conversions include:

- Removing the garage door and designing a new structural header and infill wall
- Evaluating existing foundation adequacy (many Placentia garages have thinner slabs than living spaces require)
- Designing foundation upgrades including thickened edges, turned-down footings, or new grade beams
- Adding insulation support framing that meets Title 24 energy requirements
- Specifying seismic upgrades to bring the converted space to current code
- Designing new window and door openings with proper headers and trimmer studs

### Permit Document Preparation

The City of Placentia requires complete structural calculations and construction drawings for ADU permits. Your ADU structural engineer near you prepares:

- Structural calculation package showing code compliance
- Foundation plans with dimensions, reinforcing schedules, and anchor bolt layouts
- Framing plans showing all structural members, sizes, and spacing
- Structural detail sheets with connection specifications
- Title page with engineer's PE stamp and signature

These documents integrate with architectural plans from your designer or architect to form the complete permit submission package.

These landmark bills established that California cities cannot impose excessive barriers to ADU construction. ## What Are the Structural Requirements for Different ADU Types in Placentia?

Placentia homeowners have several ADU options, each with distinct structural engineering requirements. Understanding these differences helps you communicate effectively with your ADU structural engineer near you.

### Detached New Construction ADU

A brand new structure built separately from your existing Placentia home. This requires complete structural engineering from the ground up:

- **Foundation**: New concrete foundation designed for Placentia's soil conditions (typically expansive clay soils requiring specific footing depths and reinforcing)
- **Framing**: Complete wood frame or alternative structural system designed for gravity and lateral loads
- **Roof**: Engineered roof framing with appropriate load paths to foundation
- **Connections**: Complete Simpson Strong-Tie or equivalent connector schedule
- **Engineering cost**: $5,000 - $12,000 depending on size and complexity

### Garage Conversion ADU

Converting an existing Placentia garage into living space. Structural engineering focuses on evaluating and upgrading the existing structure:

- **Foundation evaluation**: Determining whether the existing garage slab meets residential live load requirements (40 psf vs. garage's 50 psf, but with different deflection criteria)
- **Garage door replacement**: Designing the structural infill wall where the garage door was removed, including new header, king studs, and cripple walls
- **Seismic upgrades**: Many Placentia garages lack adequate shear walls; conversion requires adding bracing
- **Floor system**: If the existing slab is inadequate, designing a raised floor system or slab topping
- **Engineering cost**: $3,500 - $7,000

### Attached ADU (Addition)

Building onto your existing Placentia home creates an attached ADU. Structural engineering must address:

- **Connection to existing structure**: Tying new framing into existing framing at the junction point
- **Foundation compatibility**: Matching new foundation depth and type with existing to prevent differential settlement
- **Roof integration**: Engineering the connection between new and existing roof structures
- **Existing structure impact**: Evaluating whether the existing home's structural system can accept new loads at the connection point
- **Engineering cost**: $4,500 - $10,000

### Junior ADU (JADU)

A JADU converts existing space within your Placentia home (bedroom, portion of house) into a separate unit up to 500 square feet. Structural engineering is typically minimal:

- **New exterior door**: Header and framing design for new doorway
- **Interior wall modifications**: Evaluating whether any walls being removed or modified are load-bearing
- **Kitchenette support**: Minor framing for new plumbing and electrical rough-ins
- **Engineering cost**: $2,000 - $4,000

## What Are Common Structural Challenges for Placentia ADU Projects?

Placentia's specific conditions create structural engineering challenges that experienced local engineers know how to address. Understanding these challenges helps you plan your ADU project effectively.

### Expansive Clay Soils

Much of Placentia sits on expansive clay soils that swell when wet and shrink when dry. This soil movement imposes forces on foundations that engineers must design for. ADU foundations in Placentia often require deeper footings (18-24 inches minimum), increased reinforcing steel, and moisture barriers compared to areas with stable soils. Your engineer may recommend a [foundation engineering assessment](/services/foundation-engineering) for complex soil conditions.

### Aging Garage Structures

Many Placentia garages were built in the 1960s and 1970s when the city experienced its primary residential development boom. These structures are now 50-60 years old with potentially deteriorated framing, outdated connections, and foundations that do not meet current residential standards. Thorough evaluation by your ADU structural engineer near you is essential before assuming a garage conversion is straightforward.

### Seismic Design Requirements

Placentia sits in Seismic Design Category D, meaning all new construction and major renovations must resist significant earthquake forces. ADU structures require engineered shear walls, foundation anchor bolts, hold-downs at shear wall ends, and continuous load paths from roof to foundation. Garage conversions must be upgraded to current seismic standards even though the original garage may have been built to less stringent requirements.

### Utility Trenching Near Existing Foundations

Running new utility lines (sewer, water, gas, electrical) to detached ADUs requires trenching that may pass near your existing home's foundation. Your structural engineer evaluates the safe distance for trenching and specifies protection measures for existing foundations when utilities must pass within the zone of influence.

### Two-Story ADU Challenges

Recent legislation allowing taller ADUs opens the possibility of two-story detached units in Placentia. Two-story wood frame structures require significantly more engineering than single-story, including second-floor diaphragm design, increased shear wall requirements, and more robust foundations to support the additional weight and height.

### Setback Limitations

Placentia's reduced 4-foot setbacks for ADUs place structures close to property lines. This proximity affects foundation design (footings cannot extend beyond property lines), requires firewall construction on zero-lot-line walls, and may limit structural system options for the affected walls.
