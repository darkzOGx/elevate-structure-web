## What Are Common Foundation Inspection Findings in La Quinta?

The most common findings include cosmetic cracking from thermal cycling, settlement-related problems from soil conditions, and age-related deterioration in older properties. Professional engineers distinguish between significant issues requiring attention and normal conditions requiring only monitoring.

**Finding 1: Thermal Cracking**

**What It Is:** Fine cracks in slabs and foundation walls caused by daily temperature cycling in the desert climate.

**Engineering Assessment:** Thermal cracks are typically cosmetic unless they exceed 1/4 inch width or show differential movement. We assess whether cracks are structural or merely thermal, and recommend sealing if needed to prevent moisture intrusion.

**Finding 2: Settlement Patterns**

**What It Is:** Differential foundation movement evidenced by sloped floors, doors that don't latch, and stepped or diagonal cracking.

**La Quinta Example:** A recent PGA West inspection revealed 1.5 inches of differential settlement across the home caused by irrigation-induced soil expansion on one side. The report enabled negotiation of repair credits.

**Engineering Assessment:** We measure floor levels precisely to quantify settlement, assess whether movement is ongoing or stabilized, and provide repair recommendations if needed.

**Finding 3: Post-Tensioning Issues**

**What It Is:** Problems with post-tensioned slab systems including tendon corrosion, strand failure, or inadequate stressing.

**Engineering Assessment:** Post-tensioned foundations require specialized evaluation. We assess anchor conditions, check for corrosion evidence, and recommend testing if concerns are identified.
