## What Is Data Center Structural Engineering?

Data center structural engineering involves designing buildings and structural systems that support concentrated IT equipment loads, provide enhanced seismic protection for critical infrastructure, and accommodate the specialized mechanical and electrical systems these facilities require.

Key engineering considerations include:
- High floor load capacities (150-300+ PSF)
- Raised floor system support
- Equipment vibration isolation
- Seismic protection for essential facilities
- Redundancy in structural systems

## What Structural Requirements Apply to Data Centers?

**Floor Load Capacity**
Data centers require significantly higher floor loads than typical commercial buildings:
- Standard office: 50-100 PSF
- Data center: 150-300+ PSF
- High-density computing: 300-500+ PSF

**Raised Floor Systems**
Elevated floor systems for cable routing and cooling distribution require:
- Pedestal and stringer design
- Panel load ratings
- Seismic anchorage
- Opening coordination

**Equipment Anchorage**
Servers, UPS systems, and cooling equipment require seismic anchorage design per CBC and ASCE 7 requirements for essential facilities.
