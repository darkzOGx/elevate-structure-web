## What Is Medical Office Building Structural Engineering?

Medical office building structural engineering involves the design of healthcare facilities to meet both standard building code requirements and the enhanced performance expectations for medical occupancies. Licensed Professional Engineers design structural systems that accommodate medical equipment loads, provide enhanced seismic performance, and support the critical mechanical and electrical systems healthcare facilities require.

While most medical office buildings (MOBs) are not regulated by OSHPD (Office of Statewide Health Planning and Development), they often require enhanced engineering for:
- Heavy medical imaging equipment (MRI, CT, X-ray)
- Vibration-sensitive diagnostic equipment
- Essential facility considerations for certain classifications
- Future flexibility for equipment changes

## What Are the Structural Requirements for Medical Equipment?

**MRI Equipment**
Magnetic resonance imaging equipment presents unique structural challenges:
- Equipment weights: 10,000-25,000+ pounds
- Vibration sensitivity requiring careful floor design
- RF shielding room structural coordination
- Cryogen quench pipe support

**CT Scanners**
Computed tomography equipment requires:
- Floor load capacity: 500-800 PSF concentrated loads
- Deflection limits for equipment calibration
- Radiation shielding room design

**Linear Accelerators**
Radiation therapy equipment requires:
- Concrete vault construction
- Specialized foundation design
- Maze entry configurations
