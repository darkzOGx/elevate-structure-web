## What Is Seismic Retrofit Engineering?

Seismic retrofit engineering involves evaluating existing buildings' earthquake resistance and designing structural modifications to improve performance during seismic events. Licensed Professional Engineers identify vulnerabilities, design retrofit solutions, and prepare PE-stamped plans for building permit approval.

Many older Hermosa Beach homes were built before modern seismic codes and may lack critical connections between foundations and wood framing, leaving them vulnerable to sliding or collapse during earthquakes.

## What Types of Seismic Retrofits Are Common in Hermosa Beach?

**Foundation Bolting**
Connecting wood sill plates to concrete foundations with anchor bolts. Essential for homes without original bolting.

**Cripple Wall Bracing**
Strengthening short wood-stud walls between foundation and first floor with structural plywood. Prevents collapse of this vulnerable zone.

**Soft-Story Retrofit**
Strengthening buildings with open ground floors (parking, retail) that are weaker than upper floors. Steel moment frames or shear walls added.

**Floor-to-Foundation Connections**
Adding hardware connecting floor framing to foundation when existing connections are inadequate.

**Chimney Bracing**
Securing unreinforced masonry chimneys that can collapse and damage homes during earthquakes.
