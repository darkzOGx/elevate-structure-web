## What Is Load Bearing Wall Removal Engineering?

Load bearing wall removal engineering involves analyzing wall loads, designing replacement beam and post systems, and ensuring the modified structure continues to safely carry all vertical and lateral loads. Licensed Professional Engineers determine which walls are load-bearing, calculate required beam sizes, design post footings, and prepare PE-stamped plans required for building permits.

## How Do You Identify Load Bearing Walls?

**Indicators of Load-Bearing Walls:**
- Walls running perpendicular to floor/ceiling joists
- Walls aligned with posts in basement or crawl space
- Walls supporting upper floors or roof structure
- Walls at the center of the home

**Engineering Evaluation Process:**
Our engineers examine:
- Original construction documents (if available)
- Framing direction and support
- Load paths from roof to foundation
- Wall locations relative to structure above

## What Beam Systems Replace Load Bearing Walls?

**Flush Beams**
Steel or engineered wood beams installed within ceiling depth, providing a flat ceiling after completion. Requires adequate ceiling height for beam depth.

**Exposed Beams**
Beams extending below ceiling level, often wrapped or finished decoratively. Common when ceiling height allows or when architectural character is desired.

**Steel Beams**
Wide flange (W-shape) or built-up steel beams provide maximum strength with minimum depth. Common for long spans or heavy loads.

**LVL/PSL Beams**
Engineered wood products (Laminated Veneer Lumber, Parallel Strand Lumber) provide high strength in natural wood appearance. Multiple plies achieve required capacity.

**Glulam Beams**
Glued laminated timber provides both structural capacity and architectural quality for exposed applications.
