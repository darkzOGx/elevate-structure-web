## What Is Earthquake-Resistant Design?

Earthquake-resistant design involves engineering building structures to resist seismic forces and maintain integrity during earthquakes. Licensed Professional Engineers analyze seismic loads, design lateral force-resisting systems, and detail connections to ensure buildings perform safely during and after seismic events.

California Building Code (CBC) requires all new construction to meet rigorous seismic design requirements, making earthquake-resistant design integral to all structural engineering projects in Westminster.

## What Are the Key Elements of Earthquake-Resistant Design?

**Lateral Force-Resisting System (LFRS)**
The primary system resisting earthquake forces:
- Shear walls (wood, steel, or concrete)
- Braced frames
- Moment frames
- Combinations of systems

**Diaphragm Design**
Floors and roofs that transfer lateral forces to shear walls:
- Wood structural panel diaphragms
- Concrete slabs
- Metal deck systems

**Foundation Design**
Foundations that anchor the building and resist overturning:
- Spread footings with seismic ties
- Mat foundations
- Deep foundations for poor soils

**Connection Design**
Hardware and details connecting structural elements:
- Hold-downs and straps
- Anchor bolts
- Beam-to-column connections
- Collector connections

## How Does Seismic Design Work for New Construction in Westminster?

Earthquake-resistant design is integrated into the structural engineering process for all new construction.

**Seismic Design Parameters**

For Westminster, seismic design is based on:
- Seismic Design Category: D (typical for Orange County)
- Site Class: Based on soil conditions (typically D)
- Seismic coefficients from ASCE 7 maps
- Importance factor based on occupancy

**Design Process**

1. **Load Determination**: Calculate seismic base shear based on building weight and seismic parameters
2. **System Selection**: Choose appropriate lateral system for building type
3. **Distribution**: Distribute seismic forces to individual elements
4. **Member Design**: Size shear walls, braces, and frames
5. **Connection Design**: Detail connections to transfer forces
6. **Foundation Design**: Ensure foundations resist seismic forces

## What Makes Buildings Earthquake-Resistant?

**Structural Regularity**
Regular, symmetric buildings perform better than irregular shapes:
- Simple floor plans
- Consistent stiffness throughout height
- Minimal setbacks and projections

**Ductility**
Ability to deform without failing:
- Proper connection details
- Steel reinforcement in concrete
- Adequate fastening in wood

**Redundancy**
Multiple load paths so failure of one element doesn't cause collapse:
- Multiple shear walls
- Distributed resistance
- Backup systems

**Quality Construction**
Proper construction matching design intent:
- Special inspections
- Testing of materials
- Quality control
