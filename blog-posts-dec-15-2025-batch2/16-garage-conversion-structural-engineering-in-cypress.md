## What Is Garage Conversion Structural Engineering?

Garage conversion structural engineering involves modifying existing garage structures to meet residential occupancy requirements. Licensed Professional Engineers assess existing conditions, design required modifications for floor systems, wall enclosures, and lateral systems, and prepare PE-stamped plans for building permits.

California's ADU laws have made garage conversions increasingly popular, but structural engineering remains essential to ensure safe, code-compliant living spaces.

## What Structural Modifications Are Required for Garage Conversions?

**Floor System Upgrades**
Many garage slabs are thinner and less reinforced than residential floor requirements:
- Slab thickness evaluation (minimum 4" for living space)
- Reinforcement assessment
- Overlay design if slab is inadequate
- Moisture barrier and insulation

**Garage Door Opening Enclosure**
Replacing the garage door with code-compliant wall:
- Load path design for overhead structure
- Shear wall or braced wall requirements
- Window and door rough openings
- Connection to existing structure

**Lateral Force-Resisting System**
Converting garage to living space changes the building's lateral system:
- Analysis of existing shear walls
- Design of new shear elements
- Hold-down connections
- Foundation anchorage

**Ceiling and Roof**
Garage ceilings often require modification:
- Ceiling height requirements (7'-6" minimum)
- Insulation requirements
- Access panel elimination
- Proper fire separation (if attached)
