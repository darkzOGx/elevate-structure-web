## What Is ADU Structural Engineering?

ADU structural engineering involves the design and documentation of foundation, floor, wall, and roof systems for accessory dwelling units. Licensed Professional Engineers prepare PE-stamped structural plans and calculations required by Brea Building Division for ADU permit applications.

California's ADU laws have streamlined permitting for accessory dwelling units up to 1,200 square feet, but structural engineering remains essential to ensure safe construction that meets California Building Code requirements.

## What Types of ADUs Require Structural Engineering in Brea?

**Detached ADUs**
Standalone structures in backyards require complete structural design including:
- Foundation system (slab or raised floor)
- Wall framing and lateral bracing
- Roof structure and connections
- Seismic and wind load resistance

**Garage Conversions**
Converting existing garages to living space requires:
- Floor system modifications (if slab is inadequate)
- Wall reinforcement and finishing
- Roof/ceiling modifications
- New window and door openings
- Removal of garage door opening infill

**Attached ADUs**
Units attached to existing homes require:
- Structural separation or integration
- Shared wall considerations
- Independent lateral systems
- Foundation coordination

**Junior ADUs (JADUs)**
Units under 500 SF within existing homes require:
- Limited structural modifications
- Separation requirements
- Egress compliance

## What Are Brea's ADU Requirements?

**Size Limits**
- Detached ADU: Up to 1,200 SF
- Attached ADU: Up to 1,000 SF or 50% of primary dwelling
- JADU: Up to 500 SF within existing space

**Setbacks**
- Side and rear: 4 feet (may be reduced in some cases)
- Front: Standard front setback
- Conversions of existing structures: No new setbacks required

**Height Limits**
- Detached ADU: Generally 16 feet
- ADU above garage: May vary by zone

**Parking**
- No additional parking required within ½ mile of transit
- Replaced parking for garage conversions waived in many cases

## What Are Common ADU Engineering Challenges in Brea?

**Challenge 1: Garage Conversion Foundation**

**Problem:** Many garage slabs are thinner and less reinforced than required for living space, requiring evaluation and potential modification.

**Brea Example:** A 1970s garage slab was only 3" thick without reinforcement, inadequate for ADU floor loads.

**Solution:** We designed a concrete overlay with proper reinforcement to create a code-compliant floor system without complete slab removal.

**Challenge 2: Maximizing ADU Size**

**Solution:** Careful design optimization to achieve maximum living area within code constraints, including creative solutions for challenging lot configurations.

**Challenge 3: Second-Story ADUs**

**Problem:** Adding ADU above existing garage or new structure requires careful structural design for loads and lateral forces.

**Solution:** Complete structural analysis and design of the support structure, connections, and lateral system for multi-story ADU configurations.
