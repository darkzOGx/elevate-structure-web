## Why Beverly Hills Corporate Campuses Need Specialized Engineering

### Seismic Design Requirements

Beverly Hills is located within the Los Angeles Basin seismic zone, requiring all corporate structures to meet California Building Code (CBC) 2025 seismic provisions. Our engineers design buildings to withstand lateral forces using:

- **Steel moment frames** for open floor plans
- **Braced frame systems** for parking structures
- **Base isolation systems** for premium protection
- **Damping systems** for high-rise towers

### Unique Beverly Hills Building Considerations

Beverly Hills corporate campuses face unique engineering challenges:

1. **Height restrictions** - Zoning limits require creative massing
2. **Underground parking** - Multi-level subterranean garages essential
3. **Historic district proximity** - Design compatibility requirements
4. **Luxury finishes** - Engineering must accommodate premium materials
5. **Sustainability requirements** - LEED and Title 24 compliance

- **Steel connection upgrades** for pre-Northridge buildings

### Do I need a structural engineer for a Beverly Hills office tenant improvement?

Yes, most Beverly Hills office tenant improvements require structural engineering when the work involves removing walls, adding floor penetrations, installing heavy equipment, or modifying the building structure. ### What seismic requirements apply to Beverly Hills corporate buildings?

Beverly Hills corporate buildings must meet CBC 2025 seismic requirements based on Risk Category II or III (for essential facilities). Buildings must be designed for Site Class D soil conditions typical of the Los Angeles Basin, with Seismic Design Category D or E requirements.

### How much does a corporate building seismic retrofit cost in Beverly Hills?

Corporate building seismic retrofits in Beverly Hills typically cost $30,000-$150,000 for engineering, plus $50-$150 per square foot for construction. Costs vary based on building type, existing construction, and retrofit approach selected.
