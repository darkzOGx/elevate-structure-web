## What Is Commercial Seismic Retrofit Engineering?

**Direct Answer:** Commercial seismic retrofit engineering involves evaluating existing commercial buildings for earthquake vulnerability and designing structural modifications to improve seismic performance. California-licensed Professional Engineers analyze existing lateral force-resisting systems, identify deficiencies, and design retrofit measures including steel moment frames, shear walls, foundation improvements, and connection upgrades.

Older commercial buildings were designed to earlier code editions with lower seismic force requirements and less stringent detailing. Retrofit engineering brings these buildings closer to current standards, protecting occupants, property, and business continuity.

## When Is Commercial Seismic Retrofit Required?

**Direct Answer:** Commercial seismic retrofit may be required during major renovation exceeding 50% of building value, change of occupancy to higher-risk use, mandatory retrofit ordinances, or voluntary upgrade for risk reduction. Some cities have mandatory retrofit programs for specific building types.
