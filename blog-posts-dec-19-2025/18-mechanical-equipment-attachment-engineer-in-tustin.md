## What Are Common Equipment Attachment Challenges?

**Direct Answer:** Common challenges include inadequate roof structure capacity for heavy equipment, coordinating seismic restraints with vibration isolators, rooftop anchorage near roof edges, and retrofitting anchorage for replacement equipment.

**Challenge 1: Roof Structure Capacity**

**Problem:** Existing roof structures may not have adequate capacity for new equipment weight plus seismic forces.

**Tustin Example:** A commercial building roof required reinforcement to support three new 5-ton rooftop units during a HVAC upgrade project.

**Solution:** We analyze existing roof framing capacity and design reinforcement or equipment stands to distribute loads safely.

**Challenge 2: Vibration Isolation Coordination**

**Problem:** Vibration isolators reduce equipment connection stiffness, affecting seismic restraint behavior and requiring special consideration.

**Solution:** We design seismic restraint systems compatible with vibration isolation, using snubbers, restraint housings, or integrated seismic-isolation systems.

**Challenge 3: Edge and Corner Locations**

**Problem:** Equipment located near roof edges has limited anchorage options and may require special support structures.

**Solution:** We design equipment stands or modified anchorage patterns that provide adequate load resistance away from roof edges.
