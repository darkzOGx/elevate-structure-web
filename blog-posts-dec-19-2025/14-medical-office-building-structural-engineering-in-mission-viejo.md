## What Is Medical Office Building Structural Engineering?

**Direct Answer:** Medical office building structural engineering involves designing structural systems for healthcare facilities including foundations, framing, heavy equipment supports, and radiation shielding structures. California-licensed Professional Engineers design structures accommodating medical equipment loads, vibration requirements, and specialized diagnostic imaging rooms.

Medical office buildings require careful consideration of heavy equipment loads from MRI machines, CT scanners, and other diagnostic equipment. Some facilities may require OSHPD/HCAi oversight depending on licensing and procedure types.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Do medical office buildings require OSHPD approval?**
OSHPD/HCAi oversight is required for certain licensed healthcare facilities. General medical office buildings without surgery centers typically don't require OSHPD review.

**What structural considerations apply to MRI rooms?**
MRI rooms require vibration isolation, heavy equipment pads, and coordination with magnetic shielding. Structural systems must minimize vibration from adjacent uses.
