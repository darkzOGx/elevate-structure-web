## What Is Commercial Tenant Improvement Engineering?

**Direct Answer:** Commercial tenant improvement engineering provides structural analysis and documentation for modifications to existing commercial buildings during tenant buildouts, including mezzanine additions, floor penetrations, demising wall construction, and equipment supports. California-licensed Professional Engineers verify that proposed modifications maintain structural integrity and meet CBC requirements.

## When Do Tenant Improvements Require Structural Engineering?

**Direct Answer:** Structural engineering is required when tenant improvements involve modifications to existing structural elements, addition of significant loads, or construction of new structural components including mezzanines, floor reinforcement, large equipment supports, and major penetrations.

**Common TI Situations Requiring Engineering:**

| Modification Type | Engineering Needed | Typical Turnaround |
|------------------|-------------------|-------------------|
| Mezzanine addition | Yes - full structural | 2-3 weeks |
| Floor penetrations | Yes - beam analysis | 1-2 weeks |
| Heavy equipment | Yes - load analysis | 1-2 weeks |
| Demising walls | Sometimes - depends | 1 week |
| Standard partitions | Usually no | N/A |
| Storefront changes | Yes - header design | 1-2 weeks |
| HVAC roof units | Yes - curb/support | 1-2 weeks |

**Mezzanine and Platform Additions**

Adding raised platforms or mezzanines within tenant spaces requires complete structural design including column placement, beam sizing, connection details, and foundation analysis for new loads.

**Floor Penetrations**

Cutting new openings in floor slabs for stairs, escalators, or utilities requires analysis of existing framing and potential reinforcement design.

**Heavy Equipment Installation**

Equipment exceeding typical floor load capacity‚Äîservers, printing presses, safes, commercial kitchen equipment‚Äîrequires load analysis and potential floor reinforcement.
