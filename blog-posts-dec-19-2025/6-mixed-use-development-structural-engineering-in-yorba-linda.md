## What Are Common Mixed-Use Structural Challenges?

**Direct Answer:** Common challenges include designing efficient podium transitions, accommodating different floor-to-floor heights, integrating parking structures, managing differential movement between construction types, and meeting fire-resistance requirements for mixed occupancies.

**Challenge 1: Podium Transitions**

**Problem:** The transition from concrete podium to wood-frame residential requires careful detailing to manage load transfer and differential movement.

**Solution:** We design robust podium connections and movement joints to accommodate wood shrinkage and concrete creep.

**Challenge 2: Variable Floor Heights**

**Problem:** Retail spaces typically require 14-18 foot ceilings while residential uses 9-10 feet, creating level changes that affect structural design.

**Solution:** We coordinate structural depths with architectural requirements to achieve efficient floor-to-floor heights.
