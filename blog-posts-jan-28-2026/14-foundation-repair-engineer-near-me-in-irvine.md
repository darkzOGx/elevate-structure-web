## What Foundation Issues Are Common in Irvine?

**Direct Answer:** Common Irvine foundation issues include settlement from original grading, expansive soil movement in some areas, poor drainage concentrating water near foundations, and age-related deterioration in older neighborhoods. Different Irvine communities experience different issues based on soil conditions and construction era.

**Settlement Issues**
Some Irvine properties experience settlement as fill soils placed during original development continue to consolidate. Settlement appears as cracks, sloping floors, and door/window operation problems. Settlement may be ongoing or may have stabilized.

**Expansive Soil Movement**
Certain Irvine areas contain expansive clay soils that swell when wet and shrink when dry. This cyclical movement causes foundation distress including cracking and heaving. Expansive soil problems often correlate with irrigation patterns and drainage conditions.

**Drainage-Related Problems**
Poor drainage allows water to saturate soil near foundations, causing both erosion and soil expansion. Many Irvine foundation problems trace to inadequate drainage that can be corrected. Drainage improvements often accompany foundation repair.

**Age-Related Deterioration**
Older Irvine neighborhoods from the 1970s and 1980s have foundations approaching 50 years old. Age-related deterioration may include concrete degradation, reinforcing corrosion, and accumulated damage from minor earthquakes.
