**Caisson Foundation Design**
Caisson (drilled pier) foundations provide deep support reaching stable soil or bedrock below unstable surface materials. Our engineers design caisson foundations sized for structural loads and lateral forces, coordinating with geotechnical recommendations for depth and capacity. Caisson spacing and configuration optimize structural performance while managing construction costs.

**Grade Beam Engineering**
Grade beams connect caissons at the surface, distributing building loads and providing continuous support for framing above. Our engineers design grade beam systems that accommodate slope variations, coordinate with architectural layouts, and resist seismic and soil pressure forces. Grade beam reinforcing details ensure adequate strength and ductility.

**Retaining Wall Design**
Hillside construction typically requires retaining walls creating level building areas and supporting cut-and-fill slopes. Our engineers design retaining structures ranging from cantilever walls to soldier pile systems, addressing earth pressures, surcharge loads, and seismic requirements specific to Laguna Beach conditions.

**Foundation Repair and Stabilization**
Existing hillside homes may experience foundation distress from settlement, slope movement, or inadequate original construction. Our engineers evaluate existing foundation conditions, design repair measures including underpinning and stabilization, and produce PE-stamped plans for permit approval.

### What is a caisson foundation and when is it required?
A caisson (drilled pier) foundation is a cylindrical concrete column drilled deep into the ground to reach stable soil or bedrock. Caissons are required on hillsides where surface soils are unstable, slopes create lateral forces on shallow foundations, or bedrock provides the only adequate bearing. Most Laguna Beach hillside projects require caisson foundations.

### Do I need a geotechnical report for hillside foundation engineering?
Yes, geotechnical investigation is typically required for hillside foundation engineering in Laguna Beach. Geotechnical reports provide soil information, slope stability assessment, and foundation recommendations that structural engineers use to design appropriate foundations. Building departments require geotechnical reports for hillside construction permits.

### What are the signs of foundation problems on hillside homes?
Signs of hillside foundation problems include cracked walls or foundations, doors and windows that don't close properly, sloping floors, gaps between walls and ceilings, sticking doors, and visible foundation movement or rotation. If you observe these signs, a structural engineer should evaluate your foundation condition.

### How does Coastal Commission affect hillside foundation engineering in Laguna Beach?
Most Laguna Beach properties fall within Coastal Commission jurisdiction, requiring Coastal Development Permits for construction. Foundation engineering must address both structural requirements and coastal development standards including grading limitations, drainage management, and visual impact. Engineers should understand these requirements.

### Can hillside foundations be repaired or strengthened?
Yes, hillside foundations can be repaired or strengthened through underpinning (adding deeper support), additional caissons, retaining wall repair or addition, and drainage improvements. Repair approach depends on the cause and extent of foundation distress. A structural engineer should evaluate conditions and design appropriate repairs.
