## Frequently Asked Questions

### How quickly can an emergency structural engineer respond in Atherton?

For immediate life-safety concerns, we provide phone guidance while dispatching and can coordinate with local emergency services. True 24/7 availability ensures response regardless of time or day.

### What should I do if I notice sudden foundation cracks after an earthquake?

Immediately evacuate the structure if cracks are severe or accompanied by visible structural distress. Document the damage photographically from a safe distance. Contact an emergency structural engineer for assessment before re-entering. Do not attempt repairs until professional evaluation determines the extent of damage and structural stability.

### Does insurance cover emergency structural engineering in Atherton?

Most homeowner's policies cover emergency structural assessment when related to covered events (earthquake with earthquake coverage, fire, storm damage, vehicle impact). We provide documentation formatted for insurance submission. Pre-event assessments (inspections not related to damage) typically are not covered.

### What is the difference between emergency assessment and permanent repair design?

Emergency assessment determines immediate safety and stabilization needs—typically completed in hours. Permanent repair design involves detailed engineering, permit drawings, and contractor coordination—typically taking 2-6 weeks. Emergency stabilization bridges the gap, protecting the structure until permanent repairs proceed.

### How much does emergency shoring cost for an Atherton estate?

Emergency shoring for Atherton properties typically ranges from $15,000-$75,000 depending on structure size and damage extent. This includes engineering design ($2,500-$8,000), contractor installation, and monitoring. Costs are generally covered by insurance for covered damage events.
