## Types of Foundation Cracks

### Vertical Cracks

**Characteristics:**
- Run straight up and down
- Usually in foundation walls
- Common in poured concrete

**Typical Causes:**
- Concrete shrinkage during curing
- Minor settlement
- Temperature changes
- Normal concrete behavior

**Severity:**
- Generally least concerning
- Monitor for changes
- Seal to prevent water intrusion
- Rarely indicate structural problems

### Horizontal Cracks

**Characteristics:**
- Run parallel to ground
- Often in stem walls
- May show inward displacement

**Typical Causes:**
- Lateral soil pressure
- Frost heave (rare in Carlsbad)
- Hydrostatic pressure
- Soil expansion pressure

**Severity:**
- More concerning than vertical
- May indicate wall failure risk
- Professional assessment recommended
- Often require engineering intervention

### Diagonal Cracks

**Characteristics:**
- Run at 45-degree angles
- Often step-pattern in masonry
- May radiate from corners

**Typical Causes:**
- Differential settlement
- Point loading issues
- Foundation movement
- Soil bearing failure

**Severity:**
- Significant concern
- Indicate movement/settlement
- Professional assessment essential
- May require underpinning

### Stair-Step Cracks

**Characteristics:**
- Follow mortar joints in masonry/block
- Step pattern appearance
- Common in CMU foundations

**Typical Causes:**
- Differential settlement
- Soil movement
- Foundation rotation
- Lateral pressure

**Severity:**
- Moderate to high concern
- Indicate foundation movement
- Engineering assessment needed
- Repair typically required

### Pattern/Map Cracking

**Characteristics:**
- Random interconnected pattern
- Surface-level typically
- "Alligator" appearance

**Typical Causes:**
- Concrete shrinkage
- Surface drying too fast
- Mix issues
- Age-related deterioration

**Severity:**
- Usually cosmetic
- May allow moisture entry
- Monitor for deepening
- Surface treatment often sufficient

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How do I know if a foundation crack is serious in Carlsbad?

Serious cracks typically are: wider than 1/4 inch, horizontal (indicating lateral pressure), diagonal with stair-step pattern, actively widening over time, or accompanied by other structural symptoms like sticking doors or sloping floors. Hairline vertical cracks are usually cosmetic. When uncertain, professional assessment provides definitive evaluation.

### How much does foundation crack assessment cost in Carlsbad?

Foundation crack assessment in Carlsbad typically costs $600-$1,200 for a comprehensive evaluation with written report. This includes on-site inspection, crack documentation, severity assessment, cause analysis, and repair recommendations. Initial phone consultations are free.

### What causes diagonal cracks in Carlsbad foundations?

Diagonal foundation cracks in Carlsbad are typically caused by differential settlement—when one part of the foundation settles more than another. Common causes include expansive clay soils (common inland), variable soil bearing capacity, fill consolidation, drainage problems, and tree root effects. Professional assessment determines specific cause.

### Should I be worried about foundation cracks before selling my Carlsbad home?

Address foundation cracks before listing by having them professionally assessed. Minor cracks may need only documentation showing they're cosmetic. Significant cracks may need repair before sale or will affect negotiations. Proactive assessment and repair typically preserves value better than reactive disclosure during transaction.

### Can foundation cracks be repaired, or do I need a new foundation?

Most foundation cracks can be repaired without full replacement. Repair options include crack injection ($150-$400 per crack), carbon fiber reinforcement ($400-$700/ft), and underpinning for settlement ($1,200-$3,000 per pier). Full foundation replacement ($15,000-$50,000+) is rarely necessary and only for severe structural failure.
