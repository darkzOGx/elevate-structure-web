## What Is Stem Wall Foundation Engineering?

**Direct Answer:** Stem wall foundation engineering involves designing raised perimeter foundation walls supporting wood-framed floor systems with crawl space below. In California, this requires PE-stamped design addressing continuous footings, stem wall reinforcement, anchor bolt placement, and seismic hold-down requirements per CBC standards.

Stem wall foundations are ideal for:
- **Matching existing homes** with raised foundations
- **Sloped sites** requiring stepped footings
- **Utility access** for plumbing and HVAC
- **Flood-prone areas** elevating finished floor
- **Room additions** connecting to existing structures

## Frequently Asked Questions

### When are stem wall foundations used instead of slab foundations?
Stem walls are preferred when matching existing raised foundations, on sloped sites, when crawl space utility access is needed, or in flood zones requiring elevated floors.

### How tall are typical stem wall foundations?
Stem walls typically range from 18-36 inches above grade for standard residential applications. Taller walls may be required for sloped sites or flood elevation requirements.

### What is foundation bolting for stem wall foundations?
Foundation bolting connects the wood mudsill to the concrete stem wall using anchor bolts, providing essential seismic resistance. Many older homes lack adequate bolting.

### Do older homes need stem wall seismic retrofits?
Many homes built before 1980 benefit from seismic retrofits including foundation bolting, cripple wall bracing, and improved connections between the house and foundation.

### Can slab additions connect to stem wall foundations?
Yes, additions can use either slab-on-grade or raised foundations when connecting to existing stem wall foundations. Proper connection and drainage details are essential.

### What is a cripple wall in stem wall foundations?
Cripple walls are short wood-framed walls between the foundation and first floor in raised foundations. They often require seismic bracing to prevent collapse during earthquakes.
