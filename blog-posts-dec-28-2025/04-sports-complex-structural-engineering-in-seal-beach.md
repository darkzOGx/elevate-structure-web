## Frequently Asked Questions

### What structural system is best for gymnasiums?
Steel truss or steel joist systems are most common for gymnasium roofs due to their ability to span long distances without intermediate columns. Pre-engineered metal buildings work for smaller facilities.

### How long does sports complex structural engineering take?
Small fitness centers take 3-4 weeks. Large multi-court complexes and arenas require 6-10 weeks depending on complexity.

### What seismic requirements apply to sports facilities in Seal Beach?
Seal Beach is in Seismic Design Category D. Sports facilities with assembly occupancies require enhanced seismic design, including special detailing for long-span roof connections and bleacher anchorage.

### What floor loads are required for fitness centers?
Fitness center floors typically require 100-150 psf for general exercise areas and 150-200 psf for free weight areas. Specialized equipment like power racks may require concentrated load analysis.

### Can existing buildings be converted to sports facilities?
Yes, many warehouse and industrial buildings can be converted to indoor sports use. Key considerations include clear height, column spacing, and floor slab capacity.

### What are the structural requirements for indoor pools?
Indoor pool structures must address high-humidity corrosion protection, pool deck loads, diving platform supports, and mechanical equipment for filtration and HVAC systems.
