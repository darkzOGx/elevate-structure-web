## Frequently Asked Questions

### What's the difference between a structural inspection and a home inspection?
Structural inspections are conducted by licensed engineers focusing specifically on structural systems. Home inspections are general assessments that may not identify all structural issues.

### When should I get a structural inspection?
Consider inspection before buying a home, after earthquake events, when planning renovations, if you notice cracks or settling, or for older properties.

### How long does a structural inspection take?
Most residential inspections take 2-4 hours on-site. Larger or complex properties may require additional time.

### Will I receive a written report?
Yes, you'll receive a comprehensive written report with photos, findings, and recommendations within 3-5 business days.

### Can structural inspectors identify foundation problems?
Yes, licensed structural engineers can identify foundation issues including settlement, cracking, drainage problems, and deterioration.

### Are structural inspections required for home purchases?
While not legally required, structural inspections are highly recommended, especially for older homes or properties in seismic zones.
