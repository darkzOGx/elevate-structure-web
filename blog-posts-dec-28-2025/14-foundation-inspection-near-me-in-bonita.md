## Warning Signs That Indicate Need for Foundation Inspection

**Direct Answer:** Schedule a foundation inspection if you notice cracks in walls or floors, doors/windows that stick, uneven floors, gaps at wall/ceiling junctions, or exterior foundation cracks. Early inspection prevents minor issues from becoming major problems.

**Warning Signs:**
- Cracks in interior walls or ceilings
- Exterior foundation cracks
- Doors or windows that stick
- Uneven or sloping floors
- Gaps between walls and ceiling/floor
- Cracks in floor tiles or concrete

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How often should foundation inspections be done?
Most homes benefit from foundation inspection every 5-10 years, or immediately if warning signs appear. Pre-purchase inspection is always recommended.

### What causes foundation problems in Bonita?
Common causes include expansive soils, poor drainage, inadequate compaction, tree root intrusion, plumbing leaks, and seismic movement.

### Can foundation cracks be repaired?
Most foundation cracks can be repaired. The method depends on crack type, cause, and severity. Our inspection report includes repair recommendations.

### How long does a foundation inspection take?
Most residential foundation inspections take 1-2 hours. Complex properties or comprehensive evaluations may require additional time.

### Will the inspector go under the house?
If accessible, our inspectors evaluate crawl space areas to assess stem walls, piers, and subfloor conditions.

### What should I do if foundation problems are found?
Our inspection report includes prioritized recommendations and cost estimates. We can also provide engineering for any necessary repairs.
