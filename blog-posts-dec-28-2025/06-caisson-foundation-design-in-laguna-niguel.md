## What Is Caisson Foundation Design?

**Direct Answer:** Caisson foundation design involves engineering drilled, cast-in-place concrete piers that transfer structural loads through weak or unstable surface soils to competent bearing material at depth. In California, this requires PE-stamped geotechnical coordination, structural calculations, and construction specifications meeting CBC seismic requirements.

Caisson foundations are ideal for:
- **Hillside construction** where shallow foundations aren't feasible
- **Expansive soils** that cause foundation movement
- **Poor bearing soils** at shallow depths
- **High load concentrations** from multi-story structures
- **Seismic zones** requiring enhanced lateral resistance

## Frequently Asked Questions

### When are caisson foundations needed?
Caissons are typically required for hillside sites, expansive soils, poor bearing conditions at shallow depths, high structural loads, or when shallow foundations would be impractical.

### How deep are caisson foundations?
Caisson depths vary based on soil conditions. Typical residential caissons range from 10-30 feet, though some hillside applications may exceed 40 feet to reach stable bearing material.

### What diameter caissons are used for homes?
Residential caissons typically range from 18-36 inches in diameter. Larger diameters are used for higher loads or where lateral resistance is critical.

### Do I need a geotechnical report for caisson foundations?
Yes, geotechnical investigation is typically required for caisson foundations to determine bearing capacity, recommended depths, and design parameters for the specific site.

### Can caissons be used to repair settling foundations?
Yes, new caissons or helical piers can be installed to underpin and stabilize existing foundations experiencing settlement. This provides permanent support without replacing the entire foundation.

### What seismic requirements apply to caissons in Laguna Niguel?
Laguna Niguel is in Seismic Design Category D, requiring caisson reinforcement and connections designed for seismic lateral loads and moment resistance.
