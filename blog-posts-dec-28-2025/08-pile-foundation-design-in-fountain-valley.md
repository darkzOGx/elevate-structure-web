## What Is Pile Foundation Design?

**Direct Answer:** Pile foundation design involves engineering deep foundation elements that transfer structural loads through weak soils to competent bearing material at depth, either through end bearing, friction, or a combination. In California, this requires PE-stamped analysis including pile capacity calculations, group effects, and seismic design per CBC requirements.

Pile foundations include several types:
- **Driven piles** (steel, concrete, or timber)
- **Auger cast piles** (continuous flight auger)
- **Drilled shafts** (large diameter cast-in-place)
- **Micropiles** (small diameter, high capacity)
- **Helical piles** (screw-in foundations)

## Frequently Asked Questions

### When are pile foundations required?
Piles are typically required when surface soils have inadequate bearing capacity, loads are too heavy for shallow foundations, or when uplift or lateral loads are significant.

### What type of pile is best for residential projects?
Helical piles are often ideal for residential applications due to minimal site disturbance, no vibration, and quick installation. Auger cast piles work well for moderate loads.

### How deep do piles need to be installed?
Pile depths vary based on soil conditions. Typical residential helical piles may be 10-25 feet, while driven piles for commercial projects can exceed 50-100 feet.

### Is a geotechnical report required for pile foundations?
Yes, geotechnical investigation is essential to determine pile type selection, required depths, capacity parameters, and installation specifications.

### Can piles be used to repair settling foundations?
Yes, helical piles and push piers are commonly used to underpin and stabilize existing foundations experiencing settlement.

### What are pile group effects?
When piles are closely spaced, their load-carrying capacity may be reduced due to overlapping stress zones in the soil. Design must account for group efficiency factors.
