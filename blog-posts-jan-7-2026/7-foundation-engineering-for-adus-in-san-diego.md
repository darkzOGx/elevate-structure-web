## What Is Foundation Engineering for ADUs in San Diego?

**Direct Answer:** Foundation engineering for ADUs encompasses the structural design of foundation systems supporting accessory dwelling units, including spread footings, continuous footings, slab-on-grade construction, and specialized foundations for garage conversions. In San Diego, this includes seismic design per California Building Code requirements, coordination with soil conditions, and compliance with setback requirements affecting foundation placement.

ADU foundation engineering differs from typical residential construction because ADUs are usually built on portions of existing properties with constraints including proximity to existing structures, utility locations, and setback limitations. Foundation design must work within these constraints while providing adequate support for the new structure.

## What Types of ADU Foundations Are Used in San Diego?

**Direct Answer:** San Diego ADU foundations typically utilize conventional spread footings with stem walls, slab-on-grade construction with thickened edges, or specialized foundations for garage conversions. Foundation selection depends on ADU type, soil conditions, topography, and construction efficiency considerations. Most ADUs require foundations designed by a California-licensed Professional Engineer.

**Conventional Spread Footing Foundations**
Traditional spread footing and stem wall foundations remain the standard for many San Diego ADUs, particularly detached units on level sites. These foundations consist of continuous concrete footings supporting concrete or masonry stem walls, with a concrete floor slab poured within the stem wall perimeter.

Spread footing foundations offer several advantages for ADU construction:
- Separation of floor from ground with crawl space below
- Flexibility for sloped sites through stepped construction
- Easy access to utilities within crawl space
- Proven performance in San Diego soil conditions

Our engineers design spread footing foundations sized for specific soil bearing capacities and seismic requirements. Footing widths, depths, and reinforcement are specified based on loading conditions and geotechnical parameters.

**Slab-on-Grade Foundations**
Slab-on-grade construction combines the floor slab and foundation into a single pour, with thickened edges replacing separate footings and stem walls. This approach is cost-effective for level sites with stable soils and offers construction efficiency advantages.

Post-tensioned slabs—reinforced with high-strength steel tendons tensioned after concrete cures—are common in San Diego, particularly in areas with expansive soils. Post-tensioning creates compressive forces counteracting soil swelling, reducing cracking and distress.

**Garage Conversion Foundations**
Converting existing garages to ADUs presents unique foundation considerations. Existing garage slabs were typically designed for vehicle loads, not residential occupancy. Our engineers evaluate existing conditions and determine:

- Whether existing slab can support residential loads
- Need for new perimeter foundations
- Insulation and moisture barrier requirements
- Connection details to new walls

Many garage conversions require new perimeter foundations to support new exterior walls, even when existing slabs are adequate for floor loads.

**Hillside and Stepped Foundations**
San Diego's numerous hillside communities—La Jolla, Del Cerro, Scripps Ranch, and others—often require stepped or split-level foundations for ADUs built on sloped terrain. Our engineers design foundations that work with site topography, minimizing grading while providing stable support.

## What Are the Key Foundation Considerations for San Diego ADUs?

**Direct Answer:** Key foundation considerations for San Diego ADUs include expansive soil mitigation, seismic design requirements, setback compliance affecting foundation placement, drainage and moisture protection, and coordination with existing site improvements. Many ADU sites present constraints requiring creative foundation solutions.

**Expansive Soil Considerations**

Expansive clay soils are prevalent throughout inland San Diego County, presenting significant challenges for foundation design. These soils change volume with moisture content, potentially causing foundation movement and structural damage. Mitigation strategies include:

- Post-tensioned slab foundations resisting soil movement
- Deepened footings extending below the active zone
- Void forms allowing soil expansion without lifting foundations
- Pre-saturation and moisture control
- Proper surface drainage away from foundations

Our engineers evaluate site-specific soil conditions and specify appropriate expansive soil mitigation for each ADU project.

**Seismic Design Requirements**

San Diego falls within Seismic Design Category D, requiring seismic considerations in all foundation design:

- Foundation ties interconnecting foundation elements
- Anchorage of superstructure to foundations
- Soil-structure interaction effects
- Verification of soil stability under seismic loading

ADU foundations must be detailed for seismic forces even though ADUs are relatively small structures. Our engineers incorporate seismic requirements efficiently.

**Setback and Placement Constraints**

State law allows ADUs with reduced setbacks—typically 4 feet from side and rear property lines—but foundation systems must work within these constraints. Shallow setbacks may limit foundation options, particularly on sloped sites where retaining structures would encroach on setback areas.

Our engineers design foundations respecting setback requirements while providing adequate structural support. When constraints are severe, we evaluate alternatives such as pier foundations, grade beam systems, or modified framing approaches.

**Drainage and Moisture Protection**

Foundation success depends on effective site drainage. Water accumulation around foundations causes soil expansion, erosion, and potential structural damage. Our foundation designs include:

- Positive drainage away from foundations
- Foundation waterproofing specifications
- Subdrain systems when required
- Vapor barrier requirements

**Do I need a structural engineer for an ADU in San Diego?**
California law requires PE-stamped structural plans for most ADU construction, including foundation design. Some prefabricated ADUs with pre-approved plans may include foundation designs, but site-specific conditions often require custom engineering.

Optimal foundation type depends on site conditions. Slab-on-grade with post-tensioning works well for level sites with expansive soils. Spread footings with stem walls suit sloped sites or areas with shallow bedrock. Our engineers recommend appropriate systems based on specific site evaluation.

**Can existing garage slabs be used for ADU conversions?**
Existing garage slabs may be adequate for floor loads but typically require new perimeter foundations to support residential walls. Our engineers evaluate existing conditions and determine the most cost-effective approach for each garage conversion.

**Do hillside ADUs require special foundations?**
