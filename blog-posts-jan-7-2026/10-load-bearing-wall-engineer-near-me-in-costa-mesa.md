## How Do Engineers Determine If a Wall Is Load Bearing?

**Direct Answer:** Engineers determine load bearing status by examining floor and roof framing orientation relative to the wall, checking whether the wall sits above foundations or beams below, and analyzing the overall structural system. Walls running perpendicular to floor or roof joists and aligned with foundations are typically load bearing.

**Indicators of Load Bearing Walls:**
- Wall runs perpendicular to floor/roof joists above
- Wall sits directly above foundation or beam below
- Wall is located in center of building
- Wall has doubled top plates
- Wall is typically thicker (2x6 vs 2x4)

**Professional Analysis Required:**
While these indicators suggest load bearing status, definitive determination requires professional analysis. Some walls that appear load bearing are not, and some that appear non-structural actually carry loads. Our engineers examine your specific construction and provide definitive answers.
