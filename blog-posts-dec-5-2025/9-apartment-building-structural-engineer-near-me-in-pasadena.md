## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is my Pasadena apartment building subject to seismic retrofit requirements?

Many Pasadena apartment buildings are subject to retrofit requirements, particularly pre-1978 soft-story wood frame buildings and unreinforced masonry structures. Contact Pasadena Building & Safety to confirm your building's status, or we can assist with preliminary determination during a consultation.

### How long does a typical apartment retrofit take in Pasadena?

Engineering design typically takes 8-12 weeks. City of Pasadena plan check adds 3-5 weeks. Construction duration depends on building size and retrofit type, ranging from 6-12 weeks for typical soft-story retrofits. Historic properties may require additional review time.

### Can tenants remain during retrofit construction?

Most soft-story retrofits allow tenants to remain with minimal disruption. Work occurs primarily in parking and common areas. Some units may require brief access for connection work. Major URM retrofits may require temporary relocation for some units. We design with tenant impact minimization in mind.

### Do I need historic commission approval for structural work?

Designated historic properties and properties in historic districts require Certificate of Appropriateness for work affecting exterior appearance. Interior-only structural work typically doesn't require historic approval. We coordinate with Pasadena Historic Preservation when required.
