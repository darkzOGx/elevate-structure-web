## Frequently Asked Questions

### How deep do pile foundations go in Coronado?

Pile depth depends on soil conditions determined by geotechnical investigation. Coronado piles typically range from 30-60+ feet to reach competent bearing strata below the loose surface sands. Some locations may require deeper installations based on site-specific conditions.

### What is a VE flood zone and how does it affect my foundation?

VE zones are coastal flood zones where wave action is anticipated during flooding. Structures must be elevated above the Base Flood Elevation (BFE) on open pile foundations. Breakaway walls are required below BFE. Enclosed space below BFE is prohibited. These requirements significantly impact foundation and structural design.

### Can I add to my existing Coronado home's foundation?

Yes, with proper engineering. Additions typically require new pile foundations coordinated with existing construction. We design integration systems that connect new and existing foundations while maintaining structural integrity of both.

### How do you protect foundations from saltwater corrosion?

Marine foundation protection includes increased concrete cover over reinforcement, corrosion-inhibiting concrete admixtures, epoxy-coated or stainless reinforcement in critical areas, and proper drainage to minimize saltwater contact. Design specifications are based on exposure conditions.
