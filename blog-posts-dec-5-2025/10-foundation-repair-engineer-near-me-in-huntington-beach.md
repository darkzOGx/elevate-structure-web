## Frequently Asked Questions

### How do I know if my foundation needs repair?

Common signs include cracks in walls (especially diagonal cracks from corners of windows/doors), doors and windows sticking, uneven or sloping floors, gaps between walls and ceiling, and visible cracks in the foundation itself. If you notice these signs, a professional assessment can determine if repair is needed.

### Can foundation problems affect my home's value?

Yes, unrepaired foundation problems significantly impact home value and can complicate sale transactions. Many buyers walk away from homes with foundation issues. However, properly engineered and documented repairs can restore value and provide buyer confidence.

### Do all foundation repairs require permits?

Not all repairs require permits. Cosmetic crack repairs and minor leveling may not trigger permit requirements. Structural underpinning with piers typically requires permits and engineering. We advise on permit requirements for your specific situation.

### How long do foundation repairs last?

Properly designed and installed foundation repairs are permanent solutions. Steel pier systems carry 75+ year warranties from manufacturers. The key is correct diagnosis of the underlying cause and appropriate repair selection‚Äîwhich is why engineering evaluation is important.

### Should I get a foundation inspection before buying a home?

Absolutely. Standard home inspections often miss foundation issues that a structural engineer would identify. A pre-purchase foundation assessment ($1,500-$2,500) can save tens of thousands in unexpected repairs and provides negotiating leverage.
