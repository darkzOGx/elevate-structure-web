## What Is Home Renovation Structural Engineering?

**Direct Answer:**
Home renovation structural engineering encompasses the professional analysis, design, and PE-stamped documentation required when modifying a home's structural elements during remodeling. Licensed Professional Engineers evaluate existing conditions, design new structural systems, and prepare permit-ready plans.

Renovation engineering differs from new construction because existing structures must be analyzed and integrated into new designs. Engineers must understand original construction methods, current conditions, and how proposed changes affect the entire structural system.

## What Types of Renovations Require Structural Engineering in California?

**Direct Answer:**
California requires structural engineering for renovations that modify load-bearing elements, remove walls, change roof structures, add floors, or significantly alter a building's structural configuration.

**Load-Bearing Wall Removal**
Removing walls that support floor or roof loads requires engineered beams, posts, and connection details.

**Open Floor Plan Conversions**
Creating open-concept living spaces by combining rooms often involves removing multiple walls.

**Second-Story Additions**
Adding a second story significantly increases loads on existing foundations and first-floor framing.
