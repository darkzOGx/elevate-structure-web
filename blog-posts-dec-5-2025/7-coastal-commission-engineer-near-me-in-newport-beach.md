## Frequently Asked Questions

### Does every Newport Beach coastal project need CCC approval?

Not all projects require direct CCC approval. The City of Newport Beach has a certified Local Coastal Program (LCP) that allows City-level CDP approval for many projects. However, projects on bluffs, within 50 feet of water, involving seawalls, or meeting certain size thresholds may require CCC review. We help determine your permit pathway.

### How long does Coastal Commission approval take?

CCC approval timelines range from 2-4 months for straightforward projects handled at CCC district level to 8-12+ months for complex projects requiring Commission hearing. City-level CDP approval is faster (60-90 days typical). Early CCC staff consultation can significantly reduce timeline.

### Can I appeal a CCC decision?

Yes, CCC decisions can be appealed, but grounds are limited. Applicants can request reconsideration based on new information or changed circumstances. Third parties can appeal City CDP approvals to the CCC. Having strong technical documentation from the start reduces appeal risk.

### What if my project is in the appeal zone?

Projects in the CCC appeal zone receive City CDP approval, but third parties have 10 working days to appeal to CCC. If appealed, CCC conducts de novo review. Engineers experienced with CCC policies can design projects that minimize appeal risk while meeting client objectives.
