## What Types of Room Additions Require Structural Engineering in California?

**Direct Answer:**
California requires structural engineering for room additions that modify load-bearing elements, add significant floor area, involve foundation work, or change the building's structural configuration. This includes most additions over 200 square feet, second-story additions, and any project requiring new foundation systems.

**Single-Story Room Additions**
Ground-level additions in Bonita typically require new foundation design, connection to existing framing, and roof structure engineering. Even seemingly simple additions need proper load path analysis to ensure forces transfer correctly through the structure.

**Second-Story Additions**
Adding a second story significantly increases loads on existing foundations and first-floor framing. Structural engineers evaluate whether existing elements can support additional weight or if reinforcement is needed. Many Bonita homes built in the 1970s-1990s require foundation upgrades for second-story additions.

**Kitchen Extensions**
Expanding kitchens often involves removing load-bearing walls and creating open floor plans. Structural engineering provides beam sizing, post locations, and connection details that maintain structural integrity while achieving desired layouts.

**Master Suite Additions**
Large master suite additions combine multiple structural challenges including foundation design, wall framing, roof integration, and often bathroom wet wall support. Professional engineering coordinates all these elements.
