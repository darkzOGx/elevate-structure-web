## What Types of Earthquake Damage Occur in Pasadena Buildings?

**Direct Answer:** Earthquake damage in Pasadena buildings includes foundation cracking and displacement, cripple wall failure in older homes, soft-story collapse in multi-family buildings, chimney damage and collapse, unreinforced masonry failure in historic buildings, shear cracking in walls, connection failures at framing joints, and damage to non-structural elements like chimneys and parapets. The type of damage depends on building age, construction type, and earthquake characteristics.

**Common Damage in Historic Pasadena Homes**

Pasadena's Craftsman and historic homes face specific vulnerabilities:

**Cripple Wall Failure:**
- Short wood-frame walls between foundation and first floor
- Lack of adequate bracing
- Can cause homes to slide off foundations
- Common in pre-1950 homes without retrofit

**Foundation Connection Issues:**
- Unbolted sill plates
- House not secured to foundation
- Can cause sliding in moderate earthquakes
- Retrofittable with anchor bolts

**Chimney Damage:**
- Unreinforced masonry chimneys
- Common in homes of all ages
- Can collapse onto roof or ground
- Particularly dangerous during shaking

**Commercial and Multi-Family Damage**

**Soft-Story Damage:**
- Buildings with parking or retail at ground level
- Weak ground floors can pancake
- Common in 1960s-1980s apartment buildings
- Pasadena has mandatory retrofit program

**Unreinforced Masonry:**
- Historic commercial buildings
- Brick and stone without steel reinforcement
- High collapse risk in earthquakes
- Many in Old Town Pasadena retrofitted

**Tilt-Up Damage:**
- Industrial and commercial buildings
- Wall-to-roof connection failures
- Can result in wall collapse
- Common issue in larger earthquakes

### How do I know if cracks are from the earthquake or were pre-existing?
