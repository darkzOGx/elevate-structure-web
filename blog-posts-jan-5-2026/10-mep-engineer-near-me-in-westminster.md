## What Is MEP Engineering?

MEP engineering encompasses the design of mechanical, electrical, and plumbing systems that serve buildings—the infrastructure that provides heating, cooling, ventilation, power distribution, lighting, water supply, and drainage. In Westminster and throughout Orange County, MEP engineers ensure that building systems function efficiently, meet code requirements, and provide comfortable, safe environments for occupants.

The mechanical component addresses HVAC systems—heating, ventilation, and air conditioning that maintain indoor comfort. California's climate creates particular HVAC demands, with cooling loads dominating energy consumption in Westminster's warm summers. Mechanical design optimizes system selection and sizing for efficient operation.

Electrical engineering encompasses power distribution, lighting design, and increasingly, renewable energy systems and electric vehicle charging. California's progressive energy codes require sophisticated electrical design that balances functionality against energy efficiency requirements.

Plumbing engineering addresses domestic water supply, sanitary drainage, and gas piping. Water conservation requirements in California have made plumbing design increasingly important for code compliance and resource efficiency.

## What Types of MEP Engineering Services Are Available?

MEP engineering encompasses diverse services addressing building systems throughout project lifecycles.

New buildings require complete MEP design coordinated with architectural and structural systems. This includes HVAC system selection and design, electrical service sizing and distribution, plumbing system layout, and all associated calculations and documentation for permit.

Westminster's new construction includes residential developments, commercial buildings, and mixed-use projects. Each building type presents distinct MEP requirements. Our designs optimize systems for each application while ensuring code compliance.

**Title 24 Energy Compliance**

California's Title 24 Building Energy Efficiency Standards apply to all new construction and most renovations. Compliance requires energy modeling demonstrating that proposed designs meet or exceed efficiency requirements. MEP engineers prepare compliance documentation and design systems that satisfy Title 24.

The 2025 Title 24 standards include enhanced requirements for electrification, solar readiness, and energy efficiency. Our current expertise ensures projects meet latest code requirements.

**HVAC Design and Load Calculations**

Heating and cooling system design requires accurate load calculations based on building characteristics, occupancy, and local climate data. Properly sized systems operate efficiently and maintain comfort; oversized systems waste energy and undersized systems cannot maintain desired conditions.

We perform Manual J load calculations for residential projects and comprehensive commercial load analysis, providing the engineering basis for HVAC system selection and duct design.

**Electrical Design and Panel Schedules**

Electrical design encompasses service sizing, panel layout, circuit design, and specification of electrical equipment. Residential projects require electrical plans for permit; commercial projects need comprehensive electrical engineering including power distribution, lighting design, and specialty systems.

California's requirements for electric vehicle charging readiness and solar photovoltaic systems add complexity to residential electrical design. Our designs address these requirements while providing for practical electrical functionality.

**Plumbing Design and Fixture Calculations**

Plumbing design addresses water supply sizing, drainage system layout, fixture selection, and gas piping where applicable. California's water efficiency requirements mandate low-flow fixtures and efficient system design.

### What does MEP stand for in engineering?

MEP stands for Mechanical, Electrical, and Plumbing—the three major building system disciplines that MEP engineers design. Mechanical includes HVAC (heating, ventilation, and air conditioning). Electrical includes power distribution, lighting, and specialty electrical systems. Plumbing includes domestic water, sanitary drainage, and gas piping. Together, these systems provide the infrastructure that makes buildings functional.

### Do I need an MEP engineer for residential construction in California?

California requires MEP engineering for most residential construction to demonstrate Title 24 energy compliance. New homes, additions, and major renovations need Title 24 compliance documentation, which typically requires engineering involvement. Additionally, projects with complex HVAC systems, significant electrical work, or unusual plumbing requirements benefit from MEP engineering to ensure proper system design.

### What is Title 24 and why does it matter?

Title 24 is California's Building Energy Efficiency Standards—the state's energy code that applies to all new construction and most renovations. Title 24 establishes minimum efficiency requirements for building envelope, HVAC systems, lighting, and water heating. Compliance documentation must be submitted with building permits, and inspection verification is required during construction. Meeting Title 24 requirements is mandatory for project approval.

### How long does MEP engineering take?

MEP engineering timelines vary with project complexity. Residential projects typically require 2-4 weeks for design plus 2-4 weeks for permit approval. Commercial projects require 4-8 weeks for design plus 4-6 weeks for permit review. Complex projects or those requiring extensive Title 24 modeling may take longer. ### Can you help with an existing building that needs HVAC replacement?

Yes, HVAC replacement projects often require engineering involvement. Even equipment-for-equipment replacements may need Title 24 compliance documentation for permit.
