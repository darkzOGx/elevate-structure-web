## What Are Common Structural Issues Found in Fullerton?

**Direct Answer:**
Common structural issues in Fullerton homes include foundation cracks from soil movement and seismic activity, wood rot and pest damage in older homes, inadequate seismic bracing in pre-1980 construction, settlement in hillside areas, and modifications made without proper engineering. Each issue has solutions, and early detection prevents costly problems.

### Foundation Cracks

**What We Find:**
Hairline cracks are common and often cosmetic. Wider cracks (>1/8"), stair-step patterns in masonry, or horizontal cracks may indicate structural movement requiring evaluation.

**Fullerton Context:**
Fullerton's varied soil conditions‚Äîfrom stable alluvial soils to expansive clays in some areas‚Äîinfluence foundation behavior. Local knowledge helps distinguish normal settling from concerning movement.

### Seismic Deficiencies

**What We Find:**
Homes built before 1980 often lack modern seismic connections‚Äîfoundation bolting, cripple wall bracing, and adequate wall-to-roof connections.

**Why It Matters:**
Fullerton is in Seismic Zone D. Older homes without proper seismic connections are vulnerable to earthquake damage. Inspection identifies deficiencies; retrofitting addresses them.

### Wood Deterioration

**What We Find:**
Water intrusion causes wood rot. Termites and other pests cause structural damage. Both are common in older Fullerton homes, particularly where moisture management is poor.

### Unpermitted Modifications

**What We Find:**
Room additions, converted garages, removed walls, and other modifications done without permits may have structural deficiencies. Our inspection identifies such issues regardless of permit status.
