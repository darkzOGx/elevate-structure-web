## What Is Foundation Crack Engineering?

Foundation crack engineering involves professional assessment of cracks in concrete foundations to determine:

- **Crack cause** - Settlement, shrinkage, structural stress, or other factors
- **Structural significance** - Cosmetic vs. structural concern
- **Activity status** - Active (growing) vs. dormant
- **Repair requirements** - If repairs are needed and what type
- **Monitoring needs** - Whether ongoing observation is appropriate

## What Types of Foundation Cracks Occur in Coronado?

### Shrinkage Cracks
Normal concrete behavior:

**Characteristics:**
- Hairline width (less than 1/16 inch)
- Random pattern or at corners/edges
- Occurred shortly after construction
- No displacement or offset
- Static (not growing)

**Significance:**
- Generally cosmetic
- May allow moisture intrusion
- Sealing typically sufficient
- Not structurally concerning

### Settlement Cracks
Foundation movement indicators:

**Characteristics:**
- Vertical or diagonal orientation
- May show offset (one side higher)
- Often accompanied by floor slope
- May be wider at top or bottom
- Can be active or dormant

**Significance:**
- Indicates foundation movement
- May require underpinning
- Cause investigation essential
- Structural evaluation needed

### Structural Cracks
Load-related damage:

**Characteristics:**
- Wider cracks (1/8 inch or more)
- Located at points of load concentration
- May show crushing or spalling
- Often accompanied by other distress
- May indicate inadequate design

**Significance:**
- Requires immediate evaluation
- May need structural reinforcement
- Often involves repair design
- Building permits typically required

### Corrosion Cracks
Reinforcement-related damage:

**Characteristics:**
- Rust staining visible
- Cracks follow rebar pattern
- Concrete delamination
- Common in marine environments
- Progressive without intervention

**Significance:**
- Indicates rebar corrosion
- Common in Coronado's salt air
- Requires corrosion mitigation
- Can be structurally significant

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How do I know if a foundation crack is serious?
Cracks wider than 1/8 inch, showing vertical offset (one side higher than other), or accompanied by other distress (sticking doors, floor slopes, wall cracks) warrant professional evaluation. Hairline cracks without offset are typically less concerning but can still benefit from assessment.

### Should I monitor cracks before calling an engineer?
Basic monitoring is helpful—photograph cracks with a ruler for scale and note any changes. However, professional monitoring with proper devices provides more accurate data. If you're concerned, don't wait—early evaluation is better than delayed assessment.

### Can foundation cracks be repaired without underpinning?
Many foundation cracks can be repaired with less invasive methods: epoxy injection for structural cracks, polyurethane sealing for water intrusion, or carbon fiber reinforcement for added strength. Underpinning is typically reserved for active settlement situations.

### Does home insurance cover foundation crack repairs?
Standard homeowner's insurance typically doesn't cover foundation settlement or normal wear. However, sudden damage from covered events (like earthquake if you have that coverage) may be covered. Check your specific policy and consult your agent.

### How long does foundation crack assessment take?
A typical inspection takes 1-2 hours on site. Written reports are usually delivered within 1 week. If monitoring is recommended, the monitoring period typically runs 3-6 months to capture seasonal variations.

### Will I need a permit for foundation crack repairs?
Minor crack sealing typically doesn't require permits. Structural repairs—epoxy injection, carbon fiber reinforcement, underpinning—usually require Coronado Building Department permits. We coordinate permit requirements as part of our repair design services.

### Can I sell a home with foundation cracks?
Yes, but proper documentation is important. A professional assessment helps you understand what you're disclosing to buyers and may provide assurance that cracks are cosmetic or have been properly addressed. Some buyers require foundation evaluation before purchase.

### What causes cracks to appear in newer homes?
Even newer homes can develop cracks from concrete shrinkage (normal), early settlement as soils compress, plumbing leaks affecting soil, or construction defects. Age alone doesn't determine crack significance—the cause and characteristics matter more.
