### Seismic Risk Factors

Key risk factors include proximity to active faults, soil conditions that can amplify ground motion, building age with many warehouses built before modern seismic codes, and common tilt-up and steel construction with specific vulnerabilities.

## Common Seismic Deficiencies

### Tilt-Up Concrete Buildings

The most common warehouse construction type often has inadequate roof-to-wall connections, insufficient wall anchors, weak diaphragm connections, and pilaster deficiencies.

### Steel Frame Buildings

Older steel warehouses frequently have undersized bracing, connection issues, foundation anchoring problems, and roof diaphragm weaknesses.
