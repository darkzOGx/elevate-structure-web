## Common Slab Foundation Problems in Corona

### Settlement and Differential Movement

Foundation settlement occurs when supporting soil consolidates or erodes:

**Causes:**
- Poor original compaction
- Soil erosion from water infiltration
- Tree root decay leaving voids
- Underground utility failures

**Symptoms:**
- Sloping floors
- Doors and windows sticking
- Cracks following floor plane
- Separation at walls and ceilings

### Expansive Soil Damage

Corona's expansive soils create distinctive damage patterns:

**Heave Damage:**
- Center of slab pushed upward
- Perimeter settling relative to center
- Diagonal cracks radiating from center
- Interior partition damage

**Shrinkage Damage:**
- Perimeter dropping as soil shrinks
- Edge cracks parallel to foundation
- Gaps at slab/wall connections
- Exterior separation visible

### Structural Cracking

Foundation cracks require professional evaluation:

| Crack Type | Width | Significance | Action Required |
|------------|-------|--------------|-----------------|
| Hairline | <1/16" | Shrinkage/curing | Monitor |
| Narrow | 1/16"-1/8" | Minor movement | Evaluate |
| Medium | 1/8"-1/4" | Active movement | Engineer assessment |
| Wide | >1/4" | Structural concern | Immediate evaluation |

## Slab Foundation Repair Methods

### Epoxy Injection

For structural crack repair:

**Application:**
- Cracks 0.002" to 0.5" width
- Structural bonding restoration
- Prevents water infiltration
- Maintains structural integrity

**Cost Range:** $300-$1,000 per crack

### Polyurethane Foam Injection

For void filling and lift:

**Application:**
- Minor settlement correction
- Void filling beneath slab
- Quick set time (minutes)
- Minimal disruption

**Cost Range:** $5-$15 per square foot

### Mudjacking (Slabjacking)

Traditional void filling method:

**Application:**
- Moderate settlement correction
- Large area treatment
- Cost-effective solution
- Proven long-term performance

**Cost Range:** $3-$8 per square foot

### Helical Piers

For significant settlement:

**Application:**
- Deep foundation support
- Bypasses problem soils
- Lift capability
- Permanent solution

**Cost Range:** $1,500-$3,000 per pier

### Push Piers

For heavy structure support:

**Application:**
- Uses building weight for installation
- Reaches deep stable soil
- Maximum support capacity
- Minimal access requirements

**Cost Range:** $1,200-$2,500 per pier

### Slab Replacement

When repair isn't feasible:

**Application:**
- Extensive damage throughout
- Post-tension slab failures
- Major re-leveling needed
- Code compliance requirements

**Cost Range:** $15-$30 per square foot

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How do I know if my foundation cracks are serious?

Crack seriousness depends on width, pattern, and progression. Cracks wider than 1/8 inch, diagonal patterns, or those showing active movement warrant professional evaluation. Our engineers can distinguish between cosmetic cracking and structural concerns requiring repair.

### Can foundation problems be fixed permanently?

Yes, when repairs address root causes. This may require drainage improvements, soil treatment, or deep foundation support in addition to direct repair. Our designs create permanent solutions rather than temporary fixes that allow problems to return.

### How long do foundation repairs take?

Repair timelines vary by method. Epoxy injection takes 1-2 days. Foam injection requires 1-3 days. Pier installation typically takes 3-7 days depending on the number of piers. Slab replacement requires 2-4 weeks. We provide accurate timelines in our repair specifications.

### Will foundation repair solve my door and window problems?

If misalignment results from foundation movement, proper repair should restore function. However, severe or long-standing movement may have permanently distorted framing requiring additional carpentry work. Our assessment identifies all affected elements.

### Do I need engineering for foundation repair in Corona?

Significant repairs require PE-stamped designs for permit approval. Even without permit requirements, engineering documentation protects your investment by ensuring appropriate repair methods and providing future buyers with professional assessment.
