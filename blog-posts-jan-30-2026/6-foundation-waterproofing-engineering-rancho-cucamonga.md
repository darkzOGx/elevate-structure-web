## Types of Foundation Waterproofing Systems

### Positive-Side Waterproofing

Applied to the exterior (water-facing) side of foundation walls:

**Advantages:**
- Water never contacts structural concrete
- Easiest installation access during construction
- Most effective long-term protection
- Industry standard for new construction

**System Types:**
- Sheet membranes (40-60 mil)
- Fluid-applied membranes
- Bentonite panels
- Cementitious coatings

### Negative-Side Waterproofing

Applied to the interior side after construction:

**Advantages:**
- Accessible for retrofit applications
- Allows inspection and maintenance
- Effective for rehabilitation projects

**Limitations:**
- Structural concrete remains saturated
- Hydrostatic pressure against system
- May require ongoing maintenance

### Blind-Side Waterproofing

Installed before foundation placement:

**Applications:**
- Zero-lot-line construction
- Adjacent to existing structures
- Limited excavation access

**System Types:**
- HDPE sheet drainage boards
- Self-adhering membranes
- Spray-applied systems

## Drainage System Design

Effective waterproofing requires integrated drainage:

### Perimeter Drain Systems

Foundation perimeter drains intercept groundwater before it reaches walls:

**Components:**
- 4-6" perforated pipe at footing level
- Filter fabric wrapping
- Clean gravel bedding
- Outlet to daylight or sump

**Design Considerations:**
- Minimum 1% slope to outlet
- Cleanout access at direction changes
- Capacity for anticipated flows
- Connection details at foundations

### Drainage Boards

Composite drainage boards provide both protection and drainage:

**Functions:**
- Physical protection for membrane
- Drainage path for groundwater
- Insulation capability (some products)
- Root barrier function

**Selection Criteria:**
- Compressive strength for backfill loads
- Flow capacity (gallons/min/ft)
- Long-term performance data

### Interior Drainage Systems

For retrofit or supplemental protection:

**Baseboard Systems:**
- Channel installed at wall/floor joint
- Directs seepage to sump
- Minimal disruption to existing space

**Under-Slab Systems:**
- Drainage mat below slab
- Collection piping to sump
- Effective for high water table conditions

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What's the difference between waterproofing and damp-proofing?

Damp-proofing resists moisture migration through concrete but cannot withstand hydrostatic water pressure. Waterproofing systems are designed to resist standing water and positive pressure. For habitable below-grade space in Rancho Cucamonga, true waterproofing is essential.

### How do I know if I need foundation waterproofing?

Any below-grade living space requires waterproofing. Signs existing waterproofing has failed include: water staining on walls, efflorescence (white mineral deposits), musty odors, visible cracks with moisture, and standing water after rain. Our engineers can assess your specific situation.

### Can I add waterproofing to an existing foundation?

Yes, through interior negative-side systems or exterior excavation and membrane installation. Interior systems are less disruptive but may not address all issues. Exterior retrofit provides complete protection but requires excavation. We recommend the appropriate approach based on site conditions.

### What warranty should I expect on waterproofing systems?

Quality waterproofing systems carry manufacturer warranties of 10-25 years depending on system type. Installation warranties vary by contractor. Our specifications require minimum warranty periods and proper warranty documentation for your protection.

### How long does foundation waterproofing last?

Properly designed and installed systems last 25-50+ years. Key factors affecting longevity include: material selection, installation quality, drainage system maintenance, and soil stability. Our designs specify durable materials with proven long-term performance.
