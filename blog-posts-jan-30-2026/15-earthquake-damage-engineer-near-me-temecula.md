## Frequently Asked Questions

### How soon after an earthquake should I get an assessment?

If you observe any damage or have safety concerns, seek assessment immediately. Even without visible damage, professional evaluation is recommended after any earthquake strong enough to be felt. Waiting can allow secondary damage to develop and may complicate insurance claims.

### What if my building was "yellow tagged" after an earthquake?

A yellow tag means restricted use with conditions specified on the posting. You should seek engineering assessment to understand the damage, determine what restrictions apply, and develop a plan for repairs needed to remove the restrictions. We can evaluate the conditions and design appropriate repairs.

### Does homeowner's insurance cover earthquake damage?

Standard California homeowner's insurance does NOT cover earthquake damage. Earthquake coverage requires separate earthquake insurance, either through private insurers or the California Earthquake Authority (CEA). Check your specific policies to understand your coverage.

### How much does earthquake damage repair typically cost?

Costs vary enormously based on damage extent. Minor repairs may cost a few thousand dollars, while major structural damage can cost tens of thousands to hundreds of thousands to repair. Professional assessment provides realistic cost expectations for your specific situation.

### Can I make my home more earthquake-resistant?

Yes. Seismic retrofitting can significantly improve your home's earthquake performance. Common improvements include foundation bolting, cripple wall bracing, shear wall addition, and connection upgrades. Assessment identifies your home's specific vulnerabilities and priorities for improvement.
