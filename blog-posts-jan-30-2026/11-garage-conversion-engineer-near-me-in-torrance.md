### Common Garage Conversion Structural Issues

**Foundation Concerns:**
| Issue | Engineering Solution |
|-------|---------------------|
| Slab thickness inadequate | Verify or overlay existing slab |
| No moisture barrier | Add vapor retarder system |
| Slab-on-grade only | Evaluate for habitable use |
| Foundation not continuous | Add grade beams if needed |

**Wall and Framing Issues:**
| Issue | Engineering Solution |
|-------|---------------------|
| No lateral bracing | Add shear panels |
| Insufficient studs | Sister or add framing |
| Large door opening | Design header and posts |
| No top plate connection | Add hold-downs and straps |

### How do I find a garage conversion engineer near me in Torrance?

To find a qualified garage conversion engineer near you in Torrance, look for California PE-licensed structural engineers with ADU and residential conversion experience. Verify their license at bpelsg.ca.gov and ask about their familiarity with Torrance Building Division requirements. AAA Engineering Design serves Torrance and the South Bay with PE-licensed garage conversion engineering services.

### Do I need an engineer for a garage conversion in Torrance?

Yes, Torrance requires PE-stamped structural plans for garage conversions to habitable space. California Building Code requires engineering analysis to verify foundation adequacy, design lateral bracing, and ensure structural safety for occupancy. Only a licensed Professional Engineer can stamp structural plans accepted by the Torrance Building Division.

### How much does a garage conversion engineer cost in Torrance?

Garage conversion engineering in Torrance typically costs $2,500-$8,000 depending on project complexity. Simple attached garage conversions start around $2,500-$4,000, while larger detached garage or two-car conversions range $5,000-$8,000. Costs vary based on existing conditions, bathroom/kitchen additions, and any structural deficiencies requiring correction.

### How long does garage conversion permitting take in Torrance?

Garage conversion permitting in Torrance typically takes 5-9 weeks total: 2-3 weeks for engineering design, 2-4 weeks for City plan check, and 1-2 weeks for any corrections. State law requires cities to process ADU permits within 60 days. Working with an experienced local engineer can help minimize delays.

### Can I convert my garage to an ADU in Torrance?

Yes, California state law (SB 9, AB 68) strongly supports garage-to-ADU conversions. In Torrance, you can convert an attached or detached garage to an ADU without replacing lost parking spaces, often with reduced fees. Structural engineering is required to ensure the conversion meets building code for habitable space.

### What structural work is needed for a Torrance garage conversion?

Typical Torrance garage conversion structural work includes: foundation assessment and possible improvements, adding lateral bracing (shear panels), installing proper floor insulation and vapor barriers, designing headers for new window and door openings, and ensuring roof structure meets habitable space requirements. Your engineer will identify specific needs during site assessment.
