## What is Flex Space Structural Engineering?

Flex space structural engineering involves designing buildings that accommodate multiple use types—office, showroom, light manufacturing, and warehouse—within a single adaptable structure. In Riverside's diverse commercial market, these buildings serve businesses ranging from e-commerce operations to professional services to light assembly.

## Flex Space Structural Requirements

### Multi-Occupancy Design

Flex buildings must accommodate various California Building Code occupancy classifications:

| Occupancy Type | Use Description | Structural Requirement |
|----------------|-----------------|----------------------|
| B (Business) | Office, professional | 50 PSF floor load |
| M (Mercantile) | Retail, showroom | 100 PSF floor load |
| S-1 (Storage) | Warehouse | 250 PSF floor load |
| F-1 (Factory) | Light manufacturing | 125 PSF floor load |

Our designs accommodate the most demanding occupancy type throughout, ensuring maximum flexibility.

### Clear Height Considerations

Flex space clear heights balance office and industrial needs:

**Standard Configurations:**
- 18-20 feet clear: Small flex (office-heavy)
- 22-24 feet clear: Medium flex (balanced use)
- 26-28 feet clear: Industrial flex (warehouse-heavy)

**Structural Implications:**
- Taller buildings require larger structural members
- Column spacing affects office layout options
- Roof structure impacts HVAC installation costs

### Divisibility Requirements

Multi-tenant flex buildings require structural provisions for division:

- Demising wall locations at structural bays
- Fire separation requirements between tenants
- Independent utility distribution capability
- Shared restroom arrangements where permitted

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What ceiling height should I design for flex space in Riverside?

For maximum tenant flexibility in Riverside, design for 24 feet clear height minimum. This accommodates light industrial users while remaining practical for office conversion. Higher ceilings (28'+) may be warranted for e-commerce and distribution tenants.

### Can I change occupancy type after construction?

Building code occupancy changes require permit review. If the original structure was designed for the highest anticipated loads (typically S-1 warehouse), most occupancy changes require only minor modifications. We design for maximum flexibility from the start.

### How do I design for future building expansion?

Expansion provisions include designing "soft" end walls without structural bracing, oversizing foundations at expansion locations, and documenting future connection details. This adds 5-10% to initial engineering cost but significantly reduces future expansion costs.

### What floor loads should I design for in a flex building?

Design for 250 PSF minimum throughout warehouse areas to accommodate standard racking systems. This exceeds pure office requirements but ensures the building remains competitive for all potential users. Office areas need only 50 PSF but are often built to warehouse standards for flexibility.

### How long does flex building permitting take in Riverside?

Riverside Building & Safety typically processes flex building plans in 4-6 weeks for initial review. Well-prepared submittals with complete structural calculations often receive approval with minimal corrections. Our engineering team's experience helps expedite the process.
