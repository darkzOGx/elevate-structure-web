## What Is Foundation Underpinning and Why Do Hermosa Beach Properties Need It?

Foundation underpinning is the structural engineering process of strengthening and deepening existing foundations by extending them to more competent bearing soil or rock below the original foundation depth. This technique stabilizes foundations experiencing settlement, enables basement additions beneath existing structures, and allows foundation lowering to increase headroom in below-grade spaces.

Hermosa Beach properties face unique foundation challenges driven by coastal geology. The city sits on a mix of beach sand, marine terrace deposits, and older alluvial soils that vary dramatically across short distances. Properties on The Strand and adjacent streets contend with shallow groundwater tables and sandy soils that provide limited bearing capacity. Hillside properties above Pacific Coast Highway encounter different challenges with layered clays and weathered bedrock.

The coastal South Bay market, including Manhattan Beach, Redondo Beach, El Segundo, and Torrance, has seen explosive renovation activity as property values exceed $1,800 per square foot in prime locations. Homeowners maximizing the value of their land frequently turn to underpinning to create basement living space, correct decades of settlement, or support major additions that increase structural loads beyond original foundation capacity.

According to the California Geological Survey, the South Bay coastal zone experiences ongoing ground settlement of 0.5-2 millimeters annually from groundwater extraction and natural compaction. Over a 50-year building lifespan, this cumulative settlement causes differential foundation movement that damages structures. Foundation underpinning engineering addresses these conditions with permanent structural solutions designed by PE-licensed engineers.

## What Foundation Underpinning Services Are Available in Hermosa Beach?

### Push Pier (Resistance Pier) Systems

Push pier underpinning uses hydraulic rams to drive steel pipe sections through existing foundation brackets into load-bearing soil or bedrock below. Each pier is test-loaded during installation, providing verified capacity data. Push piers are ideal for Hermosa Beach properties where competent bearing material exists at depths of 15-35 feet. Installation is clean, quiet, and generates no spoils, making it suitable for the tight lot conditions typical of beach communities.

### Helical Pier Systems

Helical piers use screw-shaped steel plates welded to shaft sections that are rotated into the ground using hydraulic torque motors. The helical plates provide bearing capacity in sandy soils where push piers might not develop adequate skin friction. For Hermosa Beach's sandy coastal conditions, helical piers frequently outperform other methods because they engage the soil through direct bearing rather than relying on friction. Torque-to-capacity correlations provide real-time installation verification.

### Mass Pour (Pit) Underpinning

Mass pour underpinning involves excavating beneath existing foundations in controlled sequences and placing new concrete to extend the foundation depth. This traditional method is particularly effective for basement additions and foundation lowering projects where the entire foundation elevation changes. In Hermosa Beach, mass pour underpinning is the primary technique for converting crawl spaces to full basements, creating valuable below-grade square footage.

### Micropile Underpinning

Micropiles are small-diameter (3-12 inch) drilled and grouted piles that transfer foundation loads to deep bearing strata. They are ideal for Hermosa Beach projects with severely limited access, such as interior underpinning beneath existing structures where conventional pier equipment cannot reach. Micropiles can be installed through the existing foundation slab using portable equipment.

### Foundation Lowering and Basement Addition

Foundation lowering combines underpinning with controlled excavation to create new below-grade living space beneath existing structures. In Hermosa Beach, where lot sizes average 2,500-3,500 square feet, basement additions provide 800-1,500 square feet of new living area without increasing the building footprint. This service integrates structural engineering, geotechnical analysis, waterproofing design, and construction sequencing.

### Settlement Correction

When foundations experience differential settlement (one area sinking more than another), underpinning piers can both stabilize and lift the foundation back toward level. Hydraulic jacking through pier brackets provides controlled lifting in increments as small as 1/16 inch, allowing precise leveling without damaging the structure above.

## How Does the Foundation Underpinning Process Work in Hermosa Beach?

### Phase 1: Foundation Assessment and Documentation

Every underpinning project begins with a thorough [structural inspection](/services/structural-inspection) of the existing foundation and structure above. The engineer documents crack patterns, measures elevation differences using a manometer or digital level survey, evaluates structural connections, and photographs existing conditions. In Hermosa Beach, this assessment also evaluates proximity to neighboring structures, access limitations, and groundwater conditions.

The elevation survey is critical. Digital manometer surveys measure foundation elevations at 50+ points across the structure, revealing the pattern and magnitude of differential settlement. This data drives the engineering design by identifying which portions of the foundation require underpinning and how much lift, if any, is needed.

### Phase 2: Geotechnical Investigation

Soil borings determine the subsurface conditions that dictate underpinning method and depth. For Hermosa Beach properties, borings typically reveal beach sand and marine deposits in the upper 10-20 feet, transitioning to stiffer materials at greater depth. The geotechnical report specifies allowable bearing pressures, groundwater depth, and recommended pier types and depths.

Properties within 500 feet of the shoreline frequently encounter groundwater at 8-15 feet below grade, which significantly influences underpinning method selection and construction sequencing. Dewatering systems may be required during mass pour operations.

### Phase 3: Engineering Design

The structural engineer designs the underpinning system based on the structural assessment and geotechnical data. Design elements include pier locations and spacing, pier type and capacity, bracket configurations, jacking sequences, and temporary shoring requirements. For basement additions, the design encompasses new foundation walls, waterproofing details, drainage systems, and structural connections to the existing building.

Construction sequencing is particularly important for underpinning. The engineer specifies the order in which sections of the foundation can be exposed and underpinned without compromising the stability of the remaining structure. In Hermosa Beach, where properties often share party walls or sit within inches of neighbors, this sequencing must also protect adjacent structures.

### Phase 4: Permitting

The City of Hermosa Beach Building Division requires PE-stamped structural plans for all underpinning work. Plan check typically takes 3-6 weeks and includes review by both structural and geotechnical plan check engineers. AAA Engineering Design navigates this process with familiarity gained from multiple projects within Hermosa Beach's jurisdiction.

Coastal development permits may be required for properties within the Coastal Zone, adding California Coastal Commission review requirements. Our team identifies these requirements early and incorporates them into the project timeline.

### Phase 5: Construction and Quality Assurance

During construction, the structural engineer provides observation and special inspection services. For push and helical pier installations, the engineer verifies that each pier achieves the specified capacity through installation data (driving resistance or torque readings). For mass pour operations, pre-pour inspections verify reinforcement placement, formwork, and bearing conditions.

AAA Engineering Design maintains communication between the contractor, homeowner, and building department throughout construction, resolving field conditions that differ from the assumptions made during design.

## What Are Common Challenges with Foundation Underpinning in Hermosa Beach?

### Challenge: Variable Sandy Soil Conditions

**Problem:** Hermosa Beach's coastal sand deposits vary in density and composition over short distances. Pier capacity can change significantly between adjacent pier locations, requiring field adjustments to the engineering design.

**Solution:** AAA Engineering Design specifies performance-based pier installation criteria (minimum driving resistance or torque) rather than fixed depths. This allows each pier to be installed to verified capacity regardless of depth variation. On-site engineering observation during installation ensures quality.

### Challenge: High Water Table Interference

**Problem:** Groundwater at 8-15 feet below grade floods excavations during mass pour underpinning and affects pier installation in saturated sand. Uncontrolled water degrades concrete quality and reduces bearing capacity.

**Solution:** Engineering designs include dewatering specifications tailored to Hermosa Beach groundwater conditions. Well-point or sump pump systems maintain dry working conditions during construction. Waterproofing details address permanent groundwater exposure for completed underpinning.

### Challenge: Adjacent Structure Protection

**Problem:** Hermosa Beach properties frequently abut neighboring structures with minimal setbacks. Underpinning excavation and pier installation can affect adjacent foundations if not properly engineered.

**Solution:** Pre-construction surveys document adjacent structure conditions. Engineering designs include monitoring protocols, excavation limits, and sequencing requirements that protect neighboring properties. Communication with adjacent property owners maintains positive relationships.

### Challenge: Coastal Commission Requirements

**Problem:** Properties within the Coastal Zone require California Coastal Commission review, which can add 3-6 months to project timelines and impose conditions that affect the engineering design.

**Solution:** Early identification of Coastal Zone properties allows incorporation of CCC requirements into the initial engineering design, avoiding costly redesigns. AAA Engineering Design identifies these requirements during the initial assessment phase.

### Challenge: Corrosion in Marine Environment

**Problem:** Salt air and brackish groundwater accelerate steel corrosion, reducing the service life of underpinning pier systems if not properly protected.

**Solution:** Engineering specifications require corrosion protection systems (galvanizing, epoxy coating, or sacrificial anodes) calibrated to the measured corrosivity of site soils and groundwater. Design life targets of 75+ years are achievable with proper protection.
