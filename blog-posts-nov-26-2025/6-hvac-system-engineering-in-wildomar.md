## What Does HVAC System Engineering Include in Wildomar Projects?

**Direct Answer:**
HVAC engineering includes load calculations, equipment sizing, duct system design, ventilation analysis, Title 24 energy compliance, and control system specification. Each element must be engineered to create comfortable environments while meeting California's energy efficiency requirements and local code standards.

**Heating and Cooling Load Calculations**
Manual J calculations determining heating and cooling requirements based on building construction, orientation, occupancy, and Wildomar's climate data. Accurate load calculations prevent oversizing (wasting energy) or undersizing (inadequate comfort).

**Equipment Selection and Sizing**
Specification of HVAC equipment including furnaces, air conditioners, heat pumps, and ventilation systems. Equipment must be appropriately sized and meet minimum efficiency requirements for Title 24 compliance.

**Duct System Design**
Layout and sizing of supply and return ductwork to distribute conditioned air efficiently. Duct design significantly impacts system efficiency, noise levels, and comfort throughout the building.

**Title 24 Energy Compliance**
Documentation demonstrating HVAC system compliance with California's energy standards, including equipment efficiency, duct insulation, and system controls. Required for all permitted HVAC installations in Wildomar.
