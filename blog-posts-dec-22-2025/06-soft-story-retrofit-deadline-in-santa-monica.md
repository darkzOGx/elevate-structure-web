## What is Soft-Story Retrofit in Santa Monica?

**Direct Answer:** Soft-story retrofit in Santa Monica involves mandatory structural upgrades to multi-unit residential buildings with weak ground floors, typically those with open parking or commercial space below apartments. The City of Santa Monica's seismic retrofit ordinance requires property owners to strengthen these vulnerable buildings to prevent collapse during earthquakes.

Soft-story buildings are characterized by first floors that are significantly weaker than upper floors due to large openings for parking, storefronts, or other uses. During earthquakes, these weak stories can collapse, causing the upper floors to pancake down.

Santa Monica's retrofit ordinance targets buildings most likely to suffer catastrophic damage, protecting residents and reducing earthquake recovery costs. Our engineers provide [complete structural engineering services](/services/structural-engineering) for compliance.

## What Buildings Require Soft-Story Retrofit in Santa Monica?

**Direct Answer:** Santa Monica's mandatory soft-story retrofit ordinance applies to wood-frame residential buildings with three or more stories, two or more dwelling units, and soft or weak first stories. Buildings built before 1978 with tuck-under parking or ground-floor commercial space are primary targets.

**Ordinance Requirements**

**Covered Buildings:**
- Wood-frame construction
- 3+ stories (or 2 stories over parking)
- 2+ residential units
- Built before January 1, 1978
- Soft, weak, or open-front first story

**Exemptions:**
- Buildings with recent seismic upgrades
- Structures with adequate existing lateral systems
- Buildings with documented retrofit history

**Compliance Timeline**

Santa Monica has established phased deadlines:
- Tier 1 buildings: Earlier deadline (check city website)
- Tier 2 buildings: Later deadline
- Extensions available under certain conditions

Property owners should verify their building's tier and deadline with Santa Monica Building & Safety.

The process includes structural evaluation, retrofit design using steel moment frames or other approved systems, permit approval through Santa Monica Building & Safety, construction, and final inspection for compliance certification.

**Santa Monica Assessment**

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Is soft-story retrofit mandatory in Santa Monica?**
Yes, Santa Monica has a mandatory soft-story retrofit ordinance requiring qualifying buildings to be upgraded by specified deadlines. Non-compliance results in fines, vacate orders, and other enforcement actions.

**How much does soft-story retrofit cost in Santa Monica?**
Total costs (engineering + construction) typically range from $60,000-$200,000 for 8-16 unit buildings. Engineering alone costs $10,000-$30,000. Costs per unit generally decrease as building size increases.

**How long does soft-story retrofit take?**
Engineering typically requires 6-10 weeks, permitting 2-4 weeks, and construction 4-12 weeks depending on building size. Total timeline from start to compliance certification is typically 4-6 months.

**Can I pass retrofit costs to tenants?**
Santa Monica rent stabilization regulations allow certain retrofit costs to be passed through to tenants. Consult with an attorney or the Santa Monica Rent Control Board for current regulations.

**What happens if I miss my deadline?**
Missing your soft-story retrofit deadline triggers enforcement actions including fines, violation notices, potential vacate orders, and recordation on property title.

**Do I need to relocate tenants during retrofit?**
Most soft-story retrofits can be completed with tenants in place. Some parking displacement is typical. Work scheduling minimizes noise impacts on residents.
