## What Is Fire-Resistant Structural Design?

**Direct Answer:**
Fire-resistant structural design integrates wildfire protection measures into building structures through ignition-resistant materials, ember-resistant detailing, and construction assemblies that resist both direct flame contact and radiant heat exposure. This includes non-combustible exterior walls, fire-rated roof assemblies, protected vents and openings, and structural systems designed to maintain integrity during fire exposure.

Fire-resistant construction in Montecito addresses multiple ignition sources:

- **Ember Attack**: Burning debris carried by wind
- **Radiant Heat**: Heat radiation from nearby flames
- **Direct Flame Contact**: Fire reaching structure
- **Ground Fire**: Surface fire near foundation
- **Brand Accumulation**: Debris collecting in vulnerable areas

Santa Barbara County and the City of Montecito enforce California's Chapter 7A requirements for buildings in fire hazard severity zones.
