## Frequently Asked Questions

**Do I need a structural engineer for coastal bluff construction in Malibu?**
Yes, California law requires a licensed Professional Engineer (PE) for all coastal bluff construction, stabilization, and foundation design. The City of Malibu and Coastal Commission require PE-stamped plans for permit approval. Coastal projects typically require coordination with geotechnical engineers and coastal geologists as well.

**How much does coastal bluff stabilization cost in Malibu?**
Coastal bluff stabilization in Malibu typically costs $50,000-$150,000+ for complete soldier pile or soil nail wall systems. Engineering costs range from $25,000-$75,000 depending on project complexity. Smaller interventions like drainage improvements may cost $15,000-$30,000.

**How long does Coastal Commission approval take in Malibu?**
Standard Coastal Development Permit (CDP) applications typically require 6-12 months for approval. Emergency permits for active erosion threats can be obtained in days to weeks. We support the entire application process and respond to Commission staff comments.

**Can you stop bluff erosion on my Malibu property?**
While erosion cannot be completely stopped, engineered stabilization systems can significantly slow erosion and protect structures for 50-75+ years. Solutions include soldier pile walls, soil nail systems, drainage improvements, and protective structures like seawalls.

**What is a Coastal Development Permit (CDP)?**
A CDP is required for most development within the Coastal Zone, including structural work on coastal bluffs. The California Coastal Commission reviews CDPs to ensure projects comply with the California Coastal Act, protect public access, and minimize coastal resource impacts.

**Do you provide emergency services for bluff erosion in Malibu?**
Yes, we offer emergency response for active bluff erosion threatening structures. We can assess conditions, recommend immediate stabilization measures, and apply for emergency CDP authorization when warranted by imminent threat.

**How do I know if my Malibu property is on an unstable bluff?**
Signs of bluff instability include visible cracks in slopes, leaning vegetation, foundation settlement, water seepage from bluff faces, and comparison of current bluff edge position to historical aerial photos. We provide bluff stability assessments to evaluate property conditions.

**Can I add a seawall to protect my Malibu beach property?**
Seawalls are increasingly difficult to permit through the Coastal Commission due to their impact on beach access and sand transport. However, they may be approved when necessary to protect existing structures. We evaluate all protection options and recommend the approach most likely to gain approval.
