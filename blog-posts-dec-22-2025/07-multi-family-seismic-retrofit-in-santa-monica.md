## What Is Multi-Family Seismic Retrofit Engineering?

**Direct Answer:**
Multi-family seismic retrofit engineering encompasses the structural evaluation and strengthening of existing apartment buildings to meet current seismic safety standards. For soft-story buildings, this typically involves adding steel moment frames or plywood shear walls to the weak ground floor, upgrading connections throughout the structure, and improving foundation anchorage‚Äîall designed to prevent collapse during major earthquakes.

Soft-story buildings are vulnerable because:

- **Weak First Floor**: Open parking or commercial space lacks adequate walls
- **Torsional Irregularity**: Uneven wall distribution causes twisting
- **Connection Deficiencies**: Older buildings lack modern hardware
- **Foundation Issues**: Inadequate anchorage to foundations

Santa Monica requires mandatory retrofit for qualifying buildings, with significant penalties for non-compliance including daily fines and loss of certificate of occupancy.

## What Are Santa Monica's Soft-Story Retrofit Requirements?

**Direct Answer:**
Santa Monica requires owners of soft-story buildings (wood-frame, 3+ stories or 2+ stories with ground-floor parking/commercial, built before 1978) to submit retrofit plans and complete construction within mandated timeframes. The ordinance establishes a phased compliance schedule with specific deadlines for screening, engineering, permits, and completion. Non-compliance results in escalating fines and potential certificate of occupancy revocation.

**Qualifying Buildings**

Santa Monica's ordinance applies to buildings meeting ALL criteria:

- Wood-frame construction
- Three or more stories, OR two or more stories with ground-floor parking or commercial
- Built before January 1, 1978
- Soft, weak, or open-front ground floor condition

**Compliance Timeline**

Building owners must meet phased deadlines:

1. **Screening**: Submit structural screening report
2. **Engineering**: Submit PE-stamped retrofit plans
3. **Permit**: Obtain building permit for retrofit work
4. **Completion**: Complete construction and final inspection

**Penalties for Non-Compliance**

- Daily fines accumulating until compliance
- Certificate of Occupancy revocation potential
- Property sale complications
- Insurance and lending issues
- Liability exposure in future earthquakes
