## Frequently Asked Questions

**Do I need a structural engineer for a basement in Palo Alto?**
Yes, California law requires a licensed Professional Engineer (PE) for basement design. Palo Alto Building Division requires PE-stamped structural drawings for all basement construction, including shoring design, foundation walls, and floor slabs.

**How much does basement construction cost in Palo Alto?**
Complete basement construction in Palo Alto typically costs $400-$600 per square foot including excavation, concrete, waterproofing, and basic finishes. A 2,500 sq ft basement may total $1-1.5 million. Engineering costs are typically 2-5% of construction cost.

**Can I add a basement under my existing Palo Alto home?**
Yes, basement addition under existing homes (underpinning) is possible in most cases. The process involves sequentially excavating beneath existing foundations while installing new support. Engineering is more complex than new construction basements.

**How deep can basements go in Palo Alto?**
Palo Alto allows basements up to 12 feet below grade in most zones. Some areas have additional restrictions. Practical limits are often determined by groundwater levels and soil conditions rather than code maximums.

**Does basement area count toward Palo Alto FAR?**
Partially. Palo Alto counts some basement area toward floor area ratio (FAR), which limits total building size. Specific calculation rules depend on basement depth and configuration. Early consultation with Planning is recommended.

**How long does basement permit approval take in Palo Alto?**
Basement permits typically require 4-8 weeks for plan check in Palo Alto. More complex projects or those requiring Planning review may take longer. We provide permit support throughout the review process.

**What about groundwater in Palo Alto basements?**
Groundwater is common in Palo Alto, with water tables at 8-20 feet depending on location. Basement design must include waterproofing systems and often drainage provisions. We design for site-specific water conditions identified in geotechnical reports.

**Do I need shoring for basement excavation in Palo Alto?**
Yes, excavation support is required for most Palo Alto basement projects. Shoring protects adjacent properties and utilities during construction. Shoring design is typically included in our basement engineering scope.
