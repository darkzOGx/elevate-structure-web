## What Is Coastal Bluff Stabilization Engineering?

**Direct Answer:**
Coastal bluff stabilization engineering encompasses structural and geotechnical solutions to reduce cliff erosion and protect bluff-top structures. This includes soldier pile walls, soil nailing, shotcrete facing, drainage systems, and foundation setback analysisâ€”all coordinated with California Coastal Commission requirements governing development in the coastal zone.

Bluff stabilization addresses multiple erosion mechanisms:

- **Wave Action**: Direct erosion at cliff base
- **Surface Water**: Runoff eroding bluff face
- **Groundwater**: Seepage undermining stability
- **Notching**: Undermining at cliff toe
- **Sloughing**: Progressive face erosion

The California Coastal Commission and City of San Diego jointly regulate bluff development and stabilization, requiring careful permit navigation.
