## What Is Historic Structural Upgrade Engineering?

**Direct Answer:**
Historic structural upgrade engineering encompasses seismic retrofitting and foundation repair for vintage homes using preservation-sensitive techniques. This includes cripple wall bracing, foundation bolting, foundation replacement, and selective structural strengtheningâ€”all designed to improve earthquake performance while maintaining historic character and meeting any local historic preservation requirements.

Historic home structural upgrades address common deficiencies:

- **Unbraced Cripple Walls**: Short stud walls in crawl spaces
- **Unbolted Foundations**: Homes simply resting on foundations
- **Unreinforced Masonry**: Brick chimneys and foundations
- **Inadequate Connections**: Missing or deteriorated hardware
- **Foundation Deterioration**: Age-related concrete and masonry damage

Pasadena's historic preservation requirements may influence how upgrades are designed and implemented, particularly for designated historic properties.
