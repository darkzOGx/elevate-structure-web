## What Does a Foundation Crack Engineer Evaluate?

**Direct Answer:**

### Crack Characteristics

**Detailed Documentation**
Engineers document specific crack features:

**Movement Assessment**

## What Types of Foundation Cracks Are Common in Fontana?

**Direct Answer:**
Common foundation crack types in Fontana include shrinkage cracks from concrete curing, settlement cracks from soil compression, heave cracks from expansive clay soil swelling, and seismic cracks from earthquake activity. Fontana's predominant expansive clay soils make heave-related cracking particularly common, especially where moisture variations are significant due to irrigation, drainage issues, or seasonal changes.

### Shrinkage Cracks

**Normal Concrete Behavior**
Shrinkage cracks are most common and least concerning:
- Typically hairline to 1/8 inch wide
- Random or map-crack patterns
- Develop during initial curing (first 1-5 years)
- Generally stable after formation
- Usually cosmetic, not structural

Most slab-on-grade foundations in Fontana develop some shrinkage crackingâ€”it's expected concrete behavior.

### Settlement Cracks

**Soil Compression Issues**
Settlement cracks indicate foundation movement:
- Often diagonal or stair-step patterns
- Wider at top than bottom typically
- May appear near building corners
- Can progress if settlement continues
- Require cause evaluation

Settlement may be uniform (even across foundation) or differential (uneven, causing tilting).

### Heave Cracks

**Expansive Soil Damage**
Heave cracks result from soil swelling:
- Common in Fontana's clay soil areas
- Often appear after wet seasons
- May close during dry periods
- Central slab heaving is typical
- Edge heaving from over-irrigation

Expansive soil issues are particularly prevalent in Fontana subdivisions built on clay-rich native soils.

### Seismic Cracks

**Earthquake Damage**
Seismic cracks result from ground motion:
- Diagonal shear cracks
- Separation at construction joints
- New cracks appearing after earthquakes
- May indicate structural distress
- Require immediate evaluation

Fontana's proximity to the San Andreas and San Jacinto faults creates ongoing seismic risk.

### Construction-Related Cracks

**Quality and Design Issues**
Some cracks relate to original construction:
- Cold joints from pour delays
- Improper reinforcement placement
- Inadequate concrete strength
- Missing or failed control joints
- Design deficiencies for site conditions

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Are all foundation cracks serious?

No, most foundation cracks are not serious structural concerns. Hairline shrinkage cracks are normal in concrete and don't indicate structural problems. However, some crack patternsâ€”particularly wide cracks, diagonal cracks, or cracks accompanied by floor slopes and sticking doorsâ€”may indicate settlement or structural issues requiring attention. Professional assessment determines which category your cracks fall into.

### Should I fill or seal foundation cracks myself?

For cosmetic improvement of minor shrinkage cracks, DIY sealing is reasonable. However, sealing cracks without understanding the underlying cause can be problematicâ€”it may hide active movement or prevent necessary drainage. Before sealing, have significant cracks professionally evaluated to ensure you're not masking a structural issue.

### How do I know if my cracks are getting worse?

Monitor cracks by marking their ends with dated pencil marks and measuring widths periodically (monthly or quarterly). If cracks extend past your marks or widths increase, the cracks are active and professional evaluation is recommended. Crack monitors (available at hardware stores) can also track movement.

### Will my homeowner's insurance cover foundation crack repair?

Standard homeowner's insurance typically doesn't cover foundation damage from normal settlement or expansive soil movementâ€”these are considered maintenance issues. However, sudden damage from covered perils (earthquakes with earthquake insurance, plumbing failures, vehicle impacts) may be covered. Review your policy or consult your insurance agent for specific coverage questions.
