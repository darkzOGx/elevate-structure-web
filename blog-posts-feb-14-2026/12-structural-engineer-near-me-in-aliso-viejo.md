## What Does a Structural Engineer Do?

A structural engineer ensures buildings and structures safely support all applied loads throughout their intended lifespan. This discipline combines physics, materials science, and construction knowledge to create safe, efficient, and code-compliant structural systems.

### Primary Functions

**Structural Design:** Engineers calculate the forces acting on a structure — gravity, wind, earthquakes, and occupancy loads — then design beams, columns, walls, foundations, and connections to resist those forces with mandated safety factors. Every element is sized according to the California Building Code and referenced standards like ASCE 7-22 and ACI 318.

**Structural Analysis:** For existing buildings, engineers evaluate whether current structural systems adequately resist applied loads. This analysis is critical during renovations, change-of-use projects, and when structural damage or deterioration is suspected. Learn more about [structural inspection services](/services/structural-inspection) and their role in property evaluation.

**Plan Preparation and PE Stamping:** California law requires a licensed Professional Engineer to stamp structural plans submitted for building permits. These plans contain detailed calculations, drawings, specifications, and notes that guide contractors during construction. Our [permit engineering services](/services/permit-engineering) streamline this process.

**Construction Observation:** During building, structural engineers verify that construction matches approved plans. This includes checking reinforcement placement before concrete pours, verifying steel connections, confirming wood framing hardware, and documenting compliance at critical construction stages.

## Aliso Viejo Building Codes and Permit Requirements

### Governing Codes

The City of Aliso Viejo enforces the **2022 California Building Code** (CBC) for all construction. Key structural provisions include:

- **Seismic Design Category D or E:** Aliso Viejo's proximity to active faults (including the Cristianitos and Newport-Inglewood fault zones) places it in a high seismic design category. All structural designs must resist significant earthquake forces.
- **ASCE 7-22 Load Standards:** Gravity, wind, and seismic loads are determined per ASCE 7-22 as adopted by the CBC.
- **Wood Frame Construction:** The majority of residential construction follows the National Design Specification (NDS) for wood and International Residential Code (IRC) provisions.
- **Concrete and Masonry:** Foundation and retaining wall designs follow ACI 318 and TMS 402 standards.

### Aliso Viejo Permit Process

1. **Pre-Application Meeting (Optional):** For complex projects, a pre-application meeting with the Building Division clarifies requirements before you invest in engineering.
2. **Permit Application Submittal:** Submit structural plans, architectural plans, and the permit application to the City of Aliso Viejo Community Development Department.
3. **Plan Check Review:** Structural plans are reviewed for code compliance. Initial review typically takes 2-4 weeks.
4. **Correction Response:** Your structural engineer addresses any plan check comments with revised plans or supplemental calculations.
5. **Permit Issuance:** Once plans are approved, the building permit is issued.
6. **Construction Inspections:** Required inspections include foundation (before concrete pour), framing (before covering), and final inspection.

### Special Requirements

- **Condo/Townhome Modifications:** Require HOA architectural committee approval in addition to city permits
- **Hillside Development:** Additional geotechnical and grading requirements apply to sloped properties
- **Historical Considerations:** Some early Aliso Viejo developments have specific CC&Rs affecting modifications
- **Energy Code Compliance:** Title 24 energy standards must be coordinated with structural modifications

### When do I need a structural engineer vs. an architect?

### What is the difference between a structural engineer and a general contractor?

A structural engineer designs the structural system and produces PE-stamped plans based on engineering calculations and building code requirements. A general contractor builds according to those plans. Contractors have construction expertise but lack the engineering education, licensure, and liability to design structural systems. Both are essential for successful projects.

### How do earthquakes affect structural engineering requirements in Aliso Viejo?

Aliso Viejo sits in Seismic Design Category D, requiring all structural modifications to resist significant earthquake forces. Engineers must design lateral force-resisting systems (shear walls, moment frames, braced frames) for every project, include proper connections and holdowns, and ensure foundations anchor structures against seismic movement.
