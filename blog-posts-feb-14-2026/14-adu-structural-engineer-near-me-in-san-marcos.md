## What Does an ADU Structural Engineer Do?

An ADU structural engineer designs every load-bearing element of your accessory dwelling unit — from foundation to roof — ensuring the structure safely supports all applied forces and meets California Building Code requirements.

### ADU-Specific Engineering Services

**Garage Conversion Engineering:** Converting an existing garage to an ADU requires significant structural engineering. The engineer evaluates the existing garage structure, designs modifications to meet residential occupancy standards, addresses the garage door opening (which requires a new structural wall and foundation), designs new window and door openings in existing walls, and engineers any interior partition walls. Our [garage conversion engineering services](/services/garage-conversion-engineering) streamline this process.

**Detached ADU Engineering:** A standalone ADU on your property requires complete structural design from the ground up. The engineer designs the foundation system (based on geotechnical conditions), floor framing, wall framing and lateral force-resisting systems, roof structure, and all connections. This is equivalent to designing a small custom home.

**Attached ADU Engineering:** Adding an ADU to your existing home involves the structural challenges of both new construction and renovation. The engineer designs the new structure while ensuring the connection to the existing building maintains structural integrity for both units.

**JADU Engineering (Junior ADU):** JADUs — units of 500 square feet or less created within the existing footprint of a home — require structural engineering when modifications involve load-bearing walls, new openings, or foundation alterations.

**Two-Story ADU Engineering:** Two-story ADUs maximize living space within the allowed footprint but require more complex structural engineering, including [two-story addition engineering](/services/two-story-addition-engineering) principles for proper load path design, lateral force resistance, and foundation capacity.

### Engineering Deliverables for ADU Projects

A complete ADU structural engineering package includes:

- **Foundation plan** showing footing locations, sizes, reinforcement, and anchor bolt layouts
- **Floor framing plan** (if raised floor or second story) with joist sizes, spacing, and bearing points
- **Roof framing plan** with rafter or truss sizes, spacing, and bearing connections
- **Shear wall schedule** identifying lateral force-resisting wall locations and construction
- **Connection details** specifying hardware at all critical structural joints
- **Structural calculations** documenting the engineering analysis supporting the design
- **General structural notes** specifying material grades, construction standards, and inspection requirements
- **PE stamp and signature** required for building permit submittal

## When Do You Need an ADU Structural Engineer in San Marcos?

Every ADU project in San Marcos requires structural engineering. The question is not whether you need an engineer, but when to bring them into the process.

### ADU Project Types Requiring Engineering

**Garage Conversion to ADU:** San Marcos homeowners converting garages to ADUs represent the most common ADU project type. Structural engineering addresses:
- Infilling the garage door opening with a new framed wall on a proper foundation
- Adding windows and doors in existing concrete block or wood-framed walls
- Verifying existing slab adequacy for residential occupancy loads
- Designing interior partition walls (load-bearing and non-load-bearing)
- Ensuring lateral force resistance meets current seismic requirements
- Upgrading connections between walls, roof, and foundation to current code standards

**New Detached ADU Construction:** Building a standalone ADU on your San Marcos property requires complete structural design. Key engineering considerations include:
- Foundation design appropriate for San Marcos soil conditions
- Gravity load design for the specific floor plan and roof configuration
- Seismic design per Seismic Design Category D requirements
- Wind design for the specific exposure category
- Connection design throughout the structure
- Energy code structural coordination (Title 24 requirements)

**Attached ADU Addition:** Adding an ADU attached to your home involves:
- Evaluating existing structural capacity to support the addition connection
- Designing the new structure to function with the existing building
- Engineering the connection between old and new construction
- Verifying existing foundation capacity or designing foundation upgrades
- Ensuring both the existing home and ADU meet current lateral force requirements

**Second-Story ADU:** Building above an existing garage or single-story structure requires:
- Evaluating existing walls and foundation for second-story loads
- Designing the second floor framing system
- Strengthening existing walls and foundations as needed
- Engineering the lateral force-resisting system for the combined structure
- Designing the stairway opening and structural support

### Optimal Timing for Engineering

**Early Design Phase (Recommended):** Engage your structural engineer during schematic design, before architectural plans are finalized. Early involvement ensures the architectural design is structurally feasible, avoids costly redesigns, and produces the most efficient structural solutions.

**After Architectural Plans:** If your architect has already completed design, the structural engineer adapts structural systems to the proposed layout. This works but sometimes results in less efficient structural solutions.

**Before Permit Submittal:** At minimum, structural engineering must be complete before submitting for a building permit. San Marcos requires PE-stamped structural plans as part of the permit application.

## San Marcos ADU Building Codes and Requirements

### California ADU Law (2026)

California's ADU legislation, updated through 2025 legislative sessions, provides the following framework:

- **By-right approval:** Cities must approve ADUs that meet objective standards ministerially (no discretionary review)
- **Size limits:** Up to 1,200 square feet for detached ADUs; 1,000 square feet for attached ADUs; 500 square feet for JADUs
- **Setbacks:** 4-foot rear and side setbacks for detached ADUs; no setback required for garage conversions within existing footprint
- **Height:** Up to 18 feet for detached ADUs (16 feet within 4 feet of property line); taller allowances for two-story ADUs in some zones
- **Parking:** No additional parking required for ADUs
- **Impact fees:** No impact fees for ADUs under 750 square feet; proportional fees for larger units
- **Owner occupancy:** No owner-occupancy requirement for ADUs permitted after January 1, 2025

### San Marcos-Specific Requirements

The City of San Marcos processes ADU permits through its Development Services Department. Key local provisions include:

- **ADU permit processing time:** 60 days maximum from complete application submittal
- **Design standards:** ADU exterior design must be compatible with the primary dwelling
- **Utility connections:** Separate utility connections required for detached ADUs; shared connections allowed for attached ADUs and JADUs
- **Fire sprinklers:** Required when the primary dwelling has sprinklers; required for ADUs with specific fire department access limitations
- **Structural plan requirements:** PE-stamped structural plans required for all ADUs

### Structural Code Requirements

All ADU structural designs in San Marcos must comply with the **2022 California Building Code**:

- **Seismic Design Category D:** Full seismic design required with proper lateral force-resisting systems
- **Foundation requirements:** Minimum 12-inch depth below undisturbed soil; deeper for expansive soils per geotechnical recommendations
- **Wood framing:** Per National Design Specification (NDS) requirements
- **Connections:** Engineered connections at all holdown locations, shear wall boundaries, and critical framing joints
- **Fire separation:** Minimum 1-hour fire-rated wall construction for ADUs within 5 feet of property lines

### Garage Conversion Code Requirements

Converting a garage to an ADU in San Marcos involves specific code transitions:

- **Occupancy change:** From Group U (utility/garage) to Group R-3 (residential), triggering additional structural requirements for habitable space
- **Floor requirements:** Garage slabs must meet residential floor standards or be modified (insulation, leveling, moisture barrier)
- **Wall requirements:** Existing garage walls must be upgraded to residential standards including insulation, drywall, and moisture protection
- **Garage door replacement:** New wall and foundation must be engineered to replace the garage door structural opening
- **Lateral force requirements:** Removing the garage door opening affects the building's lateral force resistance — engineering must compensate with new shear walls

### What is the difference between an ADU and a JADU?

An **ADU (Accessory Dwelling Unit)** is a complete independent living unit up to 1,200 square feet with its own kitchen, bathroom, and entrance. A **JADU (Junior ADU)** is 500 square feet or less, created within the existing footprint of the primary dwelling, and must include a cooking facility (efficiency kitchen). JADUs are simpler and less expensive to build, but offer less space and independence.

The City of San Marcos typically requires a geotechnical investigation report for new detached ADU construction, particularly on sites with expansive soils, fill material, or slopes.
