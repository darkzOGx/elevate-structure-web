## What Does a Seismic Retrofit Engineer Do?

A seismic retrofit engineer evaluates existing buildings for earthquake vulnerability and designs structural modifications that improve their ability to resist seismic forces. This specialized engineering discipline requires deep knowledge of earthquake behavior, existing building construction, retrofit techniques, and California's seismic building codes.

### Core Seismic Retrofit Services

**Seismic Vulnerability Assessment:** The engineer inspects your building to evaluate its current seismic resistance. This assessment examines the foundation anchorage system, cripple wall condition and bracing, shear wall adequacy, structural connection hardware, roof-to-wall connections, chimney stability, and overall structural configuration. The assessment identifies specific vulnerabilities and prioritizes them by risk severity.

**Foundation Bolting Design:** Many older Whittier homes sit on their foundations without proper anchor bolts — the house simply rests on the foundation by gravity. During an earthquake, these unbolted homes slide off their foundations, causing catastrophic damage. Foundation bolting design specifies the size, spacing, and installation method of anchor bolts connecting the wood framing to the concrete foundation.

**Cripple Wall Bracing Design:** Cripple walls are short wood-framed walls between the foundation and first floor found in homes with raised foundations. These walls are extremely vulnerable to earthquake forces — they collapse sideways, dropping the house onto its foundation. Bracing design adds structural plywood sheathing and engineered connections to prevent this failure.

**Soft-Story Retrofit Design:** Soft-story buildings have a weak first floor — typically parking garages with apartments above, or commercial spaces below residential units. The open first floor lacks the lateral stiffness of upper floors, creating a collapse-prone condition. Soft-story retrofit engineering adds steel moment frames, braced frames, or plywood shear walls to strengthen the weak story.

**Structural Connection Upgrades:** Beyond foundation bolting, comprehensive seismic retrofitting includes upgrading connections throughout the structure — holdowns at shear wall ends, framing clips at roof-to-wall connections, strapping at floor-to-wall connections, and hardware at beam-to-post joints. These connections ensure earthquake forces transfer smoothly through the entire structure to the foundation.

**Chimney Bracing and Reinforcement:** Unreinforced masonry chimneys are among the most dangerous elements during earthquakes. Seismic retrofit engineers design bracing systems that prevent chimney collapse or specify chimney replacement with lightweight alternatives.

## Whittier Seismic Building Codes and Retrofit Requirements

### Applicable Codes and Standards

**2022 California Building Code (CBC):** All new construction and major renovations in Whittier must meet current CBC seismic provisions, which place Whittier in **Seismic Design Category D or E** based on site-specific soil conditions and proximity to the Whittier Fault.

**California Existing Building Code (CEBC):** Seismic retrofits of existing buildings follow the CEBC, which provides specific provisions for evaluating and strengthening existing structures without requiring full compliance with new construction standards. This pragmatic approach makes retrofitting feasible and cost-effective.

**ASCE 41 (Seismic Evaluation and Retrofit of Existing Buildings):** This standard provides detailed engineering procedures for evaluating seismic performance and designing retrofits. Engineers use ASCE 41 for performance-based retrofit design that targets specific earthquake performance objectives.

**Los Angeles County Soft-Story Ordinance:** LA County requires seismic retrofit of qualifying soft-story buildings (typically wood-frame structures with ground-floor parking or commercial space and residential units above). Property owners must comply within specified timelines, including engineering evaluation and retrofit construction.

### Whittier Permit Requirements for Seismic Retrofits

1. **Building Permit Required:** All seismic retrofit construction in Whittier requires a building permit from the City of Whittier Building Division.
2. **PE-Stamped Plans:** The permit application must include structural plans stamped by a California-licensed PE.
3. **Plan Check Review:** Structural plans are reviewed for code compliance, typically taking 2-4 weeks for residential retrofits.
4. **Special Inspections:** Certain retrofit elements (epoxy anchor bolts, structural steel connections) require special inspections by independent testing agencies.
5. **Construction Inspections:** City inspectors verify retrofit construction at key milestones before the work is covered.

### Financial Incentives for Seismic Retrofitting

- **FEMA Hazard Mitigation Grants:** Federal funding available through California Office of Emergency Services for qualifying residential retrofits
- **Earthquake Brace + Bolt (EBB) Program:** California Residential Mitigation Program offers grants up to $3,000 for qualifying homeowners to complete foundation bolting and cripple wall bracing
- **California Earthquake Authority (CEA) Premium Discounts:** Retrofitted homes qualify for reduced earthquake insurance premiums, saving hundreds of dollars annually
- **Property Tax Exclusion:** California Revenue and Taxation Code Section 74.5 excludes seismic retrofit improvements from property tax reassessment
