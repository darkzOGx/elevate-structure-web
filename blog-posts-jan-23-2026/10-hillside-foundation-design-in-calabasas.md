## Hillside Foundation Systems

### Drilled Caisson Foundations

Caisson foundations penetrate through surface materials to reach stable bearing for most Calabasas hillside projects.

**Caisson System Elements:**
- Drilled piers to rock or competent soil
- Typical depths 15' to 50' or more
- Reinforced concrete construction
- Grade beam connections
- Void space below grade beams
- Drainage provisions

Our engineers design caisson systems optimized for specific site conditions and structural loads.

### Grade Beam Systems

Grade beams connect caissons and support building framing.

**Grade Beam Design:**
- Spans between caissons
- Structural load distribution
- Void box provisions for soil movement
- Drainage accommodation
- Seismic connection requirements
- Architectural coordination

Properly designed grade beams integrate caisson systems into complete foundation solutions.

### Stepped Footing Systems

Moderate slopes sometimes accommodate stepped continuous footings.

**Stepped Footing Applications:**
- Slopes below 25-30%
- Adequate bearing at shallow depth
- Cost-effective where appropriate
- Retaining wall integration
- Drainage incorporation

We evaluate whether stepped footings can serve specific site conditions.

### Retaining Wall Integration

Hillside construction typically requires retaining walls working with foundations.

**Retaining Wall Types:**
- Conventional cantilever walls
- Soldier pile and lagging systems
- Mechanically stabilized earth
- Crib and bin walls
- Shotcrete walls
- Combination systems

Our foundation designs coordinate with retaining walls for integrated hillside solutions.
