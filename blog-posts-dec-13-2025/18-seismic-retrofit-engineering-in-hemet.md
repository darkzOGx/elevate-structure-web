## What Are Common Seismic Deficiencies in Hemet Homes?

**Direct Answer:** Common deficiencies include unbolted foundations, unbraced cripple walls, weak garage wall lines, unreinforced chimneys, and inadequate wall-to-roof connections. Many Hemet homes built before 1980 have multiple seismic vulnerabilities.

**Deficiency 1: Unbolted Foundations**

**Problem:** Older homes often have mud sills simply resting on foundations without bolting, allowing sliding during earthquakes.

**Solution:** Installation of foundation bolts connecting wood framing to concrete foundation, typically every 4-6 feet per retrofit standards.

**Deficiency 2: Unbraced Cripple Walls**

**Problem:** Short stud walls between foundation and first floor lack bracing, allowing collapse during seismic shaking.

**Solution:** Installation of structural plywood sheathing on cripple walls, connecting foundation to first floor diaphragm with proper nailing and blocking.

**Deficiency 3: Weak Garage Openings**

**Problem:** Large garage door openings significantly reduce lateral resistance on one side of buildings.

**Solution:** Steel moment frames or other reinforcement to provide lateral resistance at garage openings.
