## Frequently Asked Questions

**When do I need an engineer for foundation cracks?**
You need an engineer when cracks are wider than 1/4 inch, show differential movement, are accompanied by floor slopes, or when selling/buying property requiring professional assessment.

**How long does foundation repair engineering take?**
Foundation assessments are typically completed in 1-2 weeks. Complete repair engineering with plans requires 2-4 weeks depending on complexity.

**Can foundation settlement be corrected?**
Yes, settlement can often be corrected through underpinning with piers that lift and stabilize foundations. Releveling degree depends on specific conditions and acceptable movement.

**What causes foundation cracks?**
Causes include soil settlement, concrete shrinkage, expansive soils, thermal movement, structural overload, and drainage problems. Accurate diagnosis is essential for effective repair.

**Do foundation repairs require permits?**
Yes, most foundation repairs require building permits in National City. Our PE-stamped plans provide documentation required for permit approval.

**Will you coordinate with foundation repair contractors?**
Yes, we work with foundation repair contractors, providing engineering support during construction and verification that repairs are completed per design.

**Are your plans accepted by National City Building Department?**
Yes, our PE-stamped plans are accepted by National City and all California building departments.

**How do I know if my foundation problem is serious?**
Signs of serious problems include large cracks (>1/4 inch), cracks with vertical offset, significant floor slopes, sticking doors/windows, and visible foundation movement. Professional assessment provides definitive evaluation.
