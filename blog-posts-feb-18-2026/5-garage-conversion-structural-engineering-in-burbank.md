## What Does a Garage Conversion Engineer Do in Burbank?

A garage conversion engineer in Burbank performs the structural engineering required to convert an existing garage into a habitable accessory dwelling unit (ADU) or additional living space. This conversion involves significant structural modifications that require professional engineering to ensure safety, code compliance, and permit approval.

Garages are designed as storage and parking spaces with structural requirements far less demanding than habitable rooms. Converting a garage to living space requires addressing fundamental differences in structural performance standards:

**Load Requirements Change**: Habitable rooms require different floor load capacities, ceiling heights, and wall configurations than garages. The structural engineer verifies that existing elements meet habitable room standards or designs modifications to achieve compliance.

**Foundation Modifications**: Garage slabs are typically thinner, less reinforced, and sloped for drainage, features that are incompatible with habitable space requirements. Foundation engineering addresses slab thickness, reinforcement, moisture barriers, and level floor surfaces.

**Lateral Force Resistance**: Removing a garage door opening eliminates a large wall section and changes the building's lateral force resistance system. The structural engineer designs new shear walls, hold-downs, and connections that maintain or improve seismic performance.

**Wall Framing**: Garage walls are often uninsulated, unfinished, and may not meet the structural requirements for habitable room walls. Engineering addresses stud spacing, header design, insulation accommodation, and fire resistance ratings.

## What Structural Modifications Are Required for a Burbank Garage Conversion?

Every Burbank garage conversion requires specific structural modifications that must be engineered and permitted. Here are the primary structural engineering elements:

### Foundation Engineering

Existing Burbank garage slabs present several challenges for conversion to habitable space:

**Slab Thickness**: Garage slabs are typically 3.5-4 inches thick, while habitable room foundations require minimum 3.5-inch slabs with specific reinforcement. Many Burbank garage slabs meet thickness requirements but lack reinforcement.

**Slab Slope**: Garage floors slope toward the garage door for drainage, typically at 1/8 to 1/4 inch per foot. Habitable rooms require level floors. Engineering solutions include self-leveling overlay, sleeper systems with leveled subfloor, or in severe cases, partial slab replacement.

**Moisture Barrier**: Garage slabs frequently lack the vapor barrier required beneath habitable room floors. Engineering solutions include topside moisture barriers, epoxy moisture mitigation systems, or raised floor assemblies with ventilated air space.

**Foundation Bolting**: When the garage conversion changes the building's lateral force resistance system, new foundation anchor bolts are required to transfer shear forces from new wall framing to the foundation. Our engineers specify bolt locations, sizes, and embedment depths based on calculated seismic forces.

### Garage Door Infill Wall Engineering

The most visible structural modification in a Burbank garage conversion is replacing the garage door opening with a framed, insulated, and finished wall. This wall must be engineered for:

- **Gravity loads**: Supporting roof and ceiling loads that were previously carried by the garage door header and adjacent framing
- **Lateral loads**: Providing shear resistance as part of the building's seismic force resistance system
- **Connection details**: Tying new framing into existing wall studs, top plates, and foundation with connections that transfer forces reliably
- **Thermal and moisture performance**: Meeting energy code requirements for wall insulation, air sealing, and weather resistance

AAA Engineering Design designs infill walls that integrate structurally with the existing building and satisfy all applicable building code requirements, including California's current energy code standards.

### Roof and Ceiling Modifications

Burbank garage conversions frequently require roof and ceiling engineering:

- **Ceiling height compliance**: California Building Code requires minimum 7-foot ceiling height for habitable rooms. Many Burbank garages meet this requirement, but some require roof raising or alternative ceiling configurations
- **Roof framing verification**: Existing roof trusses or rafters must be verified for habitable room ceiling loads, including drywall attachment, insulation weight, and any mechanical equipment mounted above the ceiling
- **Attic access**: Building code requires attic access in the form of a 22x30-inch minimum opening when the attic space exceeds 30 square feet

### Lateral Force Resistance System Redesign

Removing a garage door and infilling the opening fundamentally changes how the building resists earthquake forces. Our Burbank garage conversion engineering includes complete lateral force analysis:

1. Calculate building seismic forces per current California Building Code
2. Evaluate existing shear wall capacity throughout the building
3. Design new shear wall panels in the infill wall and redistribute forces as needed
4. Specify hold-down anchors, shear transfer connections, and drag strut details
5. Verify foundation capacity for concentrated retrofit forces

This analysis ensures that the converted building meets or exceeds the seismic performance of the original garage configuration.

## What Are Common Engineering Challenges in Burbank Garage Conversions?

### Insufficient Ceiling Height

Some older Burbank garages have ceiling heights below the 7-foot minimum required for habitable rooms. Engineering solutions include lowering the floor (slab removal and re-pour at a lower elevation), raising the roof (structural modification to increase wall height), or designing alternative ceiling configurations that meet code while working within existing constraints.

### Inadequate Foundation

Burbank garages built before 1980 frequently have unreinforced or minimally reinforced slabs that do not meet current foundation requirements. Engineering solutions range from slab overlay systems that add reinforcement and level the surface to complete slab replacement when existing conditions are too deficient to salvage.

### Utility Conflicts

Existing utility runs (gas lines, sewer laterals, water lines, electrical conduits) frequently conflict with proposed garage conversion layouts. Structural engineering must coordinate with mechanical and plumbing design to route utilities without compromising structural elements.

### Shared Wall Conditions

Many Burbank garages share walls with the primary residence. Conversion of these garages requires fire-resistance rated wall assemblies at the shared wall, sound attenuation meeting current code requirements, and structural modifications that do not compromise the existing building's structural integrity.
