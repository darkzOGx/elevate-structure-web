Our Malibu structural inspections include chloride penetration testing for concrete and corrosion mapping for exposed and embedded steel. ## What Are the Warning Signs That Your Malibu Property Needs a Structural Inspection?

Property owners in Malibu should schedule a structural inspection immediately upon observing any of the following conditions:

**Foundation Warning Signs:**
- Cracks wider than 1/4 inch in foundation walls or slabs
- Uneven or sloping floors (measurable with a 4-foot level)
- Doors and windows that stick, bind, or fail to close properly
- Gaps between walls and floor or ceiling surfaces
- Visible water intrusion or efflorescence on foundation surfaces

**Framing and Structural Warning Signs:**
- Sagging rooflines or ridge beams
- Bouncy or deflecting floors under normal foot traffic
- Visible cracks in drywall following diagonal or stair-step patterns
- Separation between chimney and main structure
- Corroded or deteriorated metal connectors visible in crawl spaces or attics

**Malibu-Specific Warning Signs:**
- Rust staining on concrete surfaces (indicating reinforcement corrosion)
- Retaining wall leaning, cracking, or showing displacement
- New cracks appearing after wildfire events, even in properties not directly burned
- Soil movement or settlement visible around foundations after heavy rain
- Deck or balcony surfaces showing bounce, sag, or connection deterioration

AAA Engineering Design recommends that Malibu homeowners schedule routine structural inspections every 5-7 years for standard properties and every 3-5 years for properties within 500 feet of the coastline or on slopes exceeding 15% gradient. Properties in known [foundation concern areas](/services/foundation-inspection) warrant more frequent evaluation.

### What is the difference between a home inspection and a structural inspection in Malibu?

A home inspection is a general visual survey performed by a licensed home inspector covering all building systems (plumbing, electrical, HVAC, structure). A structural inspection is an engineering evaluation performed by a licensed Professional Engineer focusing exclusively on the building's structural systems, load paths, and safety. Only a PE-stamped structural report carries legal weight for building permits, insurance claims, and litigation.

Yes. Structural inspections identify earthquake damage including foundation cracking, shifted framing connections, shear wall failures, chimney separation, and soil settlement.
