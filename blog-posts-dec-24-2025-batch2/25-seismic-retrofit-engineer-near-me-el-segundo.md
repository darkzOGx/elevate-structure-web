## What Seismic Retrofit Services Are Available in El Segundo?

**Foundation Bolting**
Securing wood-frame houses to foundations prevents homes from sliding off during earthquakes—the most common and cost-effective residential retrofit.

**Cripple Wall Bracing**
Strengthening short stud walls between foundations and first floors prevents collapse in the crawl space area.

**Soft-Story Retrofit**
Multi-family buildings with parking on the ground floor require structural reinforcement to prevent collapse of the "soft" first story.

**Unreinforced Masonry (URM) Retrofit**
Older brick buildings require specialized retrofits to prevent collapse during earthquakes.

**Commercial Building Retrofits**
Tilt-up warehouses, concrete buildings, and other commercial structures may require seismic upgrades for safety or regulatory compliance.

## Common Seismic Vulnerabilities in El Segundo

**Older Single-Family Homes**
Pre-1980 homes often lack foundation bolts and cripple wall bracing, making them vulnerable to sliding and collapse.

**Multi-Family Buildings**
Apartment buildings and condos with ground-floor parking (soft-story buildings) are particularly vulnerable to earthquake damage.

**Commercial Buildings**
Tilt-up concrete buildings and older masonry structures may require retrofit to protect tenants and meet insurance requirements.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**How do I know if my El Segundo home needs a seismic retrofit?**
Homes built before 1980 often lack modern seismic features. Key vulnerabilities include: unbolted foundations, unreinforced cripple walls, and unreinforced chimneys. A professional assessment can identify your specific risks.

**Is seismic retrofitting required in El Segundo?**
El Segundo has not mandated residential retrofits, but some multi-family "soft-story" buildings may fall under LA County requirements. Many property owners pursue voluntary retrofits for safety and peace of mind.

**How much does a complete residential seismic retrofit cost?**
Including engineering and construction, typical residential retrofits cost $3,000-$10,000 depending on house size and existing conditions. The investment is small compared to potential earthquake damage.
