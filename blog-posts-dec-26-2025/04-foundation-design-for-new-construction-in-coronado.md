## What Are the Foundation Challenges in Coronado?

**Direct Answer:**
Coronado foundation challenges include sandy soils with limited bearing capacity, high groundwater affecting excavation and construction, flood zone requirements for coastal properties, marine corrosion exposure, and the Coronado Building Department's detailed review process. Professional Engineers address these through appropriate foundation types, corrosion protection, flood compliance, and thorough documentation.

**Sandy Soil Conditions**
Coronado's sandy island soils require careful foundation design to achieve adequate bearing capacity without excessive settlement. Soil improvement or pile foundations may be necessary for larger structures.

**Groundwater**
High groundwater levels affect foundation construction methods, require dewatering during construction, and influence long-term moisture protection requirements.

**Marine Environment**
Salt air and occasional salt spray accelerate concrete deterioration and steel corrosion. Protective measures extend foundation service life.
