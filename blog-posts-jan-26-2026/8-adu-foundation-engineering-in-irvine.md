## Foundation Types for Irvine ADUs

### Slab-on-Grade Foundations

The most common foundation type for Irvine ADUs:

**Standard Slab Design:**
- 4-inch concrete slab minimum
- Thickened edges (footings) at perimeter
- Interior footings under bearing walls
- Reinforcement per engineer's design

**Post-Tension Slab Design:**
- Steel tendons embedded in concrete
- Tendons tensioned after concrete cures
- Creates compression that resists cracking
- Ideal for expansive soil conditions

**When Slab-on-Grade Works:**
- Flat or gently sloping sites
- Adequate drainage away from foundation
- Non-flood zone locations
- Standard soil conditions

### Raised Foundations

Elevated floor systems for specific conditions:

**Raised Foundation Components:**
- Continuous perimeter footing
- Stem wall to desired height
- Floor joists or trusses
- Crawl space below floor

**When Raised Foundations Apply:**
- Flood zone requirements
- Sloping sites
- Preference for crawl space access
- Existing grade constraints

**Design Considerations:**
- Ventilation requirements per code
- Moisture barrier specifications
- Access for maintenance
- Seismic bracing of cripple walls

### Garage Conversion Foundations

Modifications to existing garage slabs:

**Typical Modifications:**
- Verify existing slab thickness
- Add thickened edge if needed
- Install moisture barrier
- Add footings for new walls

**When Full Replacement Needed:**
- Severely cracked or settled slab
- Inadequate thickness
- Major reconfiguration
- Incompatible with structural needs

**Engineering Assessment:**
- Evaluate existing slab condition
- Determine load-bearing capacity
- Design modifications as needed
- Coordinate with framing plans

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How much does ADU foundation engineering cost in Irvine?

ADU foundation engineering in Irvine typically costs $3,500-$8,000 as part of complete structural plans. Standalone foundation engineering for complex projects ranges $5,000-$12,000. Garage conversions with existing foundations cost less than new detached ADUs requiring full foundation design.

### What type of foundation do I need for an Irvine ADU?

Irvine ADUs typically use slab-on-grade foundations or raised foundations depending on site conditions. The choice depends on soil type, drainage, flood zone status, and ADU type. Garage conversions may use existing foundations with modifications. A geotechnical report determines the best approach.

### Do garage conversions need new foundations in Irvine?

Most Irvine garage conversions can use existing foundations with modifications. Engineering assessment verifies the existing slab can support residential loads. Common modifications include edge thickening, adding footings for new walls, and moisture barrier installation. Some deteriorated slabs require replacement.

### What soil conditions affect ADU foundations in Irvine?

Irvine has areas with expansive clay soils that require special foundation design. The geotechnical report identifies expansion potential and recommends foundation type, depth, and reinforcement. Post-tension slabs are common for expansive soil areas in Irvine to control cracking.

### How long does ADU foundation engineering take in Irvine?

ADU foundation engineering in Irvine typically takes 2-4 weeks as part of complete structural plans. The City of Irvine ADU permit review adds 4-8 weeks. Total timeline from engineering start to permit approval ranges 6-12 weeks for most projects.

### Do I need a geotechnical report for my Irvine ADU?

The City of Irvine requires geotechnical reports for new detached ADU foundations. Garage conversions may not require new reports if existing information is available. The geotechnical report determines soil conditions and foundation recommendations. We coordinate with geotechnical engineers.
