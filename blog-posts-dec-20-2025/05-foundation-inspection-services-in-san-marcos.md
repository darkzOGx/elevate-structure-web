## What Are Common Foundation Issues in San Marcos?

Common San Marcos foundation issues include slab cracking from expansive soils, settlement near hills, and moisture-related deterioration. Understanding these local conditions helps homeowners recognize problems early and take appropriate action.

**Expansive Soil Movement**

**The Primary Concern in San Marcos**
Much of San Marcos sits on clay-rich soils that expand and contract with moisture changes. This movement causes:
- Perimeter foundation cracking
- Slab heaving and settlement
- Seasonal door/window sticking
- Stucco and drywall cracking

**High-Risk Areas:**
- Discovery Hills neighborhoods
- Lake San Marcos area
- Older developments without post-tensioned slabs

**Hillside-Related Issues**

**San Marcos Hillside Properties**
Homes in the San Elijo Hills, Double Peak, and eastern San Marcos hills may experience:
- Downhill creep affecting foundations
- Retaining wall-related movement
- Drainage problems causing settlement

**Age-Related Deterioration**

**Older San Marcos Neighborhoods**
Homes built in the 1970s-1980s may show:
- Rebar corrosion in slabs
- Original construction defects
- Deteriorating moisture barriers
- Outdated foundation designs

## Frequently Asked Questions

**How is a PE foundation inspection different from a home inspection?**
Home inspectors provide general observations but cannot render engineering opinions or provide PE-stamped reports. Foundation-specific concerns require a licensed Professional Engineer who can evaluate structural adequacy and sign responsible opinions.

**How long does a foundation inspection take?**
On-site inspection typically takes 2-4 hours for a standard single-family home. Complex properties or significant concerns may require longer evaluation periods.

**Should I get an inspection before or after making an offer?**
Either approach works. Some buyers prefer inspecting contingent on acceptable findings, while others prefer inspecting before offer to avoid emotional attachment. In competitive San Marcos markets, pre-offer inspections provide advantage.

**What if the inspection finds problems?**
Our report provides repair recommendations and cost estimates. You can use this information to negotiate repairs, price adjustments, or to make informed decisions about proceeding with purchase.

**Can you inspect new construction foundations?**
Yes, we perform new construction inspections during critical phases including pre-pour, post-pour, and final inspection stages.

**Do lenders require foundation inspections?**
Some lenders require engineering assessment for properties with visible foundation concerns. FHA and VA loans sometimes trigger foundation inspection requirements based on appraiser observations.
