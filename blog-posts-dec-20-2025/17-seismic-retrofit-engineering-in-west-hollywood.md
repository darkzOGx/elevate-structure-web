## What Retrofit Systems Are Used in West Hollywood?

West Hollywood seismic retrofits typically use steel moment frames, plywood shear walls, or hybrid systems depending on building configuration, tenant impact considerations, and budget. Our licensed Professional Engineers select optimal systems for each building's specific conditions.

**Steel Moment Frames**

**The Preferred Solution:**
Rigid steel frames installed in ground-floor openings:
- Minimal tenant displacement
- Works with existing parking
- Clear aesthetic
- Efficient installation

**Typical West Hollywood Application:**
Moment frames at garage openings maintain parking function while providing seismic resistance.

**Plywood Shear Walls**

**Alternative Approach:**
Structural plywood sheathing applied to existing walls:
- Lower material cost
- May require tenant relocation
- Blocks some openings
- Good for certain configurations

**Hybrid Systems**

**Combining Approaches:**
Many West Hollywood buildings benefit from combining systems:
- Frames at large openings
- Shear walls at solid areas
- Optimized cost/performance

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Is my West Hollywood building subject to the retrofit ordinance?**
Buildings with 3+ stories and ground-floor parking or commercial space, built before 1978, are typically covered. Contact WeHo Building & Safety for official determination.

**What happens if I miss the retrofit deadline?**
Non-compliant buildings face daily fines, potential rent restrictions, and may have difficulty selling or refinancing. Compliance is mandatory, not optional.

**Can tenants remain during retrofit construction?**
Usually yes. We design retrofits to minimize tenant disruption, and most work occurs in common areas and parking structures.

**How long does retrofit construction take?**
West Hollywood soft-story retrofits typically take 3-6 months for construction, depending on building size and retrofit scope.

**Are there financing options for retrofits?**
Yes, PACE financing, seismic retrofit loans, and other programs may be available. Some costs may be passed through to tenants per local regulations.

**Will retrofitting increase my property taxes?**
Seismic retrofitting is generally excluded from reassessment under California law, meaning it shouldn't increase your property tax basis.
