## Why Foundation Inspection Matters in Chula Vista

Chula Vista's foundation challenges stem from multiple factors:

**Expansive Soils:** Much of Chula Vista features clay soils that expand and contract with moisture changes, creating foundation stress.

**Varied Terrain:** From flatlands near the bay to hillside neighborhoods in Eastlake and Otay Ranch, terrain affects foundation performance.

**Rapid Development:** Areas with newer construction on former agricultural or fill land may experience settlement.

**Property Values:** With Chula Vista home prices averaging $680,000+, foundation condition directly impacts your investment.

## Signs You Need Foundation Inspection

### Visible Foundation Problems

**Exterior Signs:**
- Cracks in foundation concrete
- Gaps between foundation and siding
- Crumbling or spalling concrete
- Exposed or rusting rebar
- Separation from patios/walkways

**Interior Signs:**
- Cracks in drywall or plaster
- Doors that stick or won't close
- Windows that don't operate smoothly
- Uneven or sloping floors
- Gaps between walls and ceiling

### Urgent Indicators

Seek immediate inspection if you notice:
- Horizontal cracks in foundation walls
- Cracks wider than 1/4 inch
- Visible foundation shifting
- Water intrusion through foundation
- Sudden changes in door/window operation

## Understanding Foundation Inspection Reports

### Condition Ratings

**Good Condition:**
- Normal cosmetic cracking only
- No settlement concerns
- Appropriate for property age
- No repairs needed

**Fair Condition:**
- Some structural cracks present
- Minor settlement noted
- Monitoring recommended
- Minor repairs may be beneficial

**Poor Condition:**
- Significant cracking patterns
- Notable settlement or movement
- Repairs recommended
- Engineering solutions needed

**Critical Condition:**
- Serious structural concerns
- Active movement present
- Immediate attention required
- Professional remediation necessary
