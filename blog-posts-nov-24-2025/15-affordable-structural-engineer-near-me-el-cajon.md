- Construction observation, or just permit drawings?

### 2. Provide Complete Information Upfront

**Save Engineer Time (and Your Money) By Providing:**
- Clear project description
- Property address and type
- Architectural plans if available
- Previous engineering if any
- Specific questions/concerns

### 3. Combine Services

**Bundle Discounts:**
- Foundation + framing together
- Multiple buildings at once
- Ongoing project relationships
- Referral programs

### 4. Avoid Rush Fees

**Standard Timeline Savings:**
- Rush (1 week): Premium pricing (+25-50%)
- Standard (2-3 weeks): Normal pricing
- Flexible (4+ weeks): Potential discount

### 5. Get It Right the First Time

**Costly Mistakes to Avoid:**
- Starting without engineering (may need redesign)
- Incomplete architect drawings (engineering changes later)
- Scope creep (adding services mid-project)

## Frequently Asked Questions

**What's the cheapest structural engineering option?**
A calculation letter for simple projects (single beam, header) is typically the most affordable option, starting around $350-$500.

**Can I do structural work without an engineer?**
Not legally for most projects in California. Building departments require PE-stamped plans for structural modifications.

**Why do prices vary so much between engineers?**
Differences in scope, experience, overhead, and approach. Always compare equivalent scopes when evaluating prices.

**Is it worth getting multiple quotes?**
