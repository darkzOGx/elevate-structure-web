## Types of Residential Foundations in San Marcos

### Slab-on-Grade Foundation
**Most Common in San Marcos**

A concrete slab poured directly on prepared ground, serving as both foundation and floor.

**Best For:**
- Flat to gently sloped lots
- Single-story and two-story homes
- Cost-conscious projects

**San Marcos Considerations:**
- Requires proper soil preparation
- May need post-tensioning in expansive soils
- Edge beams designed for local conditions

**Typical Cost:** $8,000-$20,000 for average homes

### Post-Tensioned Slab Foundation
**Recommended for Expansive Soils**

A reinforced slab with steel cables tensioned after concrete cures, creating a rigid foundation that resists soil movement.

**Best For:**
- Expansive clay soils common in San Marcos valleys
- Larger homes with open floor plans
- Areas with variable soil conditions

**Advantages:**
- Superior crack resistance
- Handles soil expansion/contraction
- Can span across weaker soil areas

**Typical Cost:** $12,000-$30,000

### Stem Wall Foundation
**Popular in San Marcos**

Continuous concrete walls (stem walls) support the perimeter, with either slab or crawl space interior.

**Best For:**
- Sloped lots requiring grade changes
- Homes requiring crawl space access
- Traditional construction methods

**San Marcos Applications:**
- Hillside properties in San Elijo Hills
- Properties with drainage considerations
- Homes requiring under-floor access

**Typical Cost:** $15,000-$35,000

### Raised Foundation (Crawl Space)
**Traditional Option**

Foundation walls create a crawl space beneath the floor framing.

**Best For:**
- Properties with high water tables
- Access needed for utilities
- Flood-prone areas

**Considerations:**
- Higher initial cost than slab
- Requires ventilation and moisture control
- Access for maintenance and repairs

**Typical Cost:** $18,000-$45,000

### Pier and Grade Beam Foundation
**For Challenging Sites**

Deep piers connected by structural beams, used when surface soils are unsuitable.

**Best For:**
- Sites with poor surface soils
- Steep hillside lots in San Marcos
- Fill soil conditions
- Expansive soil mitigation

**San Marcos Applications:**
- New developments on previously undeveloped land
- Hillside custom homes
- Properties near creek areas

**Typical Cost:** $25,000-$60,000+

## Common Foundation Design Mistakes to Avoid

**Skipping the Soils Report:**
San Marcos soil conditions vary significantly. Foundation designs based on assumptions rather than actual soil data often fail.

**Undersized Footings:**
Inadequate footing size leads to settlement and cracking. Proper engineering ensures footings match actual soil bearing capacity.

**Improper Drainage Design:**
Water is the enemy of foundations. Design must include proper drainage away from the foundation.

**Ignoring Expansion Potential:**
Expansive soils require specific design features. Generic foundation designs often crack in San Marcos clay soils.

**Wrong Foundation Type:**
Choosing the cheapest foundation option for challenging sites leads to expensive repairs later.
