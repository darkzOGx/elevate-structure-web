## Concrete Foundation Types for Temecula

### Monolithic Slab Foundation
**Most Common in Temecula**

A single concrete pour creating slab, footings, and thickened edge in one operation.

**Specifications:**
- Slab thickness: 4"-6"
- Edge beam: 12"-18" deep
- Reinforcement: #4 rebar grid or welded wire
- Concrete strength: 2,500-3,000 PSI minimum

**Best For:**
- Level or gently sloped lots
- Single-story construction
- Cost-effective projects
- Standard residential

**Cost:** $8-$14 per square foot

### Stem Wall Foundation
**For Temecula Grade Changes**

Separate pours for footings, stem walls, and floor slab create a raised foundation system.

**Specifications:**
- Footing width: 12"-24"
- Stem wall: 6"-8" thick
- Slab: 4" minimum
- All elements reinforced

**Best For:**
- Sloped lots common in Temecula wine country
- Properties requiring grade changes
- Crawl space access
- Traditional construction

**Cost:** $12-$20 per square foot

### Post-Tensioned Slab
**Recommended for Expansive Soils**

Steel cables tensioned after concrete cures create a rigid foundation resistant to soil movement.

**Specifications:**
- Slab thickness: 5"-6" minimum
- Post-tension cables per PT design
- Tendons stressed to 33,000 PSI
- Edge beams: 18"-24" deep

**Best For:**
- Temecula's expansive clay soils
- Open floor plans
- Larger homes
- Maximum crack resistance

**Cost:** $15-$25 per square foot

### Pier and Grade Beam
**For Challenging Temecula Sites**

Deep piers support grade beams, with floor above or slab-on-grade construction.

**Specifications:**
- Pier diameter: 12"-36"
- Pier depth: To bearing stratum
- Grade beams: 12"-18" wide
- Reinforcement per engineering

**Best For:**
- Poor surface soils
- Hillside properties
- Areas with fill
- Deep expansive soils

**Cost:** $20-$35 per square foot

## Concrete Specifications for Temecula Foundations

### Concrete Mix Design

**Standard Residential:**
- Compressive strength: 2,500 PSI at 28 days
- Slump: 4"-6"
- Maximum aggregate: 3/4"
- Air entrainment: 4-6% (optional)

**Enhanced Performance:**
- Compressive strength: 3,000-4,000 PSI
- Reduced water-cement ratio
- Fiber reinforcement option
- Improved crack resistance

**Post-Tensioned:**
- Compressive strength: 3,500-4,000 PSI
- Low shrinkage mix
- Early strength development
- Consistent quality critical

### Reinforcement Requirements

**Standard Slab:**
- #4 rebar at 18" on center both ways
- Or 6x6 W2.9/W2.9 welded wire
- Edge beam: #4 continuous top and bottom
- Dowels at all connections

**Stem Wall:**
- Vertical: #4 at 24" on center
- Horizontal: #4 at top, mid, and bottom
- Corner bars and ties
- Foundation dowels

**Post-Tensioned:**
- Per PT design engineer
- Typical: tendons at 48"-60" spacing
- Edge tendons additional
- Conventional rebar supplemental

## Concrete Placement in Temecula

### Hot Weather Considerations
Temecula's warm climate affects concrete:
- Early morning pours preferred
- Concrete temperature control
- Rapid curing mitigation
- Adequate curing measures

**Best Practices:**
- Pour before 10 AM in summer
- Use retarding admixtures
- Pre-wet subgrade
- Begin curing immediately

### Curing Requirements
Proper curing is critical:
- Moist cure for 7 days minimum
- Curing compound application
- Protect from direct sun
- Maintain moisture

### Common Temecula Issues

**Plastic Shrinkage Cracks:**
Caused by rapid surface drying
Prevention: Fog misting, windbreaks, evaporation retardant

**Settlement Cracks:**
Caused by soil movement
Prevention: Proper compaction, appropriate foundation type

**Control Joints:**
Required at regular intervals
Spacing: 10'-15' in both directions
Depth: 1/4 of slab thickness
