## How to Verify a Structural Engineer's License

### California License Verification

**Online Verification:**
1. Visit the California Board for Professional Engineers: [www.bpelsg.ca.gov](https://www.bpelsg.ca.gov)
2. Click "License Lookup"
3. Enter engineer's name or license number
4. Verify license is active and in good standing

**What to Check:**
- License status: "Active" required
- Expiration date: Must be current
- Disciplinary actions: Should be none
- License type: Civil or Structural Engineering

## Frequently Asked Questions

**How do I verify a structural engineer is licensed?**
Visit the California Board for Professional Engineers website (bpelsg.ca.gov) and search by name or license number.

**What's the difference between civil and structural PE?**
Civil engineers can perform some structural work, but complex structural projects often benefit from a Structural Engineer (SE) license holder.

**Can an unlicensed person draw my plans?**
Anyone can draw plans, but in California, plans requiring structural analysis must be prepared by or under supervision of a licensed PE.

**How long does licensing take?**
Becoming a licensed PE requires education (4+ years), experience (4+ years), and passing multiple exams. It typically takes 8-12 years minimum.

**Why are licensed engineers more expensive?**
Licensed engineers have extensive education, carry professional liability insurance, and bear legal responsibility for their work.
