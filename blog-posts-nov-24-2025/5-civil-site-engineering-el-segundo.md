## What is Civil Site Engineering?

Civil site engineering encompasses the planning, design, and documentation of all site infrastructure elements that support building development and connect to municipal systems. In California, licensed Professional Engineers prepare comprehensive plans showing site grading, drainage systems, utility connections, roadway improvements, parking layouts, and landscape infrastructure required for building permits and public works approvals.

These integrated systems must function reliably while meeting increasingly complex regulatory requirements from multiple agencies. Every component—from storm drains to sewer laterals—requires precise engineering and coordination. ## How Does the Civil Site Process Work?

The civil site engineering process typically takes 6-12 weeks from initial assessment to construction documents. The process includes feasibility analysis, conceptual design, detailed engineering, agency coordination, and permit acquisition. Licensed Professional Engineers manage complex coordination ensuring all systems integrate properly.

**Phase 1: Due Diligence (1-2 weeks)**

Site assessment identifies opportunities and constraints affecting development. We review title reports, survey existing conditions, research utility availability, and evaluate regulatory requirements. El Segundo properties often have aviation easements, oil field legacies, or environmental considerations requiring investigation.

**Phase 2: Conceptual Design (2-3 weeks)**

Preliminary engineering establishes site layout, grading concepts, and utility strategies. We prepare exhibits for planning entitlements and coordinate with the project team. Multiple alternatives explore development options. Cost estimates guide decision-making. Early agency consultation identifies key requirements.

**Phase 3: Detailed Design (4-6 weeks)**

Construction documents detail all civil improvements with precise dimensions and specifications. We perform hydraulic calculations, structural analysis, and coordinate with other disciplines. El Segundo's design standards require extensive detail. Quality control reviews ensure accuracy and completeness.

**Phase 4: Permitting (3-8 weeks)**

Multiple agencies review civil plans including City Public Works, Building & Safety, Fire Department, and utilities. We respond to comments, attend meetings, and secure approvals. Complex projects may require County Sanitation District or Caltrans permits. Construction permits follow plan approvals.
