## What Is Foundation Inspection?

Foundation inspection is a systematic evaluation of your building's foundation by a qualified professional. For Rancho Cucamonga properties, thorough inspection identifies:

- Current foundation condition
- Signs of settlement or movement
- Structural cracks and their significance
- Drainage and moisture issues
- Code compliance concerns
- Recommended repairs or monitoring

Professional foundation inspection goes beyond visual observation, using engineering expertise to assess structural integrity and identify problems before they become costly repairs.

## Types of Foundation Inspections

### Visual Foundation Assessment
**Basic Evaluation**

A thorough visual examination of accessible foundation elements.

**What's Checked:**
- Visible foundation walls and slabs

**Best For:**

## What Foundation Inspectors Check in Rancho Cucamonga

### Interior Inspection Points

**Floors:**
- Level measurements
- Soft spots or bounce
- Cracked tile or flooring
- Slope direction and magnitude

**Walls:**
- Drywall cracks (pattern and severity)
- Corner separation
- Wall-ceiling gaps
- Stucco condition

**Doors and Windows:**
- Operation difficulty
- Frame distortion
- Gap patterns
- Hardware stress

**Other Interior:**
- Cabinets pulling from walls
- Ceiling cracks
- Basement/crawl space access
- Utility penetrations

### Exterior Inspection Points

**Foundation Walls:**
- Visible cracks
- Spalling or deterioration
- Exposed rebar
- Water staining

**Slab Edges:**
- Edge exposure
- Settling or heaving
- Gap to siding
- Erosion evidence

**Surrounding Grade:**
- Slope direction
- Standing water evidence
- Root proximity
- Drainage adequacy

**Attached Structures:**
- Garage separation
- Patio connections
- Porch/step settlement
- Retaining walls

## Understanding Foundation Inspection Reports

### Condition Ratings

**Good Condition:**
- Minor cosmetic cracks only
- No significant settlement
- Normal age-related wear
- No repairs needed

**Fair Condition:**
- Some structural cracks
- Minor settlement present
- Monitoring recommended
- Minor repairs may be needed

**Poor Condition:**
- Significant structural issues
- Active movement present
- Repairs required
- Professional remediation needed

### Interpreting Crack Findings

| Crack Type | Typical Assessment |
|------------|-------------------|
| Hairline (<1/16") | Usually cosmetic |
| Small (1/16"-1/8") | Monitor for growth |
| Moderate (1/8"-1/4") | Evaluate cause |
| Large (>1/4") | Structural concern |
| Horizontal | Serious - immediate attention |

### What Reports Don't Include

- Prediction of future problems
- Guaranteed repair costs
- Contractor recommendations
- Insurance claim support (unless requested)

## Preparing for Foundation Inspection

### Before the Inspector Arrives

**Clear Access:**
- Move items from garage perimeter
- Clear basement/crawl space entry
- Trim vegetation from foundation
- Unlock gates and side yards

**Gather Information:**
- Property disclosures
- Previous inspection reports
- Repair records
- Construction dates

**Note Concerns:**
- Specific problem areas
- When issues first noticed
- Changes over time
- Seasonal patterns

### During Inspection

**Your Options:**
- Walk through with inspector
- Point out specific concerns
- Ask questions
- Take your own notes

**Inspector Needs:**
- Full property access
- Time to work thoroughly
- Honest disclosure of known issues
