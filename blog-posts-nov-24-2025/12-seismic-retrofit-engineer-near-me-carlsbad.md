## Types of Seismic Retrofits Available

Seismic retrofit engineers near you design various strengthening strategies from simple foundation bolting to complex structural systems depending on building configuration and vulnerability assessment.

**Foundation Bolting and Bracing**
Basic retrofits anchor wood sills to concrete foundations using expansion bolts or epoxy anchors. Typical spacing is 4-6 feet with closer spacing at corners. Hardware connects floor framing to foundations. Cost-effective for simple rectangular foundations. Most Carlsbad single-family homes benefit from this retrofit.

**Cripple Wall Strengthening**
Structural plywood sheathing reinforces short foundation walls. Metal connectors tie cripple walls to foundations and floor systems. Ventilation requirements maintained with proper vent placement. Hold-down anchors resist overturning forces. This retrofit dramatically improves older home performance.

**Soft Story Retrofits**
Steel moment frames or cantilevered columns strengthen open ground floors. Shear walls strategically placed to maintain functionality. Fiber-reinforced polymer (FRP) wrapping strengthens existing columns. Drag beams distribute loads to new lateral elements. Our [commercial engineering solutions](/services/commercial) address complex soft story conditions.

**Supplemental Damping Systems**
Advanced retrofits incorporate energy dissipation devices. Viscous dampers reduce earthquake forces and building movement. Buckling-restrained braces provide ductile response. Base isolation systems decouple structures from ground motion. These systems protect sensitive equipment and contents.
