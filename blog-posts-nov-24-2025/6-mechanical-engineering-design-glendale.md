## How Does the Mechanical Design Process Work?

The mechanical engineering process typically takes 6-10 weeks from initial assessment to construction documents. The process includes programming, conceptual design, detailed engineering, energy compliance, and construction support. Licensed Professional Engineers ensure code compliance while optimizing system performance and cost.

**Phase 1: Programming and Analysis (1-2 weeks)**

Initial assessment establishes project requirements, constraints, and goals. We evaluate existing conditions for renovation projects, interview stakeholders about comfort and operational needs, and identify applicable codes and standards. Glendale's reach codes may exceed state minimums for efficiency.

**Phase 2: System Selection (2-3 weeks)**

Conceptual design explores system alternatives balancing first cost, operating efficiency, and maintenance requirements. We compare centralized versus distributed systems, evaluate renewable energy integration, and assess phasing for occupied renovations. Life-cycle cost analysis guides decision-making.

**Phase 3: Detailed Design (3-4 weeks)**

Construction documents detail all mechanical systems with equipment schedules, ductwork layouts, piping diagrams, and control schematics. We coordinate with architectural, structural, and electrical disciplines. BIM modeling identifies conflicts before construction. Specifications ensure quality and performance.

**Phase 4: Compliance and Permitting (1-2 weeks)**

Title 24 compliance documentation demonstrates energy code conformance. Glendale Building & Safety reviews mechanical plans for code compliance. We respond to plan check comments and secure approvals. Complex projects may require additional Fire Department or AQMD review.
