You likely need a structural engineer if your project involves: removing or modifying walls, adding floor space, building a second story, foundation work, seismic upgrades, or any project requiring a building permit with structural components. **How long does structural engineering take?**
Most residential projects take 2-4 weeks from initial consultation to plan delivery. Simple projects may be faster; complex projects may take longer. We can discuss your timeline needs and accommodate rush requests when possible.

**Can I use the same engineer for my entire project?**
Structural engineers focus on the building's structure. You may also need an architect for overall design and permit drawings, and other engineers (mechanical, electrical) for larger projects. We coordinate with your design team to ensure integrated solutions.

**What's the difference between a structural engineer and an architect?**
Architects design the overall building appearance, layout, and function. Structural engineers design the structural system that holds the building up. Many projects require both. For smaller residential projects, the structural engineer's plans may be sufficient for permit.
