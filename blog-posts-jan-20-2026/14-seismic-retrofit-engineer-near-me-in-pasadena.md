## What Seismic Retrofit Services Are Available?

**Soft Story Retrofit**
Multi-family buildings with open ground floors (parking, storefronts) are particularly vulnerable to earthquakes. Soft story retrofitting adds structural elements—steel frames, plywood shear walls, or bracing—to strengthen these weak stories.

Pasadena's mandatory soft story retrofit program requires qualifying buildings to be retrofitted by specific deadlines. We help property owners understand requirements and design compliant solutions.

**Foundation Bolting**
Older homes often lack adequate connections between wood framing and concrete foundations. Foundation bolting adds anchor bolts connecting the sill plate to the foundation, preventing the house from sliding off its foundation during earthquakes.

**Cripple Wall Bracing**
Homes with crawl spaces have short stud walls (cripple walls) between the foundation and first floor. These walls can collapse during earthquakes if not properly braced. Cripple wall bracing adds plywood sheathing to strengthen these vulnerable elements.

**Unreinforced Masonry (URM) Retrofit**
Pasadena's older commercial areas contain unreinforced masonry buildings that pose significant earthquake risks. URM retrofits add structural elements—interior frames, wall anchors, parapet bracing—to prevent collapse.

**Chimney Bracing**
Unreinforced masonry chimneys are common failure points in earthquakes. Chimney bracing or reconstruction improves safety and protects both the home and neighboring properties.
