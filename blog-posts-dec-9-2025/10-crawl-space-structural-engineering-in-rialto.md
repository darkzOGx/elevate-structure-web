## Frequently Asked Questions

### Do I need a structural engineer for crawl space repairs in Rialto?

Most structural repairs in crawl spaces require PE-stamped plans. The City of Rialto requires engineering for floor joist replacement or reinforcement, beam modifications, post additions, and any work affecting structural capacity. Minor non-structural repairs may not require engineering.

### How do I know if my crawl space has structural problems?

Signs of crawl space structural issues include: sagging or bouncy floors, cracks in walls (especially near windows and doors), doors that won't close properly, and visible damage to framing. If you notice these signs, professional assessment is recommended.

### How long does crawl space engineering take?

Assessment projects take about 1 week. Minor repair design takes 2 weeks. Major floor system repairs or comprehensive projects take 2-4 weeks depending on scope and complexity.

### What is the cost of crawl space structural engineering?

Crawl space engineering in Rialto ranges from $1,500-$4,000 depending on project type. Assessment-only starts around $800-$1,200. Repair design ranges $1,500-$3,000. Complete renovation or seismic retrofit projects cost $3,000-$5,000+.

### Can I convert my crawl space to a basement?

Converting a crawl space to full basement is major construction typically requiring excavation, new foundation walls, waterproofing, and complete floor system modification. While possible, it's often more cost-effective to build a basement addition. We can assess feasibility for your specific situation.

### Should I be concerned about moisture in my crawl space?

Yes, moisture in crawl spaces can cause structural decay, mold growth, and pest attraction. Standing water, wet soil, or visible condensation should be addressed. Our assessment evaluates both structural impact and identifies moisture sources for correction.
