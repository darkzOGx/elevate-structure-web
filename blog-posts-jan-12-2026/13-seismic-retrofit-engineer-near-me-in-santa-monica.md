## What Are Santa Monica's Soft-Story Retrofit Requirements?

**Direct Answer:** Santa Monica's mandatory soft-story retrofit ordinance requires owners of qualifying wood-frame buildings with soft, weak, or open-front ground floor conditions to complete seismic retrofits by specified deadlines. Qualifying buildings were notified by the city, and non-compliance results in penalties, loss of certificate of occupancy, and potential tenant relocation requirements.

**Ordinance Overview**

Santa Monica's soft-story ordinance targets:
- Wood-frame buildings with soft-story conditions
- Multi-family residential buildings (typically 3+ units)
- Buildings constructed before certain dates
- Ground floors with parking, large openings, or weak walls

**Compliance Deadlines**

The ordinance established phased compliance deadlines:
- Priority 1 buildings: Earliest deadlines
- Priority 2 buildings: Extended timelines
- Extensions available under specific circumstances
- Penalties for non-compliance increase over time

**Required Retrofit Scope**

Compliant retrofits must achieve:
- Strength and stiffness improvements to ground floor
- Load path continuity from roof to foundation
- Foundation upgrades if inadequate
- Compliance with city-approved standards

**Enforcement**

Non-compliance consequences include:
- Civil penalties accumulating over time
- Loss of certificate of occupancy
- Prohibition on tenant occupancy
- Mandatory tenant relocation costs
- Property sale complications
