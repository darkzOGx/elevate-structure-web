The most common challenges include coordinating MEP systems with building architecture, meeting Title 24 energy requirements cost-effectively, addressing retrofit constraints in existing buildings, and balancing system performance with budget limitations. Chula Vista projects also must address regional climate conditions affecting system sizing. Professional Engineers address these through integrated design approaches and regional expertise.

**Challenge 1: Title 24 Compliance**

**Problem:** California's energy code (Title 24) has increasingly stringent requirements. Meeting compliance while maintaining project budget requires careful engineering.

**Chula Vista Example:** A commercial project needed to meet Title 24 requirements while staying within budget constraints. Standard equipment selections were insufficient for compliance.

**Solution:** Performance-based compliance approach:
- Energy modeling to optimize systems
- Strategic efficiency investments
- Trade-off analysis between components
- Cost-effective compliance path

**Challenge 2: Existing Building Retrofits**

**Problem:** Retrofitting MEP systems in existing buildings involves constraints from building structure, space limitations, and coordination with occupied spaces.

**Solution:** Thorough field investigation and creative design:
- Document existing conditions
- Identify routing opportunities
- Design within available space
- Phasing for occupied buildings

**Challenge 3: System Coordination**

**Problem:** MEP systems must coordinate with architecture and structure. Ceiling space, shaft locations, and structural elements all constrain system layouts.

**Solution:** Integrated design process:
- Early coordination with design team
- Clash detection where appropriate
- Flexible routing strategies
- Clear communication of spatial needs

**Challenge 4: Cooling Load Management**

**Problem:** Chula Vista's warm climate creates significant cooling demands. Undersized systems result in comfort problems while oversized systems waste energy and money.

**Solution:** Accurate load calculations and appropriate design margins:
- Climate-specific design data
- Accurate envelope assumptions
- Appropriate diversity factors
- Right-sized equipment selection
